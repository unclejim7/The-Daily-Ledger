📈 Markets & Finance
Record Close

S&P Breaks 7,600 for First Time — Dow Hits All-Time High as Rotation Lifts Defensives

The S&P 500 closed at 7,584.31 Thursday — its first close above 7,600 — while the Dow Jones Industrial Average surged 874.86 points to a record close of 51,561.93. The Nasdaq eked out a 0.03% gain at 27,093.90 as investors rotated out of semiconductor names and into non-tech blue chips.

UnitedHealth led the Dow higher, rising more than 5%. JPMorgan Chase added 3% and Walmart gained nearly 1%. On the flip side, chip names were under pressure ahead of Friday's Broadcom earnings release, with Marvell Technology having surged 25% earlier in the week after Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang called it a potential trillion-dollar company. Quantinuum completed a $1.68B IPO at Nasdaq, priced at $60, opening at $68.

🏛️ Federal Reserve
Warsh Era

New Chair Confirmed 54–45 — First FOMC Meeting June 16–17

Kevin Warsh was confirmed May 13 and took the helm May 15. His first FOMC as Chair is scheduled for June 16–17. Markets price virtually no chance of a 2026 cut. Warsh has signaled preference for lower rates but faces a committee oriented toward keeping options open for hikes given sticky inflation.

Rate Outlook

🌐 Geopolitics
US–Iran Talks

Trump "Honored" to Meet New Iranian Leader If Deal Materializes

President Trump said Thursday he'd be "honored" to meet Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei if conditions were right. Crude Declining Off $98 Peak — But Inflation Premium Persists

WTI crude oil has been declining after its earlier spike toward $98/barrel. The geopolitical risk premium remains embedded in energy prices and is a key driver of elevated US CPI. Progress in Iran talks could meaningfully lower oil prices and ease the Fed's inflation calculus.

🏠 Real Estate & Mortgage Rates
Current Rate

30-Year Fixed: 6.588% — Slight Dip From Thursday

The average 30-year fixed purchase mortgage eased slightly to 6.588% on June 5 per Zillow data, from 6.604% on June 4. Freddie Mac's June 1 PMMS weekly average was 6.53%. Refinance rates are running higher, around 6.68–6.70%. Housing affordability has improved year-over-year but remains well below pandemic-era lows.

Outlook

Rates Expected 6.0–6.4% Through Mid-2027

Forecasters at Fannie Mae, the MBA, and major banks converge on a 6.0–6.4% range for 30-year mortgages through at least mid-2027. The lock-in effect keeps resale inventory depressed. Monthly median mortgage payment is $2,152, per the MBA, keeping affordability strained for first-time buyers.

📡 Armstrong Economics

Armstrong Market Talk — June 3–5, 2026

Armstrong's bond yield data this week showed US 10-year yields at 4.496% and the 30-year crossing 5.00% — consistent with his ECM framework's June 2026 cycle inflection warning. Peripheral European yields continued to widen (Italy +12 bps, Turkey +270 bps) while commodity markets showed energy inflation persistence (crude +2.65%, heating oil +4.11% on June 3).

Armstrong's war cycle and capital flow models have been flagging this period as a critical juncture where sovereign debt stress builds and capital rotates toward the US dollar despite widening US deficits. The strong Friday jobs print (172K) adds complexity to the rate and cycle picture, consistent with the type of "late-cycle" labor market strength Armstrong models often show preceding a meaningful inflection.

💊 Health & Wellness
Supplements

Collagen Study: Works — But Requires Consistent Long-Term Use

A review of nearly 8,000 participants found collagen supplements improve skin health and ease osteoarthritis symptoms when taken consistently over longer periods. Modest benefits also found for muscle mass and joint pain. The key caveat: sporadic use showed minimal benefit — consistency is the variable.

GLP-1 Research

Semaglutide Linked to Lower Addiction Risk in 600K-Person Study

A study of over 600,000 US veterans found that GLP-1 receptor agonists like semaglutide may reduce the likelihood of developing substance use disorders involving alcohol, nicotine, and cannabis. Adds to mounting evidence that GLP-1s may reshape treatment of chronic disease well beyond metabolic conditions.

Cancer Breakthrough

KRAS Mutation Finally Targeted — Pancreatic Cancer Survival May Double

Scientists cracked the long-considered "undruggable" KRAS mutation that drives most pancreatic cancers. The new drug daraxonrasib showed potential to nearly double survival rates in early trials. Given pancreatic cancer's historically dismal prognosis, this represents one of the most significant oncology advances in years.
