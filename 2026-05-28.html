World Affairs · Middle East Conflict

Fresh Strikes Rattle Fragile U.S.-Iran Ceasefire as Peace Talks Drag On

The IRGC and U.S. military exchanged blows overnight, testing a shaky agreement that has yet to produce a lasting settlement — with oil markets reacting sharply at the open.

Hours before dawn on the East Coast, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps launched an attack targeting an American air base, claiming it was the source of prior U.S. strikes. The U.S. military confirmed new strikes against Iranian drones and a launch site near the Strait of Hormuz. The overnight exchange sent crude oil futures surging more than 2% back above $90 a barrel.

The conflict, which began February 28 when the U.S. and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury — eliminating Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and destroying nuclear infrastructure — has ground into an exhausting stalemate of on-again, off-again hostilities despite a nominal ceasefire agreed in April. Negotiations toward a formal settlement are ongoing, with President Trump warning he will not be rushed into a deal. Iranian officials have raised demands with each passing day, and hostilities with Hezbollah continue in Lebanon.

The economic toll is significant: gas prices hit a national average of $4.56/gallon over Memorial Day weekend — the highest in four years — after the Strait of Hormuz closure cut off roughly 20% of global oil supplies. Iranian state media said Tuesday that Iran is committed to reopening the strait within one month, prompting Wednesday's rally in equities.

"We won't be rushed. Iran's strategy of outlasting us at the negotiating table won't work."
— President Donald Trump, on U.S.-Iran peace negotiations, May 2026
Macroeconomics

Fed Holds Rates Steady — But One Dissenter Pushed for a Cut

Stephen Miran voted for a 25 bps cut; three other members opposed the committee's easing bias. Jerome Powell's term expires May 15 — a new Fed chair is expected soon.

§ VI
Research Roundup · Week of May 26

Five Findings Worth Your Attention This Week

01

Scientists Crack Why Ozempic's Weight Loss Eventually Plateaus

New research (May 25) has identified the biological mechanism behind the well-known GLP-1 plateau effect in semaglutide users. The discovery may lead to next-generation formulations that sustain weight loss beyond the current ceiling — significant for the millions of Ozempic and Wegovy users seeking long-term outcomes. Separately, a major analysis found semaglutide works remarkably well in adults over 65, improving both weight and heart health.

02

Beans & Soy Could Cut High Blood Pressure Risk by Nearly 30%

A major dietary analysis published May 26 found that increased consumption of beans, lentils, chickpeas, tofu, and soy foods correlated with a nearly 30% reduction in hypertension risk. Researchers say the effect appears dose-dependent — the more legumes consumed, the greater the benefit. A notably cheap and practical dietary intervention.

03

USC Scientists Identify Hidden Alzheimer's Trigger — and a Possible Drug Target

USC researchers (May 26) identified novel drug compounds that may reduce brain inflammation linked to Alzheimer's disease, particularly in genetically susceptible individuals. Separately, researchers at Texas A&M developed a nasal spray that appears to reverse brain aging by calming inflammation and restoring energy metabolism in mouse models.

04

Vitamin B12: More Isn't Always Better for Aging Brains, UCSF Finds

UC San Francisco researchers found that older adults with "normal but lower" B12 levels — technically within guidelines — still showed signs of accelerated brain aging. The findings (May 22) challenge current RDA thresholds and suggest older adults may need higher B12 targets to protect cognitive function over time.

05

Supercharged Vitamin K Compound May Help the Brain Regenerate Neurons

Japanese scientists created novel vitamin K-based compounds (May 27) that stimulated neuronal regeneration in preclinical models — a potential breakthrough for Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and other neurodegenerative diseases. The compounds work by combining modified vitamin K with regenerative signaling molecules, opening a new therapeutic avenue.
