Economy · Inflation Watch

PCE Hits 3.8% — Highest Since May 2023; Bogus Iran Peace Report Jolts Markets

The Fed's preferred inflation gauge, the Personal Consumption Expenditures index, came in at 3.8% year-over-year — the highest reading in nearly three years. The print sent futures briefly lower before markets digested the data and recovered. Meanwhile, a false report circulating on social media claimed a US-Iran peace deal had been signed. Markets spiked on the news before outlets debunked it within minutes. The episode underscores how thin the tape is and how desperately investors want a geopolitical resolution.

Federal Reserve

PCE at 3.8%: Warsh's First Dilemma

The June 16–17 FOMC meeting is now even more fraught. PCE at 3.8% gives the new chair zero room to sound dovish. Three of the four April dissenters voted against the easing bias — not against the hold — signaling the hawkish wing of the committee wants to remove any hint of future cuts. Warsh must navigate this on day one.

Iran · Markets

A fabricated report of a US-Iran ceasefire sent equity futures surging before it was quickly debunked. The rapid spike — and equally rapid reversal — illustrates how much of the current war premium is priced into energy and risk assets. An actual deal announcement could trigger a significant market rally across multiple asset classes simultaneously.

🔭 Armstrong Economics

Martin Armstrong · Market Outlook

Capital Flight to Tangibles Accelerating — Gold and Oil Signal Distrust of Paper

Armstrong's model tracks capital flows as a proxy for confidence in institutions. The current pattern — rising gold, rising oil, equity markets at record highs, and Bitcoin's institutional bid — is consistent with what he calls the "capital concentration" phase: money flowing into the assets it trusts most before a broader confidence reset. The fake Iran peace report incident fits the pattern perfectly: markets are so eager for good news that even a rumor moves them. Armstrong has long argued that the real danger isn't a market crash but a slow erosion of purchasing power as governments inflate away debt. At 3.8% PCE with rates at 3.5%–3.75%, real rates are barely positive — not enough to slow inflation meaningfully.

💊 Health & Wellness

Alzheimer's New Drug Target Identified — Protein Aggregation Pathway

Researchers have identified a new therapeutic target in the Alzheimer's disease pathway — a protein aggregation mechanism that precedes the tau tangles and amyloid plaques typically associated with the disease. The finding suggests earlier intervention may be possible before irreversible neurological damage occurs. Clinical trials targeting this pathway are in early stages at multiple institutions.

B12 Deficiency Linked to Accelerated Brain Aging — New Large-Scale Study

A large-scale imaging study found measurable associations between vitamin B12 deficiency and accelerated brain aging markers, including reduced white matter integrity and faster cognitive decline trajectories. The finding is particularly relevant for adults over 50 and strict vegans, who are at higher risk of deficiency. Supplementation studies are ongoing.

🎲 Closing Notes

Random Fact of the Day

The Chicago "L" is the second busiest rapid transit system in the US.

After New York City's subway, Chicago's elevated train system carries more riders than any other US rapid transit system — approximately 230 million trips per year. The Loop section, where multiple lines converge downtown, gave the city center its nickname.

Word of the Day

Stagflation

The simultaneous occurrence of stagnant economic growth and high inflation — the Fed's nightmare scenario. With PCE at 3.8% and payroll growth slowing, stagflation risk is the word analysts are reluctant to say out loud in June 2026.

Quote of the Day

"Inflation is always and everywhere a monetary phenomenon."

— Milton Friedman. At 3.8% PCE with rates at 3.5%, the Fed is arguably still behind the curve on the economist's most famous dictum.
