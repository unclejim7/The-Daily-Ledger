Breaking
Iran Fires Ballistic Missile at Kuwait — CENTCOM Calls It "Egregious Ceasefire Violation"
Iran fired a ballistic missile at Kuwait early Thursday morning, killing three people and wounding dozens near the port of Shuwaikh. CENTCOM called it an "egregious violation" of the ceasefire framework that had been announced just 48 hours earlier. U.S. carrier strike groups in the Gulf were put on heightened alert. Oil spiked above $95/bbl before retreating as the State Department said talks in Oman would continue. Health, Wellness & Fitness

Brain Health

B12 Deficiency Linked to Accelerated Brain Aging

A large UK Biobank study found that adults with low B12 levels in their 40s and 50s showed measurably faster brain aging on MRI scans — more gray matter loss, thinner cortex, worse cognitive test scores. The association held even after controlling for diet and lifestyle. B12 is found in meat, eggs, and dairy; many plant-based eaters are chronically deficient.

Longevity

Step Count Target: 7,500 Steps Is the New 10,000

A comprehensive meta-analysis of 17 studies and 226,000 participants found that 7,500 daily steps captures 90% of the mortality-reduction benefit previously attributed to 10,000. The 10,000-step target, it turns out, was a marketing number from a 1960s Japanese pedometer company — not science. 7,500 is the evidence-based target.

Neuroscience

Vitamin K Found to Stimulate Nerve Regeneration

Researchers at Northwestern University found that Vitamin K2 activates a pathway that promotes peripheral nerve regeneration — potentially useful for diabetic neuropathy, traumatic nerve injury, and post-surgical recovery. The finding is early-stage but opens a new therapeutic avenue distinct from current pain-management approaches.

Word of the Day

Recidivism (n.)

The tendency to relapse into prior behavior — in markets, it describes countries, companies, or actors that repeatedly revert to destabilizing actions despite consequences. Iran's missile strike on Kuwait just 48 hours after a ceasefire announcement is textbook geopolitical recidivism. The market has priced this pattern in — hence why oil didn't spike to $110 on the news.
