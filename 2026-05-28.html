Policy Outlook

In a 2024 op-ed, Warsh wrote that the Fed should not ease until core PCE is "durably and demonstrably" at 2% — a higher bar than the current Chair's posture. With core PCE at 3.2%, markets now see no cuts until at least Q1 2027 under the Warsh scenario.

📊 Armstrong Economics
Market Talk — May 28, 2026

Armstrong: Bond Market Is the Tell — War Cycle and Monetary Cycle Converging

Martin Armstrong noted Thursday that the bond market's reaction to the Warsh nomination is the most important signal of the week — more important than the equity records. Armstrong's models show that when monetary tightening cycles coincide with War Cycle peaks, the result is historically disruptive to sovereign debt markets, not just equities.

He reiterated his view that gold's strength is driven by institutional loss of confidence in sovereign bonds rather than inflation hedging per se. "When governments can't control their deficits and central banks tighten simultaneously, gold is the only asset with no counterparty risk," Armstrong wrote.

💪 Health & Wellness
Sleep Research

Getting 7–9 Hours Reduces Inflammatory Markers by Up to 40%

A study in Nature Communications found that adults consistently sleeping 7–9 hours showed inflammatory biomarker levels 35–40% lower than those sleeping under 6 hours. Chronic low-grade inflammation is linked to cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and accelerated cognitive decline — making sleep one of the highest-leverage health interventions available.

Fitness

Walking 8,000+ Steps Daily Cuts All-Cause Mortality Risk 51%

Researchers analyzing data from 15 studies found that walking 8,000 or more steps daily reduces all-cause mortality risk by 51% compared to walking fewer than 4,000 steps. The benefit plateaued around 10,000 steps — meaning the marginal return diminishes sharply beyond that threshold. Step count, not intensity, was the primary driver.

🎲 Closing Notes
Random Fact of the Day

The Dow Jones Has Only Closed Above 50,000 for the Second Time Ever

The Dow first crossed 50,000 intraday in November 2024 but closed definitively above that level in early 2025. Today's 50,516 close marks only the second time the index has closed above 50,000 two consecutive sessions. It took the Dow 128 years to go from its 1896 inception at 40 points to 10,000 — and just 25 more years to add another 40,000.

Word of the Day

Yield Curve

A graph plotting interest rates of bonds with equal credit quality but different maturity dates. When short-term rates exceed long-term rates, the curve "inverts" — historically a recession predictor. Today's curve remains flat-to-slightly-inverted as Warsh premium pushes long-end yields up while short rates are anchored by current Fed policy.

— Milton Friedman. Kevin Warsh is a Friedman disciple — he believes the prior Fed's QE programs were a primary driver of the 2021–2023 inflation surge. His nomination signals a return to monetarist discipline. Bond markets are taking him at his word.
