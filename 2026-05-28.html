🗞 Front Page

Markets / Fed / Iran

Records Fall as Stocks Shrug Off Inflation Shock — Iran Deal Still Unsigned

All three major indexes hit all-time records Thursday even as April PCE came in at 3.8% year-over-year — its hottest reading in three years. The stock market has no memory of pain: AI enthusiasm, strong corporate earnings, and ceasefire optimism in the Strait of Hormuz are overriding what would once have been a disqualifying inflation print. The Iran MOU framework is largely negotiated, but Trump still has not signed off. Both sides continue to exchange strikes while diplomats haggle. Oil slid on deal hopes, providing modest inflation relief even as the PCE data landed with a thud.

Real Estate

Case-Shiller +0.7% YOY — Sluggish but Stable

The Case-Shiller national home price index rose just 0.7% year-over-year — one of the softest readings in years, reflecting the affordability squeeze from elevated mortgage rates. Median home prices nationally hit a record $417,700. Inventory is rising modestly. The spring market is being described by agents as "rebalancing" — a polite word for subdued. Pending sales are up 1.4%, suggesting demand is alive but constrained.

Fed / Inflation

PCE 3.8% Seals the Deal: No Cuts This Year

April PCE at 3.8% YOY — driven by energy pass-through from the Iran war — removes any remaining debate about 2026 rate cuts. Core PCE sits at 3.3%. Real disposable income fell 1.4% YOY. Real spending rose just 0.1%. The savings rate is at its lowest since 2022. Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting (June 16–17) will almost certainly be a hold, with the new Chair watching the conflict closely before making any moves.

💊 Health & Wellness

Brain Health

Vitamin K Compound Shows Brain-Regenerating Properties

New research highlights a Vitamin K-derived compound showing potential to support myelin repair — the protective sheath around nerve fibers. Early-stage findings suggest relevance to multiple sclerosis and age-related cognitive decline. Researchers caution this is still far from clinical application but the mechanism is compelling.

Mental Health

Immune-Targeted Depression Treatment Shows Promise

A clinical trial targeting inflammatory pathways rather than serotonin showed meaningful depression symptom reduction in patients who hadn't responded to traditional antidepressants. The approach — treating depression as partly an immune/inflammatory disorder — is gaining traction as a new therapeutic paradigm alongside conventional SSRI treatments.

📅 Word of the Day
Stagflation
(n.) An economic condition combining stagnant growth and high inflation — the Fed's worst nightmare. PCE at 3.8% with Q1 GDP revised lower is as close as we've gotten in years.
💡 Random Fact
The Chicago River Once Flowed Backward
In 1900, Chicago engineers reversed the flow of the Chicago River — one of the most audacious engineering feats in U.S. history — to prevent sewage from contaminating Lake Michigan, the city's water supply.
