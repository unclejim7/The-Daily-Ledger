Markets Shrug Off Iran Headlines — Dow Hits Second Consecutive Record Close

The Dow Jones Industrial Average notched its second consecutive record close Thursday, gaining 246 points to 50,516 as investors focused on strong earnings revisions and continued AI infrastructure spending. The S&P 500 climbed 0.16% to 7,602 while the Nasdaq gained 0.35% as chipmakers rebounded from Wednesday's export-control selloff. Bond yields edged higher to 4.55% as Warsh's Senate confirmation looks increasingly certain — markets are front-running a more hawkish Fed regime.

The Iran ceasefire talks in Oman entered their second day with no breakthrough reported, but the lack of escalation is itself being read as constructive. Oil retreated to $91.40 from Tuesday's $95 intraday peak.

Federal Reserve

Warsh Hearings Set for June — Easing Bias at Risk

Senate Banking Committee confirmation hearings for Kevin Warsh have been scheduled for the first week of June. Warsh is expected to signal at hearings that he intends to review the Fed's "easing bias" and reopen the question of the neutral rate. The two rate cuts currently priced into futures for 2026 are looking increasingly fragile.

Tax & Accounting

OBBBA Tax Package Heads to Conference — Key Provisions in Play

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act tax provisions are heading to a House-Senate conference after passing each chamber in different forms. Key battlegrounds include the SALT deduction cap, expanded child tax credit, and the estate tax threshold. CPAs are advising clients to hold off on major year-end planning decisions until conference reports emerge — likely in July.

Housing Market

New Home Sales Surge as Existing Inventory Stays Frozen

April new home sales came in at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 743,000 — up 6.3% from March and the strongest reading since 2022. Health & Wellness
Sleep Science

Consistent Sleep Schedule More Important Than Duration

A new meta-analysis of 74 studies covering 4.2 million participants finds that sleep schedule consistency — going to bed and waking at the same time daily — is a stronger predictor of cardiovascular health and cognitive function than total sleep duration. Irregular sleep timing is associated with a 26% higher risk of metabolic syndrome.

Nutrition

Time-Restricted Eating Window Narrows to 8 Hours for Optimal Benefit

Stanford researchers confirm that an 8-hour eating window (e.g., noon to 8 PM) produces superior metabolic outcomes compared to 10- or 12-hour windows in adults over 40. The study found significant improvements in insulin sensitivity, blood pressure, and inflammatory markers after just 12 weeks.

Armstrong Economics
Martin Armstrong

Dollar Strength Persisting Into ECM Window — Capital Flows Tell the Story

Armstrong's latest Market Talk notes that despite widespread predictions of dollar weakness, capital continues to flow into U.S. assets as the only functional deep market globally. European political instability, China's property sector overhang, and Middle East conflict are all driving capital toward U.S. equities and Treasuries — the very dynamic Armstrong's model has flagged since 2020. He cautions that this concentration of capital makes the eventual reversal more dramatic when it comes.

Random Fact

Chicago's "Magnificent Mile" Was a Rebranding Masterstroke

The stretch of Michigan Avenue north of the Chicago River was renamed the "Magnificent Mile" in 1947 by real estate developer Arthur Rubloff as a marketing strategy to attract upscale retailers. The rebranding worked — it's now one of the most expensive retail corridors in the world.

Word of the Day

Contango

A futures market condition where prices for future delivery are higher than current spot prices — implying markets expect current conditions to ease over time. Today's oil market is in moderate contango, reflecting traders' bet that the Iran situation eventually resolves.
