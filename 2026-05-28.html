Markets & Finance

Earnings
Dell Surges on AI Blowout — Revenue +88%, Data Center Orders Triple
Dell Technologies reported fiscal Q1 results Wednesday that stunned even the most bullish analysts. Revenue surged 88% year-over-year to $43.84 billion, powered by a 181% explosion in data center business as hyperscalers race to build AI infrastructure. Inflation

PCE Hits 3.8% — Highest in 3 Years

April's Personal Consumption Expenditures price index came in at 3.8% year-over-year, a three-year high and well above the Fed's 2% target. Core PCE held at 3.3%. Real disposable income fell 1.4% year-over-year — a squeeze that is beginning to show up in lower-income consumer spending data.

Treasury Market

10-Year Yield Spikes to 4.595% — 30-Year Above 5%

The bond market is having a difficult week. The 10-year Treasury yield hit 4.595% and the 30-year crossed above 5.12% as hot PCE data and a Moody's credit downgrade of U.S. debt rattled fixed income. A credit downgrade plus high inflation is the worst-case scenario for bond holders and a complicating factor for the incoming Fed Chair.

Macro & Federal Reserve

Federal Reserve

Warsh Confirmation Hearings Begin — Hawks Cheer

Kevin Warsh's Senate confirmation hearings for Fed Chair opened this week. His testimony emphasized credible, rules-based monetary policy and signaled he'd be slower to cut than the market expects. Real rates are at their highest since 2007; Warsh appears comfortable leaving them there until inflation is clearly beaten.

Credit

Moody's Downgrades U.S. Debt — Bond Market Rattled

Moody's stripped the U.S. of its last remaining AAA rating this week, citing persistent deficits and rising debt service costs. The downgrade follows similar actions by S&P (2011) and Fitch (2023). Treasury yields spiked in response. The long-term fiscal math continues to deteriorate.

Iran Conflict
U.S. and Iran Exchange Fire Even as MOU Talks Proceed
In an unusual diplomatic moment, U.S. and Iranian forces exchanged fire Thursday even as negotiators sat across the table in Muscat finalizing a memorandum of understanding for a 60-day ceasefire. The simultaneous fighting and talking reflects the messy reality of modern conflict resolution — both sides appear to want a deal but neither wants to appear to want it more than the other. The MOU text is largely agreed upon; what remains is the Trump administration's formal sign-off, which sources say will come with conditions on missile verification. Markets have largely priced in a resolution; a collapse of talks now would be sharply negative for equities and risk assets.

Tax & Accounting

Moody's Downgrade and Your Portfolio: What It Actually Means

A credit downgrade of U.S. sovereign debt sounds alarming but has limited immediate practical impact for most individual investors. Here's why: U.S. Treasuries remain the world's dominant safe-haven asset regardless of rating agency actions. The S&P downgrade in 2011 caused a brief spike in yields then a rally. What the Moody's downgrade does matter for is long-term: it signals that the trajectory of U.S. debt-to-GDP is genuinely concerning, that interest expense is crowding out other government spending, and that fiscal discipline — currently absent — will eventually be forced by markets rather than chosen by policymakers. For tax planning purposes, this has no immediate effect on rates or rules. But it underscores the long-term argument for hard asset exposure in a diversified portfolio.

Armstrong Economics

Armstrong: Sovereign Debt Crisis Building — Capital Fleeing Into Equities

Martin Armstrong has long predicted that the next great crisis will not be a stock market crash but a sovereign debt crisis — and the Moody's downgrade is precisely the kind of signal his models treat as meaningful. Armstrong's view is that capital flowing out of European and now U.S. bond markets is finding a home in equities, hard assets, and crypto — which explains why stocks and Bitcoin can both be near all-time highs during a period of geopolitical conflict and sticky inflation. His Socrates model continues to flag a major cyclical test in the September–October 2026 window. He remains bullish on U.S. equities as a destination for global capital even as he warns that the bond market is the structural vulnerability.

Health & Wellness

Sleep Research

Sleep Deprivation Ages the Brain Faster Than Smoking

A large-scale neuroimaging study published this week found that chronic sleep deprivation (less than 6 hours per night) accelerates brain aging at a rate comparable to heavy smoking — approximately 1.5 additional years per decade. The mechanism involves impaired glymphatic clearance of toxic proteins during slow-wave sleep.

Nutrition

Time-Restricted Eating Benefits Hold Up in Long-Term Study

A 24-month randomized controlled trial confirmed that time-restricted eating (eating within an 8-10 hour window daily) provides meaningful metabolic benefits independent of caloric intake — including improved insulin sensitivity, lower fasting triglycerides, and modest but consistent reductions in visceral fat.

Random Fact

The Sears Tower Was the World's Tallest for 25 Years

The Willis Tower (formerly Sears Tower), completed in 1973, held the title of world's tallest building for 25 years until the Petronas Towers in Kuala Lumpur surpassed it in 1998. At 1,450 feet, it remains the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere.

Word of the Day

Yield Curve

A graph plotting interest rates of bonds with equal credit quality but different maturity dates. When short-term rates exceed long-term rates ("inverted yield curve"), it historically signals a coming recession. The curve has been flirting with inversion again as the 30-year yield jumps above 5%.
