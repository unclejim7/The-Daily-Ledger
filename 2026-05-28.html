Today's Front Page
World Affairs · Breaking
U.S. & Iran Reach 60-Day Truce Extension — Trump Has Not Yet Signed Off
American and Iranian negotiators have agreed on a memorandum of understanding to extend the fragile ceasefire for 60 days and launch formal negotiations on Tehran's nuclear program, according to Axios and Bloomberg, citing officials on both sides. But President Trump has not yet given his approval — and the two countries exchanged fire in the Strait of Hormuz even as the deal was being finalized on Thursday. The MOU would be the most significant diplomatic movement since the war began on February 28. Key sticking points remain: the fate of Iran's enriched uranium stockpile, the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, and billions in frozen Iranian assets. Iranian state TV claims the draft calls for U.S. military withdrawal; Washington calls that a fabrication. Trump said Wednesday he is "not satisfied" with terms and is "not in a rush."

"This is an agreement to get everybody to the table. We will work out the details in the negotiations."

— U.S. Official, via Axios, May 28, 2026

Markets Desk
BofA Warning
Bank of America Flags "Summer Pullback" Risk as Rally Shows Fatigue
U.S. stocks may be running out of room after a relentless post-Iran-war rally, according to Bank of America strategists who issued a note this week urging clients to start preparing for a seasonal retreat. The bank delayed its Fed rate-cut forecast all the way to July–September 2027, citing persistent inflation kept elevated by high energy prices from the ongoing conflict. BofA notes that energy prices — a direct byproduct of the Strait of Hormuz closure — have been the primary inflation driver keeping the Fed on hold at its current 3.50–3.75% target range. The Fed's April 29 meeting produced an unusually divided 8-4 vote to hold rates, the closest split since 1992, with dissents ranging from hawks who opposed any easing bias to a dove who wanted to cut immediately.
Macroeconomics & The Fed
Federal Reserve
Fed Holds at 3.50–3.75% — Inflation "Elevated in Part Due to Global Energy Prices"
The Federal Open Market Committee voted 8-4 on April 29 to maintain the federal funds rate at 3½–3¾ percent, its most contentious meeting since 1992. The official statement noted that inflation "is elevated, in part reflecting the recent increase in global energy prices" from the Middle East conflict. The committee acknowledged "a high level of uncertainty" from the Iran war. With Jerome Powell's term having expired in May, focus is now shifting to who leads the Fed next — and whether a new chair might push toward rate cuts in the 3.00–3.25% range, which iShares and others project as the eventual landing zone. BofA now doesn't see any cuts until mid-2027.
Housing & Rates
Mortgage Rates Hit 6.51% — Freddie Mac Reports 15bp Weekly Jump
The 30-year fixed mortgage rate jumped to 6.51% as of May 21 per Freddie Mac, up 15 basis points in a week, as persistent inflation and rising Treasury yields apply upward pressure. The S&P Cotality Case-Shiller index released May 26 showed national home prices grew just 0.7% over the past year. Bankrate reports the median existing home sold in April at $417,700, with a standard monthly payment eating about 24% of the typical family's income based on HUD's $106,800 median family income. Half of the nation's 50 largest metro areas saw price declines over the past year, per Zillow. Fannie Mae's May forecast pegs the 30-year rate at 6.3% for the rest of Q2, with a slow glide toward 6.1% by year-end — though that forecast was set before the latest mortgage-rate jump.
Tax & Accounting
IRS / Planning
Higher Energy Prices Create Complex Tax Scenarios for Mid-Year Filers
With CPI running at approximately 3.8% — driven heavily by energy costs from the Strait of Hormuz closure — tax planners are revisiting estimated quarterly payment schedules for clients with variable income. The Fed's decision to hold rates has kept borrowing costs elevated, which affects deductibility calculations for businesses carrying floating-rate debt. CFOs and controllers should note that the April 29 FOMC statement's explicit reference to "global energy prices" as an inflation driver makes it increasingly likely that any near-term rate relief will be limited — keeping pressure on interest expense line items. For individuals, inflation at current levels may push more taxpayers into higher brackets ahead of any TCJA-related adjustments expected later in the year.
Real Estate Tax
Case-Shiller Flat at +0.7% YOY — What It Means for 1031 Exchanges and Property Tax Assessments
National home price appreciation of just 0.7% year-over-year — as reported by Case-Shiller on May 26 — has meaningful implications for real estate investors. For those holding properties in markets that saw price declines (roughly half of the top 50 metros per Zillow), there may be grounds to appeal property tax assessments if assessors haven't adjusted for the cooling. On the 1031 exchange front, the flat to negative price environment in many markets is giving investors more time to identify replacement properties without pressure from bidding wars, though rising mortgage rates compress cap rates and complicate deal math for leveraged buyers.
Real Estate
Market Conditions
The "Lock-In Effect" Loosens — But Affordability Remains the Decade's Defining Challenge
With mortgage rates at 6.51% and home prices up a bare 0.7% nationally, the housing market is entering a new phase: inventory is slowly returning as sellers who locked in 3% rates gradually accept that rates aren't coming back to those levels. The median monthly principal and interest payment of $2,125 — based on a 6.56% rate and the $417,700 national median price with 20% down — still represents about 24% of a typical family's gross monthly income, just inside the traditional 25–28% affordability threshold. Forecasters broadly expect the 30-year rate to stay in the 6.0–6.4% range through the end of 2026, with a slow descent toward 5.5–5.7% by 2030 only if inflation normalizes. For buyers in Chicago, the market remains more affordable than coastal metros, making this an unusual window as national price pressure eases.

Iran War · Status
Skirmishes in the Strait Even as MOU Nears
The U.S. attacked Iranian drones and a launch site around the Strait of Hormuz on Thursday hours before news of the ceasefire MOU broke — underscoring how fragile the peace is. The proposed 60-day accord would restart formal nuclear negotiations and potentially reopen the Strait, which has been closed since Iran began counter-strikes in March following the February 28 attack. Iran insists any nuclear talks begin only after an MOU ending the war is signed. Washington says Iran gave verbal commitments on nuclear concessions "in the room" — but won't confirm until formal talks begin.
U.S. Politics
Federal Reserve Chair Vacancy: Who Leads the Fed Next?
Jerome Powell's term as Fed chair expired in May 2026, creating a leadership vacuum at one of the most consequential institutions in global finance. iShares and other analysts expect a new chair to push rates toward the 3.00–3.25% range over time, but timing depends heavily on whether inflation — currently running around 3.8% — begins to ease as Iran-related energy pressures potentially subside. The April 29 FOMC vote's unusual divisiveness (four dissents) reflects just how uncertain the policy path remains until new leadership is installed.
Global Economy
Energy Crisis "Worst in Decades" — Gulf States Press for Resolution
Gulf states have called the Iran-driven energy shock "the worst global energy crisis in decades." Pakistan and Qatar are actively mediating talks, with envoys in constant contact with U.S. special envoy Steve Witkoff. Trump's reported weekend social media post that a deal to reopen the Strait is "largely negotiated" and will be announced "shortly" raised brief market hopes, though subsequent ceasefire violations have tempered expectations. Analysts note that even a partial reopening of the Strait could meaningfully reduce oil prices and ease inflationary pressure globally.

Health & Wellness
Neuroscience
USC Researchers Identify New Drug Candidates That May Reduce Alzheimer's Brain Inflammation
Scientists at USC have identified potential new drug compounds that could reduce the brain inflammation associated with Alzheimer's disease, particularly in people carrying the high-risk APOE4 gene. The research focuses on a previously underappreciated inflammatory pathway, and the May 26 findings add to a growing body of evidence that neuroinflammation — not just amyloid plaques — is a central driver of cognitive decline.
Nutrition Science
Beans and Legumes May Slash High Blood Pressure Risk by Nearly 30%
A major global analysis of studies published this week found that people who regularly consume beans, lentils, chickpeas, tofu, and soy foods had nearly 30% lower risk of high blood pressure. The findings align with longstanding cardiovascular guidance but provide some of the strongest population-level evidence to date for plant-based legume intake as a blood pressure intervention — particularly relevant given that hypertension affects roughly 1 in 2 American adults.
Vitamins & Supplements
Scientists Raise New Cautions About Vitamin B12 and Cancer — "More Isn't Always Better"
Researchers publishing May 27 in ScienceDaily are complicating the long-held view of Vitamin B12 as unconditionally beneficial. While B12 is essential for red blood cell production, DNA repair, and nerve function, scientists are finding that in high supplemental doses, the "more is better" assumption may not hold — with some studies suggesting possible links to certain cancer risks at supra-physiologic levels. The guidance: dietary B12 from food sources remains unambiguously healthy; mega-dose supplementation warrants scrutiny.
Weight Loss Science
NIH Discovers Why Ozempic and Wegovy Weight Loss Eventually Plateaus
New research from the National Institutes of Health reveals that semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) sparks different responses inside appetite-controlling brain cells over time, helping explain why the dramatic initial weight loss from GLP-1 agonists tends to plateau. The findings open new avenues for combination therapies or dosing strategies that might sustain weight loss beyond the current plateau window — a finding of major commercial interest as the GLP-1 drug category continues to dominate pharmaceutical revenue globally.
