Technology · Markets · Weekend Edition

OpenAI Blinks: IPO Pushed to 2027 as SpaceX’s 32% Post-Debut Collapse Kills Appetite for Trillion-Dollar AI Debuts — The Week the AI Bubble Started Leaking

The New York Times report that landed Thursday evening was the pin that deflated what remained of the AI market’s peak euphoria: OpenAI is leaning toward delaying its initial public offering until 2027. The proximate cause was SpaceX. Priced at $135 per share on June 12, SPCX rocketed to an intraday peak above $225 before a swift, steep retracement brought it back to approximately $152.86 by June 26 — nearly 32% off its high in less than two weeks. That trajectory told OpenAI’s board everything it needed to know about what a $1 trillion debut would face in a market that has spent June systematically questioning every AI valuation assumption it once held as settled.

The internal dynamics are more revealing than the headline. CEO Sam Altman has reportedly refused to authorize any IPO at a valuation below $1 trillion — a figure well above OpenAI’s most recent private valuation of $730–850 billion. CFO Sarah Friar has argued for waiting until 2027, citing ongoing cash burn, compute infrastructure commitments running into the tens of billions annually, and the quarterly public reporting burden against a financial profile that remains deeply unprofitable. OpenAI filed confidentially with the SEC on June 8 but emphasized timing was flexible. Advisors presented two options: delay and wait for the trillion-dollar moment, or lower the target and move now. Altman chose 2027. The advisory team’s warning that retail investors are “highly unlikely to show much enthusiasm” for OpenAI stock at current valuations reflects a broader market shift that this week made impossible to ignore: public investors in June 2026 are asking for revenue, operating leverage, and clear profitability paths — not future potential narratives, however compelling the story.

The fallout hit SoftBank first and hardest. Masayoshi Son’s firm holds approximately $65 billion in OpenAI equity — roughly a 13% stake, second only to Microsoft’s 27% — after co-leading the company’s $122 billion funding round in March 2026. SoftBank shares plunged 12.5% on the OpenAI delay news, wiping out nearly 5.6 trillion yen in market value in a single session. Son had separately announced Roze — an AI and robotics company targeting a $100 billion IPO valuation in the second half of 2026 — a timeline that now looks aspirational. The ripple hit Asian tech broadly: the KOSPI fell 5.8% Friday as Samsung and SK Hynix declined again. Japan’s Nikkei slid 0.88%.

Friday’s US session confirmed the damage. The Nasdaq posted its fifth consecutive losing session, dropping 0.24% to close at 25,297.62 — its longest losing streak since February. The S&P 500 ticked down 0.05% to 7,354.02. The Dow shed just 44 points to 51,876.11, again outperforming as defensive sectors provided ballast. Micron’s Thursday surge unwound: MU fell 6.7% Friday on profit-taking, while Broadcom dropped 3.7%, Nvidia lost 1.6%, and Arm shed 4%. For the week, the Nasdaq lost 4.6% — its worst week since April — while the S&P declined nearly 2%. The Dow, buoyed by Microsoft (+5.7%), Salesforce (+5.4%), and IBM (+5.1%), managed a 0.6% weekly gain. That divergence — Dow positive, Nasdaq deeply negative — is a market structure signal: the rotation from high-multiple growth into value and defensive names is now a trend, not a blip.

Health, Wellness & Fitness

Heat Wave Survival Guide: What the Research Says About Staying Safe When the Heat Index Hits 105°F

The heat wave arriving Monday and extending through the Fourth of July presents a genuine public health risk for the northwest suburbs. The NWS has flagged a rare Level 3 of 3 high-risk heat event for Illinois. Evidence-based guidance from the American College of Sports Medicine and the CDC: Timing is everything — core body temperature regulation is most efficient before 10 AM and after 6 PM; avoid outdoor exertion during peak afternoon hours on triple-digit heat-index days. Hydration precedes thirst — by the time you feel thirsty during heat stress you are already 1–2% dehydrated; drink 16–24 oz of water in the two hours before any outdoor activity. Cooling center access matters — Cook County and Kane County both operate cooling centers during heat emergencies. Heat stroke warning signs: stopping sweating during exertion, hot and red skin, confusion or slurred speech. These require immediate 911 contact, not a wait-and-see approach. For the northwest suburbs — Hoffman Estates, Schaumburg, Streamwood — the worst window is expected to be Tuesday through Thursday. Check on elderly neighbors and those without reliable air conditioning. The body doesn’t negotiate with 105°F heat index values.

Q2 Closes Sunday June 29

The Tax-Loss Harvesting Window Is Open Right Now — Bitcoin Down 45% From ATH, AI Stocks Off Their Highs, Q2 Ends Sunday

Sunday, June 29 closes the second quarter of the tax year and with it a practical decision window many investors overlook. Bitcoin is down nearly 45% from its October 2025 all-time high and approximately 19% over the past month. Micron surged and pulled back. Broadcom fell nearly 4% on the week. AI software positions that ran up through May have pulled back substantially. For taxable accounts, these unrealized losses represent tax-loss harvesting opportunities worth acting on before Q3 begins. The mechanics: selling a position that is down and realizing the loss allows you to offset capital gains elsewhere in the portfolio, or deduct up to $3,000 of net capital losses against ordinary income annually (remainder carried forward). The IRS wash-sale rule prohibits repurchasing the “substantially identical” security within 30 days — but you can immediately replace a Bitcoin ETF with a different Bitcoin ETF, or swap Micron for Applied Materials to maintain sector exposure while harvesting the loss. Note: this applies only to taxable brokerage accounts; IRA and 401(k) positions have no tax-loss harvesting benefit. With Bitcoin near $60,000 and markets in Extreme Fear, the psychological pull is to wait for a recovery. But if you have gains elsewhere that need offsetting, waiting costs real after-tax dollars. Q2 ends Sunday. The window closes Monday.

(noun, from French: capituler, “to make terms”) — in financial markets, the point at which a critical mass of investors abandon hope of price recovery and sell at a loss, producing a sharp final decline that often marks a cycle bottom. Capitulation is recognized in retrospect — impossible to identify in real time with certainty. The current Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index at 13 is historically associated with capitulation-adjacent conditions.
