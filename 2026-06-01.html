Current Events & Geopolitics

Iran Ceasefire
Ceasefire Holds Into Week 2 — Iran MOU Deadline for Formal Treaty Now June 15
The Iran–U.S. ceasefire MOU is entering its second week intact. No kinetic incidents were reported over the Memorial Day weekend. The Strait of Hormuz has seen 12 commercial tanker transits since reopening Sunday. The formal treaty deadline embedded in the MOU is June 15 — if both parties agree to convert the MOU into a binding instrument. Failure to meet that deadline doesn't automatically restart hostilities, but it removes the legal framework. State Department officials say talks in Oman are "proceeding constructively."
Tax

One Big Beautiful Bill: Senate Vote Expected This Week

Trump's OBBBA heads to a full Senate vote this week. Key provisions: extended TCJA rates, no tax on tips, no tax on overtime, $2,500 child tax credit, SALT cap eliminated. CPA firms are modeling client impacts now. If passed, retroactive provisions could affect Q2 estimated tax payments due June 15.

SpaceX IPO

SpaceX Roadshow Underway — Demand "Unprecedented"

The SpaceX IPO roadshow is live. Goldman reports "unprecedented" institutional demand. The order book is reportedly 8x oversubscribed on day one. Starlink's 40% annual revenue growth and the Starship program's commercial viability are the key pitches. Retail allocation is expected to be minimal — under 5% of total shares.

Economy

ISM Manufacturing Returns to Expansion — PMI 51.2

The ISM Manufacturing PMI for May came in at 51.2 — back above 50 (expansion territory) for the first time since February. New orders surged on Iran ceasefire-related inventory restocking. The data adds to the picture of a broad-based economic rebound as the oil shock fades.

Health, Wellness & Fitness

Cancer

Dietary Fat Type — Not Amount — Drives Pancreatic Cancer Risk

A new study finds the kind of fat consumed may matter more than total fat intake for pancreatic cancer risk. One fat type promoted tumor growth; another cut incidence in half. Olive oil and omega-3-rich fats are the protective categories. Qualitative dietary guidance is the emerging frontier — not just "eat less fat."

Longevity

Thymus Health Predicts Longevity — New AI Study

Mass General Brigham used AI on CT scans of tens of thousands of adults and found healthier thymus glands correlate strongly with better cancer survival and aging outcomes. The thymus shrinks after puberty, but its midlife function is a meaningful longevity signal. Preserve your immune infrastructure while you can.

Sleep

Melatonin Boosts DNA Repair for Night Shift Workers

New research suggests melatonin supplements help night shift workers strengthen the body's DNA repair processes, offsetting some cellular damage from disrupted circadian rhythms. Low-cost intervention that may partially mitigate the elevated cancer and metabolic disease risk associated with night shift work.

Random Fact

June 1 Is the Official Start of Hurricane Season

The Atlantic hurricane season officially begins June 1 every year. NOAA's 2026 outlook calls for an above-normal season — 17 to 21 named storms, 8 to 10 hurricanes, and 4 to 5 major hurricanes. La Niña conditions and record warm Atlantic sea surface temperatures are the primary drivers of the elevated forecast.

Word of the Day

Inflection (n.)

A point of significant change in direction or rate of change. The Iran ceasefire and PCE print together represent an inflection in the macro environment — from tightening and conflict to easing and détente. Markets love inflection points; they create the conditions where the most money changes hands in the shortest time.
