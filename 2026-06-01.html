Iran MOU Signed — Markets Surge to New Records as Ceasefire Takes Effect

U.S. and Iranian officials signed a memorandum of understanding in Oman on Monday formalizing the ceasefire that had been in preliminary discussions since late May. The formal signing sent oil down to $84.60 — a four-month low — and drove stocks to fresh records. The S&P 500 hit 7,621, the Dow crossed 51,188, and the Nasdaq gained 0.55%. Airlines, consumer discretionary, and transports led the advance on the oil relief.

Bitcoin pulled back to $104,800 as some risk-on capital rotated from crypto into equities. The rotation is consistent with institutional rebalancing at the start of a new month. The fundamental Bitcoin picture remains constructive — Strategic Reserve legislation continues to advance in the Senate.

Federal Reserve

Warsh Confirmation Vote Wednesday — Easing Bias Removal Imminent

Kevin Warsh's Senate confirmation vote is scheduled for Wednesday, June 3. With bipartisan support confirmed, his confirmation is a formality. The real market event is his first post-confirmation public statement, expected to signal a formal removal of the FOMC's "easing bias" — the forward guidance that the next move would likely be a cut. That language removal would be a meaningful hawkish pivot.

Iran Ceasefire MOU — 90-Day Monitoring Period Begins

The signed MOU initiates a 90-day monitoring period overseen by Qatari and Swiss intermediaries. U.S. forces in the region remain at elevated readiness but active operations have ceased. ⚾ Cubs

Cubs Win 7th Straight — Best Start to June Since 2016

The Cubs extended their winning streak to 7 with a 6-2 victory over the Reds in Monday's home opener of the June homestand at Wrigley. Shota Imanaga pitched 7 dominant innings, allowing just 1 earned run. The Cubs are 32-24, their best record through June 1 since the 2016 championship season. The offense is clicking on all cylinders — 6 hitters posting OPS above .800.

Health & Wellness
Sleep Science

7-Hour Sleep Window Optimal for Cognitive Performance Over 45

A new Cambridge and Fudan University study of nearly 500,000 adults across multiple countries confirms that for people over 45, 7 hours of sleep per night is associated with optimal cognitive function and mental health outcomes. Both less and more sleep correlate with cognitive decline — the U-shaped curve is particularly steep above age 55.

Fitness

Grip Strength as a Longevity Biomarker — Simple Test Predicts Outcomes

Multiple longitudinal studies confirm grip strength — measurable with a simple hand dynamometer — as one of the strongest predictors of future mobility, independence, and all-cause mortality. For men over 50, a grip strength below 26 kg is associated with significantly elevated health risk. Farmers carry, dead hangs, and heavy compound lifts all improve it.

Random Fact

Chicago Was the First City to Use a Street Grid System Across an Entire Metropolitan Area

After the Great Chicago Fire of 1871, the city rebuilt using a systematic street grid that's now one of the most consistent in any major American city. The grid uses State and Madison as the zero coordinates — every address in the city references these two streets.

Word of the Day

Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)

A non-binding agreement between parties expressing their mutual intent to work toward a common goal — used in diplomacy, business, and real estate. Today's Iran ceasefire MOU is technically non-binding, which is exactly why Armstrong views it with skepticism.
