Markets & Finance

June Opens
June Begins at Record Highs — Warsh Era Starts, Iran Deal Pending
June 1 opens with U.S. equity markets at all-time highs after the best May since 2003. The S&P 500 closed May up 5%, the Nasdaq +8.1%, and the Dow at 51,032. Futures are modestly positive Monday morning. The market's agenda for June is clear: Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting on June 16–17, the long-awaited Iran MOU signature, and the Senate's handling of the OBBBA tax bill. Any one of those three events could set the tone for the summer. Bitcoin spot ETFs saw $2.3 billion in outflows during May despite BTC holding near $73,500 — an unusual divergence that analysts attribute to profit-taking by early institutional adopters.
Federal Reserve

June 17 — Warsh's First FOMC Decision

Kevin Warsh chairs his first Federal Open Market Committee meeting June 16–17. The fed funds rate is at 3.50–3.75%. Markets expect a hold but are watching for any shift in the committee's forward guidance language. Warsh's first press conference will be scrutinized for signals on his tolerance for above-target inflation versus his stated preference for credibility restoration.

Iran / Oil

MOU Awaiting Trump Signature — Deal or No Deal?

The 60-day ceasefire MOU between the U.S. and Iran is agreed upon by negotiators but still awaits President Trump's personal sign-off. Trump's additions — stricter missile verification, expanded inspection rights — remain contested by Tehran. The window is open; the question is whether either side blinks first. Oil is trading with a binary outcome premium.

Macro & Federal Reserve

PCE / Inflation

PCE at 3.8% — No Relief in Sight Before Fall

April PCE held at 3.8% year-over-year — the highest in three years. Core PCE at 3.3%. Services inflation, particularly in rent and insurance, is driving persistence. The Fed's path to 2% now extends well into 2027 under most forecasts. Warsh inherits a hotter-than-target inflation environment with limited room to ease.

Labor Market

Jobs Report Friday — Consensus 145K New Jobs

Friday's May employment report is the week's key economic event. Consensus forecasts call for approximately 145,000 new jobs and an unemployment rate holding at 4.1%. A strong number would reinforce the "higher for longer" rates narrative; a miss could reopen the rate cut conversation ahead of the June 17 FOMC.

Current Events

Iran
Iran MOU: The Last Mile Is the Hardest
Diplomats describe the Iran ceasefire MOU as "95% complete" — but that last 5% is the hardest. Trump's insisted additions to the verification protocol and missile restrictions represent genuine red lines for Tehran, which views any constraints on its missile program as a concession too far. U.S. officials counter that a deal without verification is worse than no deal. The Muscat talks are continuing through the weekend; a decision one way or the other is expected by Wednesday. The market has priced in roughly 70% probability of a deal based on oil's current level — a collapse would push WTI toward $85 immediately.
Domestic

OBBBA Reaches Senate — Deficit Hawks Mobilizing

The Senate received the House-passed OBBBA Friday. Majority Leader Thune has promised a floor vote before July 4. The SALT expansion, 199A extension, and PMI deductibility restoration are the provisions most important to middle and upper-middle income taxpayers. The fiscal cost — estimated at $3.8 trillion over 10 years — is drawing opposition from a bloc of five Republican senators.

Economy

Memorial Day Travel Record — But K-Shaped Recovery Deepens

AAA's record 43 million Memorial Day travelers masked a deepening consumer bifurcation: affluent households are spending freely while lower-income consumers show rising delinquency rates and reduced discretionary spending. The "K-shaped" economy — up for the top, down for the bottom — is now the consensus characterization of the 2026 consumer landscape.

Real Estate

National

Pending Sales Fall 3.2% in April — 3rd Straight Monthly Drop

The NAR's pending home sales index fell 3.2% month-over-month in April — the third consecutive monthly decline. The lock-in effect remains dominant: homeowners sitting on sub-3% pandemic mortgages have no financial incentive to sell and upgrade at 6.5%. Until rates drop meaningfully, transaction volume stays depressed.

Chicago

North Shore Condos Buck the Slowdown

Evanston and Wilmette condos are moving in under 20 days with multiple offers — a pocket of demand strength in an otherwise sluggish market. Downsizing baby boomers and remote workers are driving condo demand on the North Shore, while single-family activity remains muted at current rates.

Tax & Accounting

June Planning Calendar: What's on the Tax Radar This Month

June is a quieter month on the tax calendar but several items deserve attention. Q2 estimated tax payments are due June 16 — the same day the FOMC meets. If the OBBBA passes with retroactive effective dates, Q2 estimates may need to be recalculated. Illinois filers should note that the state has not conformed to all federal changes; a federal SALT expansion does not automatically change Illinois' own tax treatment. For business owners, the June 30 deadline for certain retirement plan establishment elections in some plan types is worth confirming with your plan administrator. Payroll tax deposits continue on their regular schedule regardless of federal legislative action.

Armstrong Economics

Armstrong: June Opens the Summer Cycle — Watch the Dollar

Martin Armstrong's Socrates system enters June flagging the dollar as the key variable for the summer cycle. A strong dollar driven by capital inflows — which his model has been predicting — acts as a deflationary force globally but is bullish for U.S. equities as international capital seeks dollar-denominated assets. Armstrong has noted that the Iran conflict's resolution, when it comes, will not end the capital flight dynamic: the structural problems in Europe, China, and emerging markets that are driving flows into the U.S. predate the conflict and will persist after it. His September–October cyclical test window remains the key watch date for a potential correction within the larger bull structure.

Health & Wellness

Sleep

Consistent Sleep Schedule Matters More Than Hours Alone

New research from the University of Michigan found that sleep timing consistency — going to bed and waking at the same time daily — provides longevity benefits independent of total sleep duration. Irregular sleepers showed elevated inflammatory markers and higher cardiovascular risk even when they logged adequate total hours. The fix is behavioral, not pharmaceutical.

Metabolic Health

Muscle Mass at 50 Predicts Quality of Life at 75

A 25-year longitudinal study published in JAMA Network Open found that lean muscle mass at age 50 was the strongest predictor of functional independence at age 75 — stronger than cardiovascular fitness, cognitive scores, or bone density. Resistance training in midlife isn't optional for healthy aging; it's the mechanism by which healthy aging happens.

The Summer That Will Define the Year

June 1 is a starting line. The next 90 days will answer the questions May left open: Does Iran get a deal or does the conflict reignite? Does Warsh signal a hawkish reset or work within the existing framework? Does the OBBBA pass the Senate intact or get carved up by deficit hawks? That's a setup that rewards patience, humility, and discipline. Stay long, stay alert, and don't confuse a good May with a guaranteed June. See you tomorrow.

Random Fact

June Is Named After the Roman Goddess Juno

June takes its name from Juno, the Roman goddess of marriage and queen of the gods. June was historically considered the luckiest month for marriages — a tradition that persists today, as June remains one of the two most popular wedding months in the United States alongside October.

Word of the Day

Forward Guidance

Central bank communication about the future path of monetary policy. When the Fed says "we expect rates to remain elevated," that's forward guidance — a tool to manage market expectations without actually changing rates. Warsh's first press conference on June 17 is primarily a forward guidance event: what does he signal about the next 12 months?
