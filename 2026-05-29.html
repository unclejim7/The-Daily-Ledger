Current Events & Geopolitics

Breaking
Iran–U.S. Ceasefire MOU Signed in Oman — Hormuz Reopening Imminent, Markets Explode Higher
The United States and Iran signed a Memorandum of Understanding in Muscat, Oman on Friday morning, establishing a 90-day ceasefire framework and a staged reopening of the Strait of Hormuz. Oil plunged from $95 to $88 on the news. The S&P 500 surged to a new record. Bitcoin hit $112,000. The Dow gained 310 points. The deal includes a U.S. commitment to pause new sanctions in exchange for Iran halting drone and missile proxy activity. Critics called it a capitulation; markets called it a relief valve. The Kuwait missile attack of Thursday appears to have been Iran's final leverage play before the deal closed.
PCE Data

Core PCE Comes in at 2.9% — First Sub-3% in Two Years

Thursday's Personal Consumption Expenditures print showed core inflation at 2.9% year-over-year — the first reading below 3% since April 2024. The number reignited rate-cut hopes. Combined with the Iran deal, it was the best possible outcome for risk assets heading into a holiday weekend.

Markets

Warsh Era Preview: New Chair Signals "Data-Dependent Patience"

Kevin Warsh, in his first public comments since being confirmed as Fed Chair designate, told the WSJ he would be "data-dependent with patience" — code for: don't expect rate cuts just because one PCE print came in soft. His first FOMC meeting is June 16–17. The market took the statement as mildly hawkish and barely reacted.

Economy

Q1 GDP Revised Upward to 2.4%

The Bureau of Economic Analysis revised Q1 2026 GDP growth upward to 2.4% from the preliminary 2.1% estimate. Consumer spending and business investment were both revised higher. The revision, combined with the PCE print and Iran deal, paints the most optimistic economic picture since late 2024.

Health, Wellness & Fitness

Fitness

Morning Exercise Found to Maximize Fat Burning

A new metabolic study found that exercise performed before 9 AM burns 20% more fat than the same workout done in the afternoon — due to circadian rhythm effects on fatty acid oxidation. The effect is most pronounced for moderate-intensity cardio. Fasted morning workouts amplify it further, though performance suffers slightly without pre-workout carbohydrates.

Mental Health

Cold Water Immersion Reduces Cortisol by 40% — Study

A Norwegian study of 120 adults found that regular cold water immersion (cold showers or ice baths, 3x per week) reduced morning cortisol levels by an average of 40% over 8 weeks. Participants also reported improved sleep quality and reduced anxiety. The effect appeared to plateau after 6 weeks, suggesting the body adapts.

Longevity

Mediterranean Diet Cuts Dementia Risk by 23% in New Meta-Analysis

A sweeping meta-analysis of 28 studies and over 400,000 participants found that high adherence to a Mediterranean diet (olive oil, fish, vegetables, legumes, nuts) was associated with a 23% reduction in dementia risk. The effect was strongest in people who adopted the diet before age 55. Olive oil emerged as the single most protective component.

Fact · Word · Quote

Random Fact

The Strait of Hormuz Carries 20% of Global Oil

The Strait of Hormuz — 21 miles wide at its narrowest — carries approximately 20% of the world's oil supply and 18% of global LNG. Its closure or disruption is the single most impactful supply shock the energy market can absorb. Every $1 increase in Brent crude costs the U.S. economy approximately $1.2 billion per year in higher energy costs.

Word of the Day

Détente (n.)

The easing of strained relations between nations, especially through diplomacy. The Iran MOU is classic détente — not peace, not normalization, but a mutual de-escalation of hostilities to give both sides room to breathe. The word comes from French: literally "loosening." Markets love détente almost as much as they love actual peace.
