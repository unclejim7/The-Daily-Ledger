S&P Hits Record Close as Iran Deal Framework Signals Ceasefire — PCE Inflation Complicates Fed Math

The S&P 500 closed at a record 7,609 Friday as the outlines of an Iran ceasefire framework emerged from the Oman talks, sending oil down 4% to $87.80 and giving markets a risk-on boost heading into the long holiday weekend. The Dow added 172 points while the Nasdaq gained 0.42%. All three indexes closed out a strong week with the Dow up 1.2% for the four-day stretch.

The good news from Iran was partially offset by a hotter-than-expected PCE inflation reading — the Fed's preferred gauge — which came in at 3.8% year-over-year, above the 3.5% consensus. The data reinforces Warsh's hawkish posture and dims the case for any near-term rate relief.

🌍 Geopolitics
Iran Breakthrough

Iran Deal Framework Agreed in Oman — Full Ceasefire Contingent on Enrichment Freeze

U.S. and Iranian negotiators agreed to a preliminary framework in Muscat Friday that would halt Iran's uranium enrichment above 20% in exchange for partial sanctions relief. The framework is not a final deal — it must be ratified by both governments — but markets treated it as a significant de-escalation signal.

Oil's 4% drop to $87.80 reflects the market's view that full Iranian oil exports could re-enter global supply within 90 days if the deal is finalized. Analysts estimate a full deal could add 1.2–1.5 million barrels per day to global supply — enough to push WTI into the low-to-mid $80s.

🏛️ Federal Reserve
PCE Data

PCE at 3.8% Crushes Rate-Cut Hopes — Warsh's First Meeting June 16

The April PCE reading of 3.8% was nearly 0.3 percentage points above consensus and reinforces the case for an extended hold. Core PCE — excluding food and energy — came in at 3.2%. Warsh's first FOMC meeting is June 16–17; a hold is near-certain, but the tone of the statement will be carefully parsed for policy signals.

Warsh View

New Chair: "Inflation Is Not Beaten Until It's at 2%" — June Meeting Preview

Warsh reiterated in pre-confirmation testimony that the Fed should communicate a clear, rules-based path back to 2% rather than declaring premature victory. His approach signals a more formal, less discretionary policy framework than his predecessor — a structural shift in how markets should read Fed communications going forward.

📊 Armstrong Economics
Market Talk — May 29, 2026

Armstrong: Iran Framework Is a "Temporary Lull" — War Cycle Peak Still Ahead

Martin Armstrong cautioned Friday that the Iran ceasefire framework should be treated as a tactical pause rather than a structural resolution of the War Cycle. His models project geopolitical tension remaining elevated through 2027, with multiple potential flashpoints beyond Iran including North Korea, Taiwan, and Eastern European theater.

"Oil markets are pricing a deal that isn't done," Armstrong noted. He sees the $87 oil level as an overcorrection driven by euphoria, with WTI likely to bounce back toward $92–95 if the framework encounters the predictable ratification obstacles. Gold's pullback, he added, is a buying opportunity for those with a multi-year horizon.

Holiday Wellness

Grilling Smart: Marinades Cut Carcinogen Formation by Up to 88%

Memorial Day grilling season is here — and research from the American Institute for Cancer Research shows that marinating meat before grilling reduces the formation of heterocyclic amines (HCAs), cancer-linked compounds formed by high-heat cooking, by up to 88%. Acid-based marinades (citrus, vinegar) are most effective; beer marinades also show significant protective effects.

🎲 Closing Notes
Random Fact of the Day

Memorial Day Was Originally Called "Decoration Day"

Memorial Day originated as Decoration Day after the Civil War, when communities decorated the graves of fallen soldiers. It was officially proclaimed in 1868 by General John Logan. The holiday wasn't standardized to the last Monday in May until 1971 with the Uniform Monday Holiday Act — which also moved several other federal holidays to Mondays to create three-day weekends.

Word of the Day

Ceasefire

A temporary suspension of fighting agreed upon by warring parties. Distinct from a peace treaty — a ceasefire halts hostilities but does not resolve underlying political disputes. The Iran framework announced today is closer to a ceasefire than a peace treaty: enrichment stops, sanctions ease, but the fundamental geopolitical tension between Washington and Tehran remains unresolved.
