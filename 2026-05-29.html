S&P Posts 9th Straight Weekly Win — Longest Streak Since 2023; Dimon Warns Markets "Exuberant"

🔭 Armstrong Economics

Martin Armstrong · Weekend Market Talk

Nine Straight Weekly Wins — Armstrong's Model Flags Rising Volatility Ahead

Armstrong's computer model has long identified 2026 as a transition year — not necessarily a crash year, but a year when the character of markets changes. Nine straight weekly S&P gains is the kind of run his model would flag as a setup for mean reversion. The ECM's downcycle midpoint arriving in June is a structural signal, not a timing call — but the combination of record equity markets, sticky inflation, geopolitical risk premium in energy, and an unresolved war suggests the "exuberance" Dimon flagged is real. Armstrong would say the capital is still flowing into US equities as a flight to safety — paradoxically, American stocks are the world's best house in a bad neighborhood right now.

Bitcoin is seeing mild ETF outflow pressure as institutional capital rotates toward AI-driven equities — particularly Nvidia and its ecosystem — which are generating returns that make even BTC look slow. The geopolitical risk premium that should theoretically benefit Bitcoin as a hard asset is being offset by the flight-to-quality bid in US equities. 30-Year Fixed Stays Elevated as Oil Keeps Inflation Sticky

Heading into the Memorial Day weekend, 30-year fixed rates remain in the 6.5%–6.6% range across major lenders. Chicago Market

"Weird" Spring — Listings Rising, Buyers Still Cautious

Chicago-area real estate brokers describe the spring 2026 season as "weird" — inventory is rising for the first time in years, but buyer demand hasn't kept pace. The lock-in effect keeps many would-be sellers sidelined (why move and give up a 3% mortgage?), but those who must sell are finding a more balanced market than at any point since 2021. Price appreciation is flattening toward 2–3% annually.

💊 Health & Wellness

8,500 Steps a Day May Be the Key to Keeping Weight Off After Dieting

A new international analysis of weight maintenance data found that people who walked approximately 8,500 steps per day were significantly more successful at maintaining weight loss compared to those walking fewer steps. The finding is notable because it's achievable without structured exercise — a brisk walking routine integrated into daily life appears sufficient. The researchers emphasized consistency over intensity.

🎲 Closing Notes

Random Fact of the Day

Memorial Day originated in Chicago.

While its exact origins are disputed, one of the strongest claims belongs to Decoration Day ceremonies held in 1868 at Rosehill Cemetery in Chicago. The holiday was established to honor Union soldiers who died in the Civil War and evolved into the national Memorial Day observed today.

Word of the Day

Contango

A futures market condition where prices for future delivery are higher than current spot prices — implying the market expects current conditions (like the Iran war premium on oil) to ease over time. Today's oil market is in moderate contango, reflecting traders' bet that a peace deal eventually comes.
