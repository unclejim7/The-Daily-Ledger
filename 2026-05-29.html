S&P Hits Record Close as Iran Deal Framework Signals Ceasefire — PCE Inflation Complicates Fed Math

The S&P 500 closed at a record 7,609 Friday as the outlines of an Iran ceasefire framework emerged from the Oman talks, sending oil down 4% to $87.80 and giving markets a risk-on boost heading into the long holiday weekend. The Dow added 172 points while the Nasdaq gained 0.42%. All three indexes closed out a strong week with the Dow up 1.2% for the four-day stretch.

The good news from Iran was partially offset by a hotter-than-expected PCE inflation reading — the Fed's preferred gauge came in at 3.8% year-over-year for April, above the 3.5% consensus. Kevin Warsh's hawkish Fed is now dealing with an inflation problem that hasn't resolved. June rate cuts are off the table.

Federal Reserve / Inflation

PCE Comes in Hot — June Cut Odds Collapse to Near Zero

April PCE inflation at 3.8% YOY is a problem for a Fed already pivoting hawkish under Warsh. Core PCE (ex-food and energy) came in at 3.2% — the highest since October 2025. CME FedWatch futures now show near-zero probability of a June cut. The FOMC's June 16–17 meeting is now widely expected to hold and signal a higher-for-longer stance through at least Q3.

Geopolitics

Iran Ceasefire Framework — Details Sparse, Markets Buy It Anyway

Oman-mediated talks produced a preliminary framework agreement in principle Thursday night. Details remain sparse — the White House confirmed only that "meaningful progress" was made. Oil's 4% single-session drop suggests markets are pricing in a real de-escalation. Defense stocks dipped slightly on the news while airline stocks surged on lower fuel cost expectations.

Health & Wellness
Research

Resistance Training 2x/Week Cuts All-Cause Mortality by 23%

A landmark meta-analysis published in the British Journal of Sports Medicine pooling 1.5 million participants confirms that two or more weekly sessions of resistance training reduces all-cause mortality by 23% and cardiovascular mortality by 31%. The benefit plateaus around three sessions per week — more is not necessarily better.

Wellness

Cold Plunge Science Update — Benefits Confirmed, Optimal Protocol Clarified

New research from the University of Oslo clarifies the optimal cold water immersion protocol: 11-15 minutes per week total (not per session) at 50-59°F produces maximal norepinephrine release and mood benefit. Daily 2-minute plunges appear superior to weekly longer sessions for sympathetic nervous system adaptation.

Armstrong Economics
Martin Armstrong

Iran Deal Framework — Armstrong Warns Ceasefire May Be Temporary

Armstrong's latest post cautions that the Oman framework is consistent with his War Cycle model's "temporary lull" pattern — where combatants reach an operational pause rather than a genuine political resolution. He points to similar frameworks in past cycles that collapsed within 3-6 months. His model suggests the War Cycle peak is still ahead, potentially in Q4 2026 or Q1 2027. Gold's persistence above $3,350 despite the ceasefire news is, in his view, the market's tacit agreement.

Random Fact

Chicago Has More Movable Bridges Than Any City on Earth

The Chicago River system includes 38 movable bridges — more than any other city in the world. Most are bascule bridges (drawbridges) that lift to allow river traffic. The bridges were central to Chicago's 19th-century grain and lumber trade.

Word of the Day

Stagflation

The combination of stagnant economic growth and persistent inflation — the scenario that most concerns economists when PCE runs hot while leading indicators soften. The 1970s stagflation era is the canonical reference point. With PCE at 3.8%, the word is creeping back into policy discussions.
