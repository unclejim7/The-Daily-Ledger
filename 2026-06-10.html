Dow Sheds 953 Points as CPI Hits Three-Year High — US Resumes Iran Strikes

May inflation surges to 4.2% on energy prices; US launches fresh strikes on Iran; bond market signals possible Fed rate hike ahead of Warsh's first FOMC.

Wall Street suffered its worst session since the Iran war began after the Bureau of Labor Statistics reported May CPI of 4.2% year-over-year — the highest reading since April 2023 and driven overwhelmingly by an energy shock from the Iran conflict. The Nasdaq fell 1.98% to close at 25,169.50 while the Dow dropped 953 points through the 50,000 floor to 49,918.78. US Central Command simultaneously confirmed fresh airstrikes on Iran, as President Trump warned Tehran would "pay the price" for stalled negotiations. Iran countered with drone strikes targeting Bahrain, Kuwait and Jordan. The ECB separately raised rates to combat war-driven European inflation. Markets enter Thursday in a nervous wait-and-see mode ahead of Warsh's first FOMC meeting June 16–17.

CPI Breakdown

Energy Accounts for 60%+ of Monthly Inflation Surge

Gasoline prices surged 40.5% year-over-year. Energy overall rose 3.9% in May and 23.5% annually. Core CPI came in at a softer 2.9% annually, with the monthly gain at 0.2% — below the 0.3% estimate. Food prices also trended higher. Economists say May could represent the CPI peak if oil prices don't spike further.

Fed Watch

Bond Market Prices In Rate Hike by October

The 2-year Treasury at ~4.15% is pricing meaningful probability of a rate hike, well above the Fed's 3.50–3.75% band. The White House placed Warsh at the Fed with an unofficial mandate to cut rates — but the bond market is communicating the opposite.

Geopolitics

Iran-US Cycle Escalates — Peace Talks Stall Again

The US launched a second round of strikes even as both countries were described as "close to a deal." Iran insists on maintaining Hormuz leverage; the US insists on energy reopening. Trump threatened to capture Kharg Island. No framework deal has been announced. The war began February 28 when the US and Israel struck Iran.

Brain Health

New Alzheimer's Target Identified — Experimental Compound Shows Promise

Researchers identified a new Alzheimer's target and created a compound that blocks a damaging process inside brain cells. In mice, the treatment slowed nerve cell loss and reduced Alzheimer's-related changes. Human trials are the next step.

Remote Work

Remote Workers More Isolated and Anxious — Study

A study found people in remote jobs reported greater social isolation, anxiety and sadness compared to non-remote workers. Researchers said the solution isn't mandatory RTO — it's intentional social design for distributed teams. Office-for-office's-sake doesn't fix the underlying problem.

Word of the Day
Stagflation
An economic condition combining stagnant growth with high inflation — the worst of both worlds. First coined in the 1960s by British politician Iain Macleod, it came back into the lexicon after 2022 and is making unwelcome appearances again.
Quote of the Day

— Milton Friedman. The Nobel laureate's quip hits differently when CPI is running at a three-year high and nobody voted for it.

From the Desk

The common thread is energy — both literally and figuratively. Iran has weaponized oil. The Fed is caught without ammunition. Warsh inherits a job nobody envied five years ago, and the job description keeps getting harder. Stay alert, stay informed, stay skeptical of anyone who tells you this is transitory.
