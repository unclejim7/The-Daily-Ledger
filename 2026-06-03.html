🌍 Geopolitics
Ceasefire Collapse

Iran-Backed Drones Strike Kuwait Airport — GCC Demands Emergency UN Security Council Session

Iranian-backed Houthi-affiliated forces launched a coordinated drone strike on Kuwait International Airport Wednesday morning, disrupting flight operations and injuring 14 airport workers. The attack — attributed to Iran-backed proxies rather than the Iranian military directly — gives Tehran plausible deniability while effectively voiding the spirit of the Oman MOU.

The GCC called an emergency summit and demanded a UN Security Council session. The U.S. State Department issued a sharp warning that sanctions relief tied to the MOU is now on hold pending investigation. Iran's foreign ministry denied direct involvement, claiming the attack was carried out by "resistance forces" acting independently.

Geopolitical Stress

Chronic News Exposure Linked to Anxiety — Strategies for Staying Informed Without Damage

Research from the American Psychological Association confirms that continuous news consumption — particularly around conflict and financial crises — is strongly associated with increased anxiety, sleep disruption, and reduced wellbeing. The recommended approach: two scheduled news-check periods per day (morning and evening), no news within 90 minutes of bedtime, and a curated rather than algorithmic news diet.

Kuwait Is the World's 6th Largest Oil Producer Per Capita

Kuwait produces approximately 2.7 million barrels of oil per day with a population of only 4.8 million — making it one of the highest per-capita oil producers in the world. Kuwait International Airport handles over 12 million passengers annually and is the primary hub for Kuwait Airways. Wednesday's drone strike is the first attack on Kuwaiti civilian infrastructure in decades.

Word of the Day

Plausible Deniability

The ability of an actor to deny knowledge of or responsibility for an action, because there is insufficient evidence to prove complicity. Iran's use of proxy forces to conduct the Kuwait attack provides exactly this — Tehran can deny direct involvement while effectively voiding the ceasefire. It's a tool that great powers and their proxies have used throughout history to pursue strategic goals while avoiding direct attribution.
