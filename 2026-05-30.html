Markets
Dell Surges 33% as AI Infrastructure Boom Drives Record Earnings
Dell Technologies delivered the most stunning single-session gain in its history Friday, rocketing 33% after blowout quarterly results powered by surging demand for AI-configured servers and storage infrastructure. Macro & Federal Reserve

Federal Reserve

Warsh Confirmed — June 17 Is The First Test

Kevin Warsh was confirmed as Fed Chair earlier this week. His first FOMC decision comes June 17. Markets are pricing a hold at 3.50–3.75%, but Warsh's known hawkish lean — and his proximity to President Trump — means every word of his first press conference will be parsed for signals on the path to further cuts.

Inflation

PCE at 3.8% — Stickier Than the Fed Wants

The April Personal Consumption Expenditures price index came in at 3.8% year-over-year, unchanged from March. Core PCE, the Fed's preferred inflation gauge, held at 3.6%. Services inflation remains the stubborn component. Until housing and services costs cool further, aggressive rate cuts appear off the table regardless of chair.

Current Events

Geopolitics
Iran MOU Talks Reach Critical Stage — Trump Adding Tougher Demands
Negotiations between the U.S. and Iran over a memorandum of understanding to end the two-month military conflict entered what both sides describe as a final stage this week. However, President Trump is personally editing the text to add stricter verification requirements and expanded restrictions on Iran's missile program — provisions Tehran has so far resisted. The military standoff has been a simmering drag on global markets since late March; a signed deal would be a significant bullish catalyst for equities and a bearish one for oil. Diplomats in Muscat are calling the window "days, not weeks," though prior deadlines have slipped before.
Domestic

OBBBA Tax Bill Heads to Senate — SALT Fight Looms

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act passed the House narrowly and now faces a tougher road in the Senate. The SALT deduction cap expansion — indexed to $40,000 for most filers — drew support from high-tax state Republicans but faces pushback from deficit hawks who want a lower threshold or sunset provision.

Economy

Memorial Day Travel Smashes Records

AAA projected 43 million Americans traveled more than 50 miles for the Memorial Day weekend — a record, topping last year by 2.3%. Strong consumer spending on experiences continues to confound recession forecasters, even as credit card delinquency rates tick higher for lower-income brackets.

Chicago Median Home Price Hits $335K

The Chicago metro median sale price rose to $335,000 in April, up 4.2% year-over-year, according to the Illinois Realtors Association. Inventory remains tight at 2.1 months of supply — well below the 4–6 months considered balanced. Northwest suburbs and the Near West Side are seeing the most competitive bidding environments.

Mortgage Rates Hold at 6.53% — Affordability Squeeze Continues

Freddie Mac's weekly survey showed the 30-year fixed rate unchanged at 6.53%. The combination of elevated rates and high home prices keeps the monthly payment on a median-priced home nearly 40% above pre-pandemic norms, depressing transaction volume even as demand remains latent.

Tax & Accounting

SALT Expansion and PMI Deductibility: What the OBBBA Means for Chicagoland Filers

Two provisions in the One Big Beautiful Bill Act are drawing attention from Illinois taxpayers. First, the SALT deduction cap would rise from $10,000 to approximately $40,000 for most filers — a meaningful change for Chicago-area homeowners paying above-average property taxes. Second, the bill would restore deductibility of private mortgage insurance premiums, which had expired. Tax professionals are advising clients not to make financial moves based on the bill until it clears the Senate, as key provisions remain in flux and the effective date for any changes is uncertain.

Armstrong Economics

Armstrong: The Cycle Turns on Geopolitics, Not Fed Policy

Martin Armstrong's models continue to flag the June–July window as pivotal for sovereign debt markets globally. His long-standing thesis holds that capital is fleeing European and emerging market debt into U.S. equities and hard assets — a process the Iran conflict has accelerated, not created. Armstrong has argued that the Federal Reserve is largely irrelevant to the current equity bull market: it is being driven by capital flight from political instability, not domestic monetary policy. His Socrates system identified May 2026 as a key monthly turning point, with the bullish resolution now aligned with the Iran deal scenario. Armstrong sees any pullback as a buying opportunity within the larger bull structure that he projects runs into late 2027.

Health & Wellness

Longevity

New Data: VO2 Max Predicts Lifespan More Accurately Than Any Blood Marker

A large meta-analysis published this week confirmed that cardiorespiratory fitness — measured by VO2 max — is the single strongest predictor of all-cause mortality, outperforming cholesterol, blood pressure, and even fasting glucose. Researchers found each 1-unit increase in VO2 max associated with a 9% reduction in mortality risk. The practical takeaway: zone 2 cardio three to four times per week is more important to longevity than almost any supplement stack.

Nutrition

Ultra-Processed Foods Linked to Accelerated Brain Aging in 40s

Harvard researchers released findings showing adults in their 40s who consumed high quantities of ultra-processed foods — defined as industrially formulated products with more than five ingredients — showed measurably greater white matter decline on MRI than matched controls. The effect was dose-dependent and persisted after controlling for overall caloric intake, suggesting it is the food type, not just the calories, driving the association.

If it doesn't, oil stays elevated and the Fed's job gets harder. The next two weeks may be the most important of the summer.

Random Fact

The Chicago River Flows Backwards

In 1900, Chicago engineers reversed the flow of the Chicago River — one of the greatest engineering feats of its era — to send the city's sewage away from Lake Michigan instead of into it. The project took 18 months and involved redirecting 1.5 billion gallons of water per day.

Word of the Day

Contango

A futures market condition where prices for future delivery are higher than current spot prices — implying the market expects current conditions to ease over time. Today's oil market is in moderate contango, reflecting traders' bet that the Iran deal eventually closes and supply normalizes.
