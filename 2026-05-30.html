🗞 Front Page

Markets / Geopolitics

May Closes at All-Time Highs — Iran Deal On the Brink as Trump Eyes Final Decision

Wall Street wrapped one of its best months in years. The Nasdaq surged 8.1% in May, the S&P 500 gained 5%, and the Dow added 3% — all at record highs Friday after a massive tech-led rally capped by Dell's best single-day performance ever, up 33% on a blowout earnings report. The catalyst: optimism surrounding a potential U.S.-Iran ceasefire extension. The two sides have agreed in principle on a 60-day MOU covering a Hormuz reopening and a nuclear talks framework, but Trump has not yet signed off. He retreated to the Situation Room Friday to make his "final determination," posting his conditions on Truth Social: no nukes, Strait open immediately. Markets have priced in the deal. Now they wait for a signature.

Market Streak

S&P Posts 9th Straight Weekly Gain — Longest Since 2023

Friday's close extended the S&P 500's winning streak to nine consecutive weeks, the longest since late 2023. The streak survived a shooting war in the Gulf, PCE inflation at 3.8%, and a hawkish new Fed Chair — all of which would have derailed markets in a prior era. Sentiment is optimistic but fragile; a deal collapse in the Strait of Hormuz could reverse May's gains quickly.

🏦 Macro & Federal Reserve

Federal Reserve

Warsh Confirmed — Cuts Off the Table, December Hike Back in Play

Kevin Warsh was sworn in as Fed Chair this month. His first FOMC meeting is June 16–17. With PCE at 3.8% and energy prices elevated by the Iran war, the futures market prices near-zero chance of a June cut. BofA, Wells Fargo, and JPMorgan have all pushed rate-cut forecasts into 2027. A December hike is at 3.5% probability — small but nonzero, and worth watching.

Consumer Data

PCE 3.8%, Savings Rate Lowest Since 2022

April PCE hit 3.8% year-over-year — its highest since 2023 — driven largely by energy pass-through from the Iran-war oil shock. Core PCE (ex-food and energy) stands at 3.3%. Real disposable income fell 1.4% YOY. Real spending rose just 0.1%. The savings rate hit its lowest since 2022, suggesting consumers are drawing down cushions to maintain spending in the face of higher prices.

🏠 Real Estate

Mortgage Rates

30-Year at 6.53% (Freddie Mac); Bankrate at 6.56%

The 30-year fixed mortgage averaged 6.53% in Freddie Mac's weekly survey as of May 28, up from 6.51% the prior week. Bankrate's daily rate shows 6.56% today. The 15-year fixed is at 5.87%. Analysts broadly expect rates to remain in the 6%–6.4% range through year-end barring a rapid Iran resolution.

Housing Market

Spring Season "Weird" — Listings Outpace Buyers for First Time

The 2026 spring buying season is being described by agents as "weird" — for the first time in this rate cycle, listings have outpaced buyer demand in April. Existing-home sales rose just 0.2% in April. Unsold inventory is at 4.4 months of supply. Pending sales have risen three straight months, signaling latent demand that has yet to convert to closings.

💊 Health & Wellness

Sleep Research

The Goldilocks Window: 6.4–7.8 Hours

Research continues to refine the optimal sleep range. The current scientific consensus puts the sweet spot at 6.4–7.8 hours per night. Too little (under 6) raises cognitive and metabolic risk. Too much (over 9) correlates with nearly as many adverse health outcomes. Consistency of sleep timing is emerging as equally important as duration.

2026 Wellness Trends

AI-Personalized Health Plans Go Mainstream

What was boutique in 2025 is mainstream in 2026: AI-driven wellness plans that adapt dynamically to your biomarkers, sleep data, and activity levels. Employer wellness platforms and preventative health memberships are the new delivery mechanism. Diagnostics that were once elite longevity testing — expanded blood panels, inflammation markers — are now routine offerings at annual physicals.
