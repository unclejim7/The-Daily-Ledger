Health, Wellness & Fitness

Nutrition

Grilling Safety: Charred Meat and Cancer Risk — What the Evidence Says

Memorial Day grilling reminder: HCAs (heterocyclic amines) and PAHs (polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons) form when meat is charred at high temperatures and have been linked to elevated colorectal cancer risk in multiple studies. The risk is real but manageable: marinate meat before grilling (reduces HCAs by up to 99%), flip frequently, cut off heavily charred portions, and don't press meat directly into open flames.

Fitness

Two Strength Training Sessions Per Week Reduces All-Cause Mortality 23%

A landmark JAMA study of 400,000+ adults found that just two 45-minute strength training sessions per week — regardless of cardio — was associated with a 23% reduction in all-cause mortality. The effect was additive with cardio but significant even in the absence of any aerobic exercise. You don't need to run. You need to lift.

Sleep

Consistent Bedtime More Important Than Total Hours, New Study Finds

A Stanford chronobiology study found that sleep timing consistency — going to bed and waking at the same time every day — was a stronger predictor of cognitive performance and metabolic health than total sleep duration. Variable sleepers who averaged 8 hours performed worse than consistent sleepers averaging 7. The takeaway: schedule matters more than quantity.

Fact · Word · Quote

Random Fact

Memorial Day Was Originally Called Decoration Day

Memorial Day originated as "Decoration Day" after the Civil War, when communities decorated soldiers' graves with flowers. The first large organized observance was May 30, 1868 — today's date — at Arlington National Cemetery. The holiday wasn't officially renamed "Memorial Day" until 1967 and became a federal holiday in 1971.

Word of the Day

Armistice (n.)

A formal agreement to cease fighting — typically temporary, pending a permanent peace treaty. The Iran MOU is technically a ceasefire, not an armistice, and certainly not a peace treaty. The distinction matters: armistices can unravel. The most famous armistice in history — WWI's November 11, 1918 — held. Most don't last this cleanly.
