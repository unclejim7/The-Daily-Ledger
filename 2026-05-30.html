💪 Health & Wellness
Holiday Health

Hydration Alert: Heat + Alcohol = Dehydration Risk — Know the Signs

With Memorial Day temperatures in the low 80s and outdoor celebrations in full swing, hydration is critical. Alcohol is a diuretic — it increases urine output and accelerates dehydration. The rule of thumb: one glass of water per alcoholic drink. Signs of heat-related dehydration include dark urine, headache, rapid heartbeat, and confusion. Severe cases require immediate medical attention.

Sunscreen Science

SPF 30 Blocks 97% of UVB — Reapply Every 2 Hours for Full Protection

SPF 30 blocks approximately 97% of UVB radiation; SPF 50 blocks 98%. The marginal difference is small — what matters far more is reapplication. Studies show most people apply only 25–50% of the recommended amount, effectively cutting their SPF in half. For a full beach day: apply 30 minutes before sun exposure and reapply every two hours or after swimming.

The Dow Jones First Crossed 10,000 in March 1999 — It Took 25 More Years to Double Again

The Dow crossed 10,000 for the first time on March 29, 1999 — a moment celebrated with party hats on the NYSE trading floor. It took until February 2024 to close above 38,000 for the first time. Word of the Day

Breadth

In market analysis: a measure of how many stocks or sectors are participating in a market move. High breadth (many stocks rising) signals a healthy, sustainable rally. Low breadth (a few mega-cap stocks carrying the index) is a warning sign. May 2026's all-sector positive performance is a textbook high-breadth signal.
