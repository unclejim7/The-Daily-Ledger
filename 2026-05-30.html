Trump Sets Red Lines for Iran Deal — Situation Room Session Ends Without Decision

A two-hour White House meeting Friday produced no signed agreement. Trump demands Hormuz open immediately, nukes surrendered, frozen assets off the table. Iran Negotiations

The MOU Exists — But So Do the Red Lines

Negotiators reached a tentative memorandum of understanding Thursday. But Trump made clear Friday from the Situation Room that it doesn't meet his standards: Iran must never develop a nuclear weapon, the Strait of Hormuz must open immediately, and frozen Iranian assets will not be on the table. VP Vance acknowledged the ceasefire has had "little flare-ups." Markets reacted calmly — oil fell — but the risk of a collapse remains live heading into the weekend.

Trade Policy

Costco Pledges to Pass Tariff Refunds to Members

With the SCOTUS IEEPA tariff ruling still rippling through the economy, Costco CEO Roland Vachris committed Thursday that the company will return tariff refund value to its members — though the exact mechanism and amount remain unspecified. It's a meaningful signal from the country's third-largest retailer that the refund pipeline is real, and that at least some of the $200B+ in tariffs paid by importers will flow back to consumers rather than be captured as margin.

Federal Reserve & Macro
Warsh — Week One

A New Chair Inherits a Divided Committee

Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting is June 16–17. Markets give 97% odds to no rate move. But the real issue is the dot plot and press conference language — Warsh's three-pronged agenda (redefine inflation, loosen guidance, shrink balance sheet) could rattle markets if communicated abruptly. PCE at 3.8% with a savings rate at its lowest since 2022 makes any pivot toward easing extremely difficult to justify politically or economically.

Hike Risk

December Hike Odds Near 70% — A Complete Reversal

CME FedWatch data shows nearly 70% probability of a rate hike by December — a stunning turnaround from the cut expectations that prevailed at the start of the year. Jamie Dimon warned of a potential "credit recession" if long rates remain elevated. The 10-year Treasury's stubborn hold above 4.4% is already weighing on housing and leveraged corporate borrowing.

Housing — May Snapshot

Spring Season "Weird" — Listings Outpacing Buyers for the First Time This Year

Freddie Mac's weekly rate: 6.53%. Bankrate: 6.56%. Fannie Mae projects the 30-year staying near 6.3% through Q2. The market shift that matters: April 2026 was the first month this year where home listings outpaced sales — a meaningful change from the supply-starved market of 2024–25. NAHB expects the 30-year to dip below 6% by December if Iran resolves and the tariff situation stabilizes. Texas and Florida are now buyer's markets; Chicago-area inventory is slowly building.

Health & Wellness
Research

Microplastics in the Brain — The Science So Far

A Nature Health study published in April found micro- and nanoplastics in virtually every human brain sample analyzed — healthy tissue included — with higher concentrations in tumor-affected tissue. A companion Nature Medicine paper found even greater accumulation in dementia patients. Experts urge caution against panic; human health consequences remain under study. Practical steps to reduce exposure: avoid plastic utensils with hot foods, use glass or steel water bottles, and choose wooden cutting boards over plastic.
