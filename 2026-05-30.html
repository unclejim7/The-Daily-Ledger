Health & Wellness
Longevity

VO2 Max Is the Single Best Predictor of Longevity — New Study Confirms

A 20-year longitudinal study of 122,000 patients confirms cardiorespiratory fitness as measured by VO2 max is the strongest single predictor of all-cause mortality — stronger than smoking status, diabetes, or hypertension. Going from "low" to "below average" VO2 max reduced mortality risk by 50%. Elite aerobic fitness cut risk by 80%.

Nutrition

Olive Oil Consumption Linked to 28% Lower Dementia Mortality

A Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health study following 92,000 Americans over 28 years found that consuming more than half a tablespoon of olive oil daily was associated with 28% lower risk of dying from dementia. The association held regardless of overall diet quality — suggesting olive oil's polyphenols have independent neuroprotective effects.

Random Fact

The Chicago River Once Flowed Into Lake Michigan — Then Engineers Reversed It

In 1900, Chicago engineers accomplished one of the greatest civil engineering feats in history by permanently reversing the flow of the Chicago River away from Lake Michigan to prevent sewage contamination of the city's drinking water. The project took two years and used more earth-moving than the Panama Canal.

Word of the Day

Bascule

A type of movable bridge that pivots on a counterweight to swing upward, like a seesaw — the mechanism behind Chicago's famous drawbridges. The word derives from French for "seesaw." Chicago has more bascule bridges than any city in the world.
