Trump Sets Red Lines for Iran Deal — Situation Room Ends Without a Decision

A two-hour White House session Friday produced no signed agreement. Trump demands Hormuz open immediately, nukes surrendered, no frozen asset relief. Iran's state outlet pushed back. Defense Secretary Hegseth warns the military is ready to resume combat operations.

Iran Negotiations

The MOU Exists — But So Do the Red Lines

Negotiators reached a tentative memorandum of understanding Thursday. But Trump made clear Friday from the Situation Room that it doesn't meet his standards: Iran must never develop a nuclear weapon, the Strait of Hormuz must open immediately, and frozen Iranian assets will not be on the table. VP Vance acknowledged the ceasefire has had "little flare-ups." Markets reacted calmly — oil fell — but the risk of a full collapse remains live heading into the weekend.

Trade

Costco Pledges to Pass Tariff Refunds to Members

Costco CEO Roland Vachris committed Thursday that the company will return IEEPA tariff refund value to its members — one of the first major retailers to commit to passing SCOTUS-related refunds downstream. The exact mechanism and amount remain unspecified, but it's a significant signal that at least some of the $200B+ in tariffs paid by importers will ultimately flow to consumers rather than be absorbed as corporate margin.

Health & Wellness
Research

Microplastics in the Brain — What the Science Says

A Nature Health study published in April found micro- and nanoplastics in virtually every human brain sample analyzed — including healthy tissue — with higher concentrations in tumor-affected areas. A Nature Medicine paper found even greater accumulation in dementia patients, with deposits in cerebrovascular walls. Experts caution that quantities are lower than some early alarming coverage suggested and that health consequences remain under active study. Simple exposure-reduction steps: avoid plastic utensils with hot food, use glass or steel water bottles, choose wooden cutting boards.
