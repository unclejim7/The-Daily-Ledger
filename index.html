OBBBA · 2026 Changes

Your SALT Deduction Just Quadrupled — What Every Chicagolander Needs to Know

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act is reshaping the federal tax landscape for 2026, and for Illinois homeowners and high-earners, the most consequential change is the dramatic expansion of the SALT deduction.

For tax year 2026, the SALT cap rises from the old $10,000 TCJA limit to $40,400 — a 1% inflation bump from the $40,000 enacted for 2025. The cap applies through 2029, after which it reverts to $10,000 unless Congress acts again. For Chicagoland residents paying high property taxes and Illinois state income taxes, this is meaningful real money.

The catch: the expanded cap phases out for households with modified adjusted gross income above $505,000 in 2026, declining at a rate of 30 cents per dollar above the threshold, bottoming out at the old $10,000 floor once MAGI exceeds roughly $605,000.

2026 SALT Cap: $40,400
Phase-out starts: $505K MAGI
Floor: $10,000
Expires: 2029

Other 2026 OBBBA changes homeowners should flag: PMI (private mortgage insurance) premiums are now deductible, the $750K mortgage interest cap is permanent, and energy-efficiency home credits (solar, EV chargers) expired at year-end 2025.

Chicago Housing Enters 'Strategic Rebalancing' — Buyers Finally Catching a Breath

After years of frenzy and historically low inventory, Chicagoland's housing market has shifted into what analysts are calling a "healthy rebalancing" phase — one that still favors sellers in desirable neighborhoods but increasingly rewards prepared buyers.

The median home sale price across the Chicagoland area sits around $340,000–$365,000 heading into mid-year 2026, representing annual appreciation of roughly 4.4% — a far cry from the double-digit spikes of the early 2020s. Approximately 13,700+ active residential listings were on the market entering the season, giving buyers more options than they've had in several years.

Mortgage rates remain above 6% — the single biggest affordability constraint in the market. Nationally, Realtor.com expects modest price growth of 1–2%, but Chicago is outperforming that baseline modestly. The bidding wars haven't disappeared entirely, especially in Lincoln Park, Lakeview, and the North Shore, but frenzied same-day offers on anything and everything are gone.

"Chicago real estate remains a long-term wealth play, especially in neighborhoods people consistently choose. Smart decisions outperform lucky ones in a calmer market."

Longevity

2026's Dominant Wellness Thesis: Healthspan, Not Just Lifespan

The Global Wellness Summit's annual Future of Wellness report identifies a decisive shift in how Americans are approaching their health in 2026: away from chasing lifespan metrics alone and toward healthspan — the years lived with energy, cognitive clarity, and physical capability. The focus is on mitochondrial support, inflammation reduction, stress resilience, and hormone balance through daily habits rather than episodic interventions.

Research continues to confirm that metabolic flexibility, muscle mass, and inflammation levels are the most powerful predictors of healthy aging — and the tools supporting these outcomes are becoming more accessible. VO2 Max has emerged as a key biomarker: research shows it's a more powerful predictor of disease than obesity, smoking, hypertension, or diabetes combined.
