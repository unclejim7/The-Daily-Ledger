U.S. Launches Fresh Strikes on Iran After Apache Helicopter Downed Over Strait of Hormuz

Markets brace for escalation as new military exchanges raise fears of a protracted conflict and sustained pressure on global oil supply routes.

The United States military launched new strikes against Iran overnight, hours after an American Apache helicopter was shot down over the Strait of Hormuz — President Trump declaring the U.S. had no choice but to respond. Iran's Revolutionary Guard confirmed the drone strike on the aircraft and said it retaliated with hits on American positions in Jordan, Bahrain, and Kuwait.

The exchange marks a fresh escalation in a war that began February 28, 2026 when the U.S. and Israel initiated coordinated strikes targeting Iran's nuclear and ballistic missile infrastructure. A conditional ceasefire reached in April has now effectively collapsed, with both sides trading blows in the Strait of Hormuz — the chokepoint through which roughly 20% of global oil passes.

Brent crude rose 1.66% to $92.97 per barrel in early trading, with the entire oil futures curve moving upward, markets apparently beginning to price in a scenario where Hormuz disruption lasts well beyond near-term expectations. Equity futures staged a mild recovery — S&P contracts up roughly 0.3%, Nasdaq +0.5% — even as oil climbed, suggesting some investors see the CPI release today as the more actionable near-term catalyst.

The May Consumer Price Index report, due at 8:30 a.m. ET, arrives in a fraught context: April CPI came in at 3.8% year-over-year, a three-year high. A hotter-than-expected number today could cement the case for the Federal Reserve to hold — or even hint at hikes — at Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting next week.

CLARITY Act Watch
Senate Floor Vote Targeted for July — White House Wants July 4 Signing

The Digital Asset Market CLARITY Act cleared the Senate Banking Committee 15–9 on May 14. The White House is targeting a July 4 deadline for presidential signature. Polymarket puts 2026 passage at 59%. The bill would split crypto oversight between the SEC and CFTC and let new projects raise up to $75M without full SEC registration.

Banking industry opposition to stablecoin provisions remains a headwind.

A parallel IRS release restructured the public-private Security Summit partnership to improve fraud protection and information sharing across the tax system.

Health, Wellness & Fitness
New Research
New Diabetes Pill Burns Fat Without GLP-1 Side Effects

Scientists announced June 3 an experimental obesity and diabetes pill that works differently from Ozempic-class drugs. Rather than suppressing hunger, it activates metabolism in skeletal muscle, lowering blood sugar and increasing fat burn — potentially avoiding the muscle-mass loss concerns associated with GLP-1 agonists.

Supplementation
Collagen Review of 8,000 Participants Confirms Skin and Joint Benefits

A major review published in early June analyzing nearly 8,000 participants found collagen supplementation consistently improves skin health and eases osteoarthritis symptoms — particularly when taken over several months. Researchers also found modest muscle-related benefits, adding credibility to a supplement long popular but poorly studied.
