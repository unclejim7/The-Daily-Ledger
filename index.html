⚔ Current Events

Breaking — US–Iran War

Negotiators Believe They Have a Deal — Now It's Trump's Call

American and Iranian negotiators have arrived at what they believe is a draft memorandum of understanding for a 60-day ceasefire extension, but the agreement still requires President Trump's sign-off before it becomes official. According to reporting from Axios confirmed by Al Jazeera and ABC News, the framework would reopen the Strait of Hormuz over a 60-day period — with Iran loosening its grip on the waterway and the U.S. pulling back its naval blockade in synchronized steps, returning the strait to its pre-war status quo. Iran would also pledge not to build a nuclear weapon, while the U.S. would discuss sanctions relief and the unfreezing of Iranian assets.

The draft deal follows months of conflict that began on February 28 when joint U.S.–Israeli strikes launched Operation Epic Fury. Earlier this Thursday, Iran's IRGC claimed an attack on a U.S. air base in retaliation for American strikes near Bandar Abbas Airport. Markets have been moving on every Iran headline all week — with both oil prices and equities gyrating on successive war and peace reports.

"The Strait of Hormuz will fully reopen over the course of 60 days — Iran loosening its grip, the U.S. pulling back its naval blockade in synchronized steps."

— Draft MoU framework, as reported by Axios & confirmed by U.S. officials
Background

How We Got Here: 90 Days of US–Iran Conflict

Operation Epic Fury launched Feb. 28, 2026, with coordinated U.S.–Israeli airstrikes targeting Iranian nuclear facilities, military leadership (including the assassination of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei), and infrastructure. Iran retaliated with missile and drone strikes on U.S. bases and allies. A two-week ceasefire was announced, but initial talks in Pakistan in April failed to yield a peace deal. Trump then extended the ceasefire open-endedly while maintaining a U.S. naval blockade. The conflict has driven Brent crude above $90, pushed PCE inflation to a three-year high of 3.8%, and roiled global markets across the spring.

Market Watch

CAPE Above 40: Bull Market in "High-Risk" Territory

The S&P 500 is up 18.5% year-to-date and the Nasdaq has surged 28%, fueled by AI optimism. But the Shiller CAPE ratio has now exceeded 40 — only the second occurrence in 100 years, the first being the 1999 dot-com bubble peak. Strategist Bob Doll has called this a "high-risk bull market." Historically, a CAPE above 30 forecasts lower average annual returns over the following decade, and above 40 the data is limited — but it counsels caution. Markets appear to be pricing in near-perfection, even as inflation surges and the Fed holds rates steady.

🏛 Macro & Federal Reserve

Inflation Alert

PCE Hits 3.8% — Highest Since May 2023

The Bureau of Economic Analysis reported Thursday that the headline PCE price index rose 0.4% in April month-over-month and accelerated to 3.8% year-over-year — matching expectations and the highest reading since May 2023. The Hormuz energy shock continued to reverberate through the consumer basket. Core PCE (ex food & energy) edged up to 3.3% annually, also a multi-year high. In a separate release, Q1 GDP was revised down to 1.6% annualized growth from the initial 2.0% estimate — slowing growth alongside accelerating inflation is a stagflationary combination the new Fed leadership will have to navigate carefully.

Fed Transition

Powell Out. Kevin Warsh In. And the Clock Is Already Ticking.

Jerome Powell's two-term tenure as Federal Reserve chairman has ended. At his final press conference, he left Wall Street with a pointed summary of the challenge ahead: "The economic outlook remains highly uncertain, and the conflict in the Middle East has added to this uncertainty." He ruled out rate cuts for the foreseeable future, noting that tariffs and elevated energy prices could produce persistent inflation. Trump has nominated Kevin Warsh to replace him — a former Fed governor known for advocating for "central bank reform." Q1 GDP revised down to 1.6%, PCE at 3.8%, the 30-year Treasury yield near 5.18%: Warsh inherits a stagflationary tightrope.

"The economic outlook remains highly uncertain, and the conflict in the Middle East has added to this uncertainty."

— Jerome Powell, final press conference as Fed Chairman

🏠 Real Estate

Chicagoland Market

Supply-Constrained But Strategically Navigable

Chicago's housing market in 2026 remains defined by limited inventory and resilient buyer demand — but the extreme competition of 2021–23 has eased. Chicagoland had roughly 4,996 active listings in late April, historically constrained but improving. The median home price in Chicago proper has risen to around $409,200 — up 7.7% year-over-year — driven by a 28.8% inventory decline. For buyers, this is no longer a panic market, but it is not a buyer's market either. Neighborhood performance varies sharply: North Side lakefront communities command premiums and steady appreciation; South and West Side neighborhoods remain more volatile. Suburban Evanston and Oak Park continue to attract families priced out of the city.

Mortgage Rates

30-Year Fixed: ~6.47–6.60% — War-Linked Pressure Persists

The 30-year fixed mortgage rate sits at approximately 6.47–6.60% today, according to Fortune, U.S. News, and Bankrate — little changed from the prior day but elevated compared to February's ~6.0% level. The Iran war's upward pressure on oil prices has directly fed into inflation expectations, which drives mortgage rates higher. The Freddie Mac weekly average stands at 6.53%. The 15-year fixed is around 5.73–5.85%. Zillow has trimmed its 2026 home-sales growth forecast to 1.2% (from 4%) citing higher energy costs. For buyers: locking now vs. waiting for a rate drop requires weighing geopolitical resolution risk — a signed Iran deal could quickly bring rates lower.

📋 Tax & Accounting

Planning Note

Stagflationary Environment Demands Fresh Tax Strategy Review

With PCE at 3.8% and GDP growth slowing to 1.6%, the macro backdrop is shifting in ways that have real tax implications. Rising energy costs are flowing through to transportation and manufacturing, affecting business deductions and cost-basis calculations across supply chains. The personal savings rate has fallen to 2.6% of disposable income — near historical lows — signaling consumer financial stress that may affect estimated tax payment planning. With the Fed funds rate at 3.50%–3.75% and no rate cuts in sight, the after-tax yield on short-duration fixed income and money market funds remains meaningful. If an Iran peace deal materializes, watch for a rapid shift in energy-sector asset valuations. Conservative scenario planning for both outcomes is prudent.

💊 Health & Wellness

🫘

Beans & Soy: A 30% Cut in High Blood Pressure Risk

A major new analysis published this week finds that eating more beans, lentils, chickpeas, tofu, and other soy foods could reduce the risk of high blood pressure by nearly 30%. Researchers point to legumes' combination of magnesium, potassium, fiber, and plant protein as the likely mechanism. For Chicagoans managing diet amid a stressful economic environment, the finding is straightforward and actionable: add more legumes to your weekly rotation.

🧠

USC Scientists Identify Hidden Alzheimer's Trigger — And a Potential Shutoff

USC researchers announced this week they have identified new drug compounds that may reduce the brain inflammation associated with Alzheimer's disease — particularly in women, who are disproportionately affected. The compounds target a newly identified inflammatory pathway distinct from amyloid plaques, potentially opening a complementary treatment front. The work is in early-stage development but represents one of the more promising mechanistic findings in recent memory.

🧬

Ozempic / Wegovy: Why the Weight Loss Eventually Plateaus

Scientists have now identified why semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy) causes weight loss to slow and eventually stall after an initial dramatic drop. The body's metabolic adaptation — reduced resting energy expenditure — appears to counteract the drug's appetite-suppression effects at higher weight-loss levels. A separate analysis confirmed semaglutide works remarkably well in adults over 65, improving heart and metabolic health alongside weight reduction. The plateau research may inform next-generation dosing protocols.

⚡

Neurowellness: 2026's Top Wellness Trend Is Nervous System Optimization

The Global Wellness Summit named "precision nervous system optimization" the top wellness trend of 2026 — the ability to objectively measure and retrain stress and resilience patterns in real time. Wearables that track heart rate variability, breath-pattern biofeedback, and nervous-system-calibrating apps are central to the movement. The shift reflects a broader cultural turn away from quick fixes toward sustainable, science-based approaches to mental-physical integration — particularly relevant given elevated economic and geopolitical stress levels.
