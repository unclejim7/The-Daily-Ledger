🔴 Breaking — Iran War
Iran Fires Ballistic Missile at Kuwait; CENTCOM Calls It "Egregious Ceasefire Violation"
The fragile ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran is under severe strain this morning. U.S. Central Command announced that Iran launched a ballistic missile toward Kuwait overnight — successfully intercepted by Kuwaiti air defenses — and that Iranian forces also dispatched five attack drones near the Strait of Hormuz. CENTCOM intercepted all five drones and prevented a sixth from launching at a ground site in Bandar Abbas. Kuwait's Foreign Ministry declared it reserves the right to take "any and all necessary measures" to defend its territory, while Saudi Arabia condemned what it called a hostile attack on the Gulf nation.

The conflict, which began February 28 when the U.S. and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury targeting Iran's military and nuclear infrastructure — including the assassination of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei — has resisted repeated ceasefire attempts. Initial talks in Pakistan in April failed to produce a peace deal. Israel has continued striking Hezbollah targets in Lebanon, hitting more than 135 sites in the past 24 hours alone, including areas near Sidon and the Beqaa Valley.
Sources: ABC News / CNN / CENTCOM · Updated 8:26 AM CT

Today's Markets
Futures Fall on Fresh Iran Strike; Inflation Data in Focus at 8:30 AM
Stock futures slid Thursday morning after Iran's latest ceasefire violation rattled commodity and equity markets. Dow futures fell about 119 points, while S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures were roughly flat. The big data event of the day: the PCE Price Index (the Fed's preferred inflation gauge) released simultaneously with the Q1 GDP second estimate at 8:30 AM ET. April PCE came in at a 3.8% annual rate — still well above the Fed's 2% target and consistent with a June hold on interest rates. The Fed kept its benchmark rate at 3.50–3.75% at the April 28–29 meeting; FOMC minutes revealed a majority of members flagging that elevated inflation could require holding rates longer than anticipated.

SpaceX Files for Largest IPO in History — $1.75T Target, Ticker SPCX
SpaceX's S-1, filed May 20, targets a staggering $1.75 trillion valuation on Nasdaq under ticker SPCX. Macro & Federal Reserve
Inflation
PCE Hits 3.8% Annual Rate — Fed's Hands Remain Tied
April's Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) price index showed a seasonally adjusted monthly increase of 0.4%, with the 12-month rate running at 3.8% — the highest since early 2025 and nearly double the Fed's 2% target. Core PCE, which excludes food and energy, stood at 3.0% as of the April FOMC meeting. The March projections had the Fed's dot plot expecting PCE to settle at 2.7% by year-end, a target now looking optimistic given the current oil price environment and Middle East conflict premium.

Four FOMC members dissented at the April meeting: Stephen Miran wanted to cut rates; Hammack, Kashkari, and Logan supported holding but opposed an easing bias in the statement — reflecting the divide between those watching inflation and those watching growth. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh (who replaced Jerome Powell after his May 15 term expiry) will hold his first press conference at the June 17 FOMC meeting.

Real Estate
Chicagoland Market
Chicago Housing: Balanced Market, Rates Above 6% — Strategic Window for Prepared Buyers
The Chicagoland housing market is entering mid-year 2026 with a more measured tone than the frenzied pandemic years. The median home sale price sits around $340,000–$365,000, with annual appreciation running roughly 4.4% — sustainable, but slower than the double-digit spikes of the early 2020s. Inventory has improved modestly, with approximately 13,700 active listings — buyers are finally getting more time and leverage.

Mortgage rates remain the chief friction point. The 30-year fixed rate is at 6.47% today (down about 4 bps from yesterday), while 15-year rates sit at 5.85%. Rates spiked to as high as 6.62% last week before pulling back. The geopolitical Iran premium — which drove oil and Treasury yields higher since late February — continues to add 20–40 bps above where rates might otherwise trade. CD rates of up to 4.30% remain attractive for patient savers.

Jeff Bezos Calls for Zero Income Tax on Bottom 50% of Earners
Amazon founder Jeff Bezos has publicly endorsed eliminating federal income taxes for Americans earning under roughly $75,000 — a position he claims would save nurses and similar workers $12,000 or more annually. Cubs
THE STREAK IS OVER — Cubs Explode for 10 Runs, End 10-Game Nightmare
CHC 10·PIT 4
After ten consecutive losses — the darkest stretch of a season that featured two separate 10-game winning streaks — the Cubs finally exhaled Wednesday night in Pittsburgh. Ian Happ was the hero twice over: a two-run single in the first inning opened the scoring, and then Happ delivered the dagger — a go-ahead three-run blast in the seventh off Yohan Ramirez to break a tie game. Pinch-hitter Michael Conforto added a two-run jack moments later to blow it open. Nico Hoerner added an RBI single in the 2nd. Even Jameson Taillon, who gave up a game-tying three-run homer in the 3rd, held on for the win as Chicago's bats carried the load. The Cubs are now 29-26, sitting fifth in the NL Central. Today's series finale in Pittsburgh features Paul Skenes on the mound for the Pirates — a stern test of the resurrection.

Brain Health
USC Discovers Hidden Alzheimer's Trigger — and a Possible Way to Shut It Down
USC researchers have identified new drug compounds that may reduce the brain inflammation linked to Alzheimer's disease, particularly in certain genetic subgroups. The compounds target a specific inflammatory pathway that has been difficult to address with existing medications. The research, published this week, is preliminary but represents one of the most targeted Alzheimer's interventions identified to date.
Nutrition Science
Beans, Lentils & Soy Could Slash Blood Pressure Risk by 30%
A major analysis published this week found that eating more legumes — including beans, lentils, chickpeas, tofu, and edamame — could reduce the risk of high blood pressure by nearly 30%. Researchers attribute the effect to a combination of high fiber, magnesium, potassium, and plant-based protein. The finding adds to growing evidence that plant-forward eating patterns provide cardiovascular benefits comparable to some medications.
Anti-Aging Research
Texas A&M Nasal Spray Appears to Reverse Brain Aging in Study
Researchers at Texas A&M have developed a nasal spray that appears to reverse markers of brain aging by calming neuroinflammation and restoring cellular energy systems. The study, conducted in animal models, showed measurable restoration of cognitive function. Human trials are in early planning stages. Separately, scientists are reconsidering "zombie cells" — previously thought uniformly harmful, some senescent cells appear to play protective roles, fueling more targeted anti-aging therapies.
