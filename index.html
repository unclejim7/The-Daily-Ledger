⚡ Breaking — U.S.–Iran War Negotiations

Trump Sends Back Toughened Iran Text — Decision Slips Into Another Week as Iranian President Reportedly Resigns

After Friday's inconclusive Situation Room session, Trump redlined the proposed agreement and returned it to Tehran demanding harder nuclear language and firmer Hormuz terms. Iranian President Pezeshkian has reportedly submitted a letter of resignation. The U.S. military disabled a cargo vessel in the Gulf of Oman on Saturday. Hegseth says forces are ready to "finish the job" if talks collapse.

Iran — Latest

The Deal That Won't Close

CNN reported Sunday that Trump sent back a revised text with toughened language on Iran's nuclear commitments and the Strait of Hormuz timeline — extending the back-and-forth into a third week of negotiations. Iranian state media Fars had already pushed back on Trump's Friday demands, calling them contradictory to the existing agreement's provisions.

The most dramatic development of the weekend: Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian reportedly submitted a resignation letter to the Office of the Supreme Leader, suggesting serious internal political turbulence in Tehran. France's Macron urged both sides to move quickly. World Bank, IMF, and IEA leaders issued a joint warning Friday about fuel security risks if Hormuz shipping doesn't normalize before summer demand peaks.

Lebanon

Israel Deepens Lebanon Offensive

Israel expanded military operations in southern Lebanon over the weekend, with the IDF warning the public to brace for "repeated firing" as its campaign against Hezbollah positions in Nabatieh advanced. The Israeli army issued fresh Home Front Command guidelines urging northern residents to remain vigilant. The Lebanon front adds a second theater of instability to an already fragile regional picture and complicates any Iran deal framework that includes Hezbollah-related provisions.

Markets Watchpoint

Oil Fell Friday — But a Breakdown Changes Everything

WTI crude slid Friday as markets priced in a deal. That trade is now at risk. The World Bank, IMF, and IEA jointly warned Friday that if shipping through Hormuz — a chokepoint for roughly 20% of global oil supply — doesn't normalize before summer demand picks up, markets face "increasing risks for fuel security." A deal collapse could snap oil back above $100/barrel and pressure yields higher, threatening the equity rally's foundations.

The AI Chip Debate

"Borderline Mania" — SOX Up 70% in Two Months

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index has surged roughly 70% since the March 30 market lows — its best stretch since the dot-com era. Nvidia topped a $5.5 trillion market cap. Micron's 2026 earnings are projected at $66.8 billion, up from $8.5 billion a year ago. Dell's 33% single-day surge on AI server demand was the exclamation point on a month that cemented chips as the dominant market story of 2026.

But skeptics are louder now. Interactive Brokers' chief strategist called the move "borderline mania, if not actual full-fledged mania." Michael Burry has loaded up on put options against SOXX, QQQ, Palantir, Nvidia, and Oracle expiring January 2027. Federal Reserve & Macroeconomics
Warsh Era — Week One Complete

The New Chair Inherits a Powder Keg

Kevin Warsh was sworn in May 22 as the 17th Fed chair — confirmed 54–45, the closest vote in modern history — inheriting rates at 3.50–3.75%, PCE at 3.8% YOY, and an FOMC with four dissents at its last meeting. His first rate decision is June 16–17. Markets assign 97% odds to no change. The real test: whether Warsh's opening dot plot and press conference signal hawkish continuity, or hint at the flexibility that Trump wants.

Rate Outlook

December Hike Odds Near 70%

CME FedWatch data shows nearly 70% probability of a rate hike by December — a complete inversion from the cut expectations that dominated the start of 2026. Warsh has three stated goals: redefine how inflation is measured, loosen forward guidance, and accelerate balance sheet reduction. Achieving all three while keeping FOMC consensus intact — and managing political pressure from the White House — may define his entire tenure.

Housing Market

Health & Wellness
Research Alert

Microplastics Found in Nearly Every Human Brain Studied

A Nature Health study published in April detected micro- and nanoplastics in virtually all human brain tissue analyzed — including healthy samples — with higher concentrations in tumor-affected tissue. A companion Nature Medicine paper found even greater accumulation in brains of dementia patients, with notable deposits in cerebrovascular walls. Experts caution that actual quantities are lower than some sensational early headlines suggested — but the finding is real and warrants ongoing study.

Practical exposure-reduction steps: avoid plastic utensils with hot foods, switch to glass or stainless water bottles, use wooden cutting boards, and reduce plastic food packaging where possible.

2026 Wellness

Neurowellness Is the Year's Defining Trend

The Global Wellness Summit crowned "neurowellness" — using technology to regulate the nervous system — as its top trend for 2026. Digital life keeps nervous systems in near-constant low-grade fight-or-flight. Demand is surging for tools that objectively measure stress and resilience patterns: HRV-tracking wearables, breathwork apps, somatic therapy, and precision nervous system coaching.

Also worth tracking: the longevity market is pivoting toward women's healthspan for the first time, focusing on ovarian aging and its downstream effects — a correction after decades of protocols built almost entirely on male physiology.

Editor's Dispatch

A May to Remember — and a June That Won't Let You Rest

May 2026 will go down as one of the most remarkable months in modern market history: all-time highs across all three major indexes, a 9-week winning streak for the S&P, a semiconductor rally that has serious people invoking 1999, and a geopolitical crisis that somehow became a tailwind for stocks. That last part is the most unsettling. When markets price in a deal that hasn't closed — and the negotiator keeps redlining the text — the correction risk isn't theoretical.

The week ahead brings jobs data Friday, the first Warsh-era Fed speaker circuit, and presumably some kind of Iran resolution one way or another. Iranian President Pezeshkian's apparent resignation letter, if confirmed, reshapes the negotiating dynamic in ways that are hard to predict. Keep an eye on oil and the 10-year. Meanwhile, the Cubs are in a dog fight down in St. Louis tonight, and the White Sox just completed a series sweep that's making the South Side believe again. Not a bad way to close out May.
