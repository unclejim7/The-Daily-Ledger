AI Rout Resumes; S&P 500 Falls to Four-Week Low as Tech Skepticism Returns

Tuesday, June 9, 2026  ·  Markets Desk

After a brief Monday respite, U.S. equity markets reversed course Tuesday as investors resumed rotating out of heavyweight technology names. The S&P 500 retreated to 7,330 — its lowest level since May — while the Nasdaq 100 dropped roughly 2% and the Dow shed half a percent. The selloff reflects mounting skepticism that the AI infrastructure rally of the past several quarters may have run ahead of fundamentals, even as hyperscalers continue to signal robust capital-expenditure plans.

Nvidia, Oracle, and AMD each fell between 1% and 3%, continuing the week-long repricing of AI-adjacent names that began after Broadcom's earnings last Thursday — when the company declined to raise its AI revenue outlook despite beating estimates. The MAGS ETF, which tracks an equal-weight basket of the Magnificent Seven, also traded lower for the session.

"US equity indices swung lower on Tuesday as the pivot out of the heavyweight tech sector was resumed following yesterday's respite." — Trading Economics

In a notable subplot, Marvell Technology — which rocketed 32.5% on a single day last week after Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang declared it the "next trillion-dollar company" at Computex 2026 — has given back much of those gains during the ensuing selloff. The company recently cleared its first GAAP profit hurdle and is now expected to join the S&P 500 index, triggering passive fund buying. Whether that technical bid can stabilize the stock amid broader semiconductor weakness remains the question traders are watching.

Federal Reserve

Warsh Era Begins in Earnest: June 16–17 FOMC Is Chair's First Test

Federal Reserve Desk

With one week to go until the June 16–17 FOMC meeting — Chair Kevin Warsh's first as the 17th head of the Federal Reserve — markets have almost entirely priced in a hold. The federal funds rate has sat unchanged at 3.50%–3.75% across three consecutive meetings (January, March, and April), and J.P. Morgan's strategists say rates will stay there through year-end with no 2026 cuts expected.

Warsh was sworn in on May 22 as Jerome Powell agreed to remain on the Board to support continuity. The June meeting is less about the rate decision itself — universally expected to be a hold — and more about tone: what language Warsh adopts in the policy statement, how the dot plot shifts, and whether the press conference suggests a lean toward eventual easing or a hawkish stance prepared to hike if inflation doesn't cooperate.

"There will likely be an explicit move away from a bias toward easing to a neutral stance on rates." — J.P. Morgan Wealth Management Chief Investment Strategist

St. Louis Fed President Alberto Musalem has argued that the current rate is near neutral and that lowering it with inflation still above the 2% target would be inadvisable. That sentiment likely reflects the committee's center of gravity as Warsh assumes command. The dot plot and the new Summary of Economic Projections will be the real deliverable from next week's meeting.

Bitcoin Stabilizes Near $63K as Saylor Buys the Dip and ETF Flows Return

Crypto Desk

Bitcoin opened Tuesday at $63,079 before slipping slightly to $62,640 at 9:15 a.m. ET — a modest 0.3% dip from Monday's open but a meaningful stabilization after last week's harrowing breach of $60,000, the first time the coin traded at that level since October 2024. Ethereum opened at $1,690 and traded modestly lower through mid-morning.

The most significant development in the Bitcoin market this week is Strategy's (formerly MicroStrategy) decision to purchase 1,550 BTC just one week after a brief, attention-grabbing $2.5 million sale that briefly rattled sentiment. The company raised $181 million through stock sales to fund the fresh purchase and now holds approximately 845,256 BTC — a signal that Michael Saylor's institutional conviction remains fully intact despite the market's prolonged correction.

Technical analysts tracking Elliott Wave structure are flagging this move as a potential wave-two bounce within a larger bearish structure — warning that the real test is whether Bitcoin can hold above $60,000 and eventually reclaim $68,000. Two resistance zones define the near-term outlook: $64,500–$66,269 as the first ceiling, and a broader band above that. For bulls, holding $60K as support and clearing $68K would constitute a confirmation signal. For bears, any failure of $60K reopens the path to lower levels.

Bitcoin has lost roughly 27% of its value in 2026 and trades approximately 51% below its October 2025 all-time high of $128,198.

On the regulatory front, the CLARITY Act continues to make progress through Congress, offering a longer-term positive backdrop for institutional crypto participation even as short-term price action remains choppy. Analysts are also noting that Bitcoin may be reasserting its role as a portfolio diversifier in an AI-dominated market — a thesis that drove some safe-haven flows during the early Iran war months and may now be attracting fresh attention as equity correlations shift.

Geopolitics

Iran–Israel Hostilities Briefly Flare, Then Subside; Ceasefire Negotiations Resume

World Desk

The fragile ceasefire framework between Iran and Israel — which had largely held since early April — was tested severely over the weekend after the IDF struck southern Beirut on June 7, days after the latest ceasefire in Lebanon. Iran responded with waves of ballistic missiles toward Israel, its first direct attack in the two months since the April 7 ceasefire agreement. Israel retaliated. The exchange marked the most serious direct confrontation since the truce took hold.

By Monday, however, tensions had again pulled back from the brink. Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu announced a halt to attacks on Iran — stopping short of formally acknowledging a new ceasefire — while Tehran suspended its own operations, conditioning any permanent halt on Israel ending strikes in southern Lebanon. Iran's Civil Aviation Organization reported that the country's airspace had returned to normal conditions as of Tuesday morning, with flight operations resuming.

"I don't think it is a bad decision to go for the ceasefire. I think it is a moronic one." — Tehran-based geopolitical analyst Ehsan Safarnejad, on Iran's position

The weekend's flare-up rattled oil markets and briefly pressured equities, but Tuesday's easing hostilities contributed to a partial stabilization in crude prices. The Strait of Hormuz — one of the world's most critical oil chokepoints — had seen severe disruption during the initial conflict phase; any renewed closure risk remains the linchpin of global energy market volatility. Trump administration officials continue to push for a comprehensive deal, with the president saying Iran has "no choice" but to come to terms, though no formal agreement has materialized.

Real Estate & Mortgage Rates

30-Year Mortgage Stuck at 6.48% — Fed Can Do Little to Budge It

Real Estate Desk

The 30-year fixed mortgage rate held at 6.48% in Freddie Mac's most recent Primary Mortgage Market Survey (June 4), down fractionally from 6.53% the prior week but still sharply higher than the 6.00% trough reached in February 2026. A year ago, the 30-year averaged 6.85%. The 15-year fixed-rate mortgage averaged 5.79%, down from 5.87% the prior week.

PBS News framed the situation directly: mortgage rates are staying high, and the Federal Reserve can do little about it. That's because the 30-year mortgage tracks the 10-year Treasury yield more closely than the fed funds rate — and with the 10-year at 4.549% and the 30-year Treasury above 5%, there is simply no mechanical pathway for the Fed to ease mortgage costs without first convincing bond markets that the long-run inflation path is improving.

Mortgage rates in the mid-6% range are weighing on home sales heading into summer, even as income growth has modestly outpaced home price appreciation — offering a thin edge of improving affordability.

Pending home sales had increased for three consecutive months heading into the spring selling season, suggesting latent demand remains intact. But with homebuyers effectively locked out by both elevated rates and still-elevated prices, transaction volume has not recovered meaningfully. Experts broadly expect the mid-6% rate environment to persist through year-end absent a significant economic deterioration that forces the Fed's hand.

Health & Wellness

Biggest Collagen Study Yet: What Actually Works

A major scientific review of nearly 8,000 participants — the most comprehensive collagen supplement analysis to date — found that collagen supplements can genuinely improve skin health and ease osteoarthritis symptoms, especially when taken consistently over longer periods. Researchers also found modest benefits for muscle health. The findings suggest collagen supplementation is not merely marketing noise, though dosing consistency and duration appear to matter significantly for outcomes. (ScienceDaily, June 5, 2026)

New Diabetes Pill Burns Fat Without Ozempic Side Effects

Scientists have developed an experimental diabetes and obesity pill that operates through an entirely different mechanism than GLP-1 drugs like Ozempic or Wegovy. Early testing shows it burns fat without the gastrointestinal side effects that cause many patients to discontinue semaglutide-class medications. The compound is still in early-stage research but represents a potentially meaningful alternative for the substantial share of obese patients who cannot tolerate GLP-1 therapy. (ScienceDaily, June 3, 2026)

Semaglutide Works Remarkably Well in Adults Over 65, Major Analysis Finds

A major analysis confirms that semaglutide — the active ingredient in Ozempic and Wegovy — performs remarkably well in adults over 65, helping many in that age group lose substantial weight while also improving heart and metabolic health markers. The results challenge earlier assumptions that GLP-1 efficacy might diminish with age and could support expanded prescribing in an older patient population where cardiovascular benefit is especially valued. (ScienceDaily, May 11, 2026)

Creatine's Hidden Power: Beyond Muscle Gains

A new research summary highlights creatine's underappreciated role beyond the gym. Naturally produced in the body, creatine rapidly regenerates ATP — the cellular fuel that powers muscles, the brain, and other high-energy tissues. Emerging data suggest creatine may support cognitive function, neuroprotection, and recovery from concussive events, making it one of the most comprehensively supported supplements for both athletic and general health applications. (ScienceDaily, May 4, 2026)

Fact of the Day
The Strait of Hormuz

At its narrowest point, the Strait of Hormuz measures just 21 miles wide — yet roughly 20% of the world's oil supply transits through it daily. The strait separates Iran from Oman and the United Arab Emirates, making it the single most strategically sensitive waterway in global energy markets.
