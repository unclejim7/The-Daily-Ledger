🗞 Front Page

Middle East / U.S. Foreign Policy

Ceasefire on Life Support: Iran Halts Talks as Renewed Strikes Shake the Deal

As June begins, the fragile U.S.-Iran ceasefire framework is under maximum strain. Both Washington and Tehran carried out strikes over the weekend — each calling them "defensive" — even as negotiators continue haggling over the 60-day MOU extension. Iran announced Monday it is pausing talks, citing "contradictory U.S. positions" and Israel's continuing military campaign in Lebanon. Trump responded on Truth Social, insisting Iran "really wants to make a deal" and that critics should "relax, it will all work out well." Oil surged 7% on Monday morning to ~$93/barrel (Brent crude), reviving inflation fears and reinforcing hawkish Fed expectations. The MOU framework reportedly includes a Hormuz reopening, a 60-day ceasefire extension, and a framework for nuclear talks — but Trump has not yet put pen to paper.
Current Events Desk  ·  June 1, 2026
Oil Shock

WTI Surges to $90, Brent Tops $93 as Iran Tensions Flare

West Texas Intermediate crude futures jumped 3% Monday morning to ~$90/barrel after reports of U.S. strikes on Iranian radar and drone sites over the weekend. Brent crude climbed 6%, near $96. The moves reverse a near-17% monthly decline for U.S. oil in May, when ceasefire optimism had pushed prices sharply lower.

Wall Street Slides as June Opens; May's Historic Rally Takes a Breath

Stocks wobbled to start June, with the S&P 500 off 0.74% to 6,845 and the Dow dropping 210 points. The Nasdaq shed 0.76%. The pullback follows a record May: Nasdaq +8%, S&P +5%, Dow +3%. All three closed at all-time highs on Friday. Oil's Monday surge revived inflation concerns and stoked bets on a possible year-end Fed rate hike.

🏦 Macro & Federal Reserve

Federal Reserve

Warsh Takes the Helm; Rate Cuts Pushed to 2027

With Kevin Warsh now confirmed as Fed Chair (his first FOMC meeting is June 16–17), Wall Street banks are pushing their cut forecasts deep into 2027. BofA now expects two quarter-point reductions in July and September 2027, citing sticky inflation above the 2% target. J.P. Morgan likewise sees the Fed on hold through end of 2026, with a possible hike of 25 bps in Q3 2027. Oil's surge Monday pushed the probability of a December hike to ~40% per CME FedWatch.

Inflation / Economy

PCE at 3.8%; Savings Rate Near Multi-Year Low

April PCE — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge — came in at 3.8% year-over-year, a 3-year high driven largely by energy pass-through from the Iran war. Core PCE sits at 3.3%. Real disposable income fell 1.4% YOY. The savings rate is at its lowest since 2022. Jerome Powell, who is staying on the Board, said the Iran energy shock is likely transitory — but acknowledged it is "too soon to know."

₿ Bitcoin

Cryptocurrency

BTC Slides Toward $70K as ETF Outflows Accelerate

Bitcoin opened the month at $72,145 — down ~$1,676 from Sunday morning and roughly $33,500 below where it stood a year ago. The tone is decisively bearish in the near term. U.S. spot Bitcoin ETF total net assets have fallen from $104B on May 15 to $94B by Friday, a steep outflow streak. Ether ETFs are running an even longer 14-session outflow streak. Technical analysts note BTC is trading below its 20-, 50-, and 100-day EMAs, with RSI near 35 (approaching oversold). Key support: $70,000. $2 billion in long BTC positions were liquidated last Friday alone amid Iran tensions. A CoinDesk report linked the May 30 spike in IBIT selling — a $1.26B block — to rapid exit by a large institutional investor.

🏠 Real Estate

Mortgage Rates

30-Year Fixed at 6.56% on June 1

Bankrate's daily survey pegs the 30-year fixed mortgage at 6.56% today. Freddie Mac's weekly survey (as of May 28) showed 6.53%, up from 6.51% the prior week. The 15-year fixed is 5.87%. Zillow shows 6.49% today. Analysts now broadly expect rates to hover in the 6.0%–6.4% range through year-end, with any sub-6% scenario contingent on a quick resolution to the Iran conflict.

Housing Market

Spring Season "Weird" — Inventory Rises but Buyers Hesitate

The spring housing market continues to defy simple characterization. Existing-home sales rose 0.2% in April to a 4.02 million annual pace — a whisker, not a roar. Unsold inventory climbed to 1.47 million units, or 4.4 months of supply. Pending home sales have risen three consecutive months, signaling latent demand. But closings have not meaningfully rebounded. New construction is adding choice. The consensus: buyers are waiting for rates to ease before jumping in.

📋 Tax & Accounting

Personal Finance

June 1 Reminder: Mid-Year Tax Moves That Can Still Save You Money

June 1 is a natural checkpoint for tax planning. Key items worth reviewing: (1) Estimated tax payment Q1 was due April 15; Q2 is due June 16 — don't miss it or face underpayment penalties. (2) If you've had income changes in 2026, now is the time to adjust withholding via W-4 amendment — the Iran-driven energy shock may have affected self-employed income or investment gains. (3) Roth conversion opportunity: with equity markets down on the day, lower portfolio values can be advantageous for conversions. (4) HSA contributions for 2026: the annual limit is $4,300 (self-only) or $8,550 (family). Max them out before year-end.

💊 Health & Wellness

Microplastics

2026: The Year Microplastics Move from Awareness to Action

The Global Wellness Summit's 2026 report identifies microplastics as one of the year's defining health shifts — moving from concern to concrete consumer and public-health response. Research increasingly links microplastic accumulation in human tissue to chronic disease, reproductive disruption, and inflammation. The summit notes this is one of few wellness threats they view as genuinely serious, in contrast to many wellness "detox" fads. Practical actions gaining traction: filtering tap water, shifting from plastic food storage, reducing single-use food packaging.

Women's Health

Longevity Medicine Pivots Toward Women's Biology

A landmark shift in the longevity medicine market: after decades dominated by male-centric data and protocols, 2026 is seeing a major reorientation toward women's healthspan. Research mounting that ovarian aging is "command central" for women's systemic health — its decline accelerates aging across multiple organ systems. Clinics, wearables, and telehealth platforms are now designing protocols tailored specifically to women at every life stage, not extrapolated from male data. The Global Wellness Summit calls it one of the most consequential trend shifts in a decade.

Wellness Trends 2026

Personalization Becomes Mainstream; "Ready Is the New Well"

The dominant wellness theme of 2026: AI-driven personalization is moving out of boutique clinics and into employer platforms, preventative memberships, and mainstream healthcare. Blood panels, inflammation markers, and metabolic indicators — once elite longevity testing — are becoming routine. A second emerging theme: crisis readiness as wellness. The Global Wellness Summit argues that having a disaster preparedness plan is now as essential as a fitness plan — a response to a world of compounding climate, geopolitical, and psychological stressors.

Editor's Dispatch — June 1, 2026

June opens not with summer ease but with a reminder of how intertwined geopolitics and economics have become. Oil's 7% Monday surge undercuts the cheerful May close; the ceasefire deal that markets priced in is proving stubbornly difficult to finalize. The Fed — under new leadership — watches and waits. Cuts are off the table; a hike is back on the table, if only barely.

On the South Side, the White Sox gave Murakami-era fans something to actually celebrate: a three-game sweep of Detroit. The Cubs return home after a frustrating road trip, but they play the Athletics next — a softer draw. And the Bulls, with Sweeney gone to Orlando, now have a cleaner picture of their path forward with Chris Quinn.

Welcome to June. Bring an umbrella. — The Editor
