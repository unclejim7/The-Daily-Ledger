⚡ Breaking — U.S.–Iran War Negotiations

Trump Toughens Iran Deal Text and Sends It Back — Iranian President Reportedly Submits Resignation

After Friday's two-hour Situation Room session ended without a decision, Trump returned a revised agreement with harder language on Iran's nuclear commitments and Hormuz reopening. CNN reports the text went back Sunday. Iranian President Pezeshkian has reportedly tendered his resignation to the Supreme Leader. The U.S. military disabled a Gambian-flagged cargo ship in the Gulf of Oman Saturday. Secretary Hegseth says forces stand ready to "finish the job" if talks collapse.

Iran — Sunday Update

The Deal That Keeps Getting Farther Away

Negotiators reached a tentative MOU Thursday. Trump spent Friday itemizing his red lines on social media — no nuclear program, Hormuz open immediately, frozen assets off the table. Iran's state outlet Fars pushed back, calling the demands "contradictory to the agreement's text." Then came Sunday's news: Trump had the text revised and sent back, extending the back-and-forth into another week.

The biggest wild card: Iranian President Pezeshkian reportedly submitted a resignation letter to Khamenei's office, signaling serious internal political turbulence in Tehran that could reshape who the U.S. is actually negotiating with. France's Macron urged both sides to close a deal quickly.

Lebanon

Israel Expands Operations Despite Ceasefire

Israel deepened its push into southern Lebanon over the weekend, with the IDF warning residents of northern Israel to brace for expanded fire as operations in Nabatieh advanced. The army issued fresh Home Front Command guidelines. Smoke was reported rising from Kfar Tebnit on Saturday. The Lebanon front adds a second theater to an already fragile regional picture — and complicates any Iran framework that touches Hezbollah provisions.

Market Watchpoint

Oil Fell on Deal Hopes — A Breakdown Would Sting

Federal Reserve & Macroeconomics
Warsh Era — Week One Complete

The New Chair Inherits a Powder Keg

Kevin Warsh was sworn in May 22 as the 17th Fed chair — confirmed 54–45, the closest vote in modern history — inheriting a central bank with rates at 3.50–3.75%, PCE inflation running 3.8% YOY, and an FOMC that produced four dissents at its last meeting (the most divided since 1992). His first rate decision comes June 16–17. Markets price 97% odds of no change. The real test: his opening dot-plot revisions and the language of his maiden press conference.

Rate Outlook

December Hike Odds Near 70% — A Stunning Reversal

CME FedWatch now shows nearly 70% probability of a rate hike by December — a complete inversion of the cut expectations that dominated early 2026. Warsh's three-pronged agenda — redefine how inflation is measured, loosen forward guidance, shrink the balance sheet faster — could reshape bond markets significantly if communicated abruptly. Jamie Dimon warned this week of a potential "credit recession" if long yields stay elevated.

Health & Wellness
Research Alert

Microplastics Found in Nearly Every Human Brain Studied

A Nature Health study published in April detected micro- and nanoplastics in virtually all human brain tissue analyzed — including healthy samples — with higher concentrations in tumor-affected tissue. Nanoplastics were more prevalent than microplastics. A companion Nature Medicine paper found even greater accumulation in brains of patients with documented dementia, with notable deposits in cerebrovascular walls and immune cells.

Experts emphasize that actual quantities in healthy brains are lower than some alarming headlines suggested, and that human health consequences remain under active study. Practical exposure-reduction steps: avoid plastic utensils with hot food, switch to glass or stainless water bottles, use wooden cutting boards.

2026 Wellness Trends

Neurowellness: Regulating the Nervous System Is the Year's Top Priority

The Global Wellness Summit named "neurowellness" — using technology to manually regulate the nervous system — as 2026's defining trend. Digital life keeps nervous systems in near-constant low-grade fight-or-flight. Demand is surging for tools that objectively measure and retrain stress patterns in real time: HRV-tracking wearables, breathwork apps, somatic therapy, and precision nervous system coaching.

Also gaining traction: the longevity market is pivoting toward women's healthspan for the first time — focusing on ovarian aging and its downstream effects after decades of protocols built primarily on male physiology.

Editor's Dispatch

A May to Remember — and a June That Won't Let You Rest

May 2026 will go down as one of the most remarkable months in modern market history: all-time highs across all three major indexes, a nine-week winning streak for the S&P, a semiconductor rally that has serious people invoking 1999, and a geopolitical crisis that somehow became a tailwind for stocks. That last part is the most unsettling. When markets price in a deal that hasn't closed — and the negotiator keeps redlining the text and sending it back — the correction risk isn't theoretical.

The week ahead brings jobs data on Friday, the first Warsh-era Fed speaker circuit, and presumably some kind of Iran resolution one way or the other. The Iranian president's apparent resignation letter, if confirmed, reshapes the negotiating dynamic in ways that are hard to predict. Keep an eye on oil and the 10-year. Meanwhile, the Cubs are in a fight down in St. Louis tonight, and the White Sox just swept Detroit to complete a run that's making the South Side believe again.

Not a bad way to close out May.
