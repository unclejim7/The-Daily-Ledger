Current Events & Geopolitics

Iran War
U.S.–Iran Ceasefire Talks Begin — But IRGC Drones Still Flying Over Hormuz
Diplomatic back-channels opened Wednesday as the U.S. and Iran began preliminary ceasefire discussions in Oman. The talks come after weeks of escalating strikes — the IRGC hit a U.S. air base in Qatar last week, and CENTCOM responded with a precision strike on IRGC command infrastructure near Tehran. Oil remains elevated above $90/barrel on Strait of Hormuz closure fears. The State Department called the talks "preliminary but constructive." Iran's foreign minister said a deal was possible "within weeks." Markets rallied on the headline before giving back gains as drone activity was reported overnight in the Gulf.
Washington

Kevin Warsh Nominated as Fed Chair

President Trump formally nominated Kevin Warsh to replace Jerome Powell as Federal Reserve Chairman. Warsh, a former Fed governor and Goldman Sachs alumnus, is seen as more hawkish on inflation. His confirmation hearing is expected in late June. Markets responded positively — a signal that Wall Street trusts his credentials.

Economy

SpaceX IPO Roadshow Targets June — $1.75T Valuation

SpaceX has hired Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley for a Nasdaq IPO targeting a $1.75 trillion valuation. The roadshow is expected to begin June 4, with the debut around June 12. It would be the largest IPO in U.S. history, surpassing Saudi Aramco. Retail investors are already lining up.

Tech

Qualcomm Falls 9.7% on New Export Controls

Commerce Department expanded export control restrictions on advanced chip technology, directly hitting Qualcomm's China business. The stock fell 9.7% — its worst single-day drop in two years. The Nasdaq sold off broadly on the news as investors rotated out of semiconductor names with heavy China exposure.

Health, Wellness & Fitness

Pharmacology

Ozempic Plateau Mechanism Cracked

Researchers identified why GLP-1 drugs like Ozempic and Wegovy stop working after 12–18 months in many patients — the brain's reward circuitry adapts, reducing the appetite-suppression signal. The finding opens a pathway to combination therapies that could sustain weight loss indefinitely. Clinical trials of next-gen combinations are expected to begin in late 2026.

Nutrition

Beans Cut Blood Pressure Risk by 30%

A large longitudinal study published this week found that people who eat legumes (beans, lentils, chickpeas) at least four times a week have a 30% lower risk of developing hypertension compared to non-consumers. The effect held across age groups and was independent of other dietary factors. Fiber and potassium are the likely mechanisms.

Neuroscience

Alzheimer's Drug Target Identified in New Research

Scientists at the Salk Institute identified a protein — SORLA — whose malfunction appears to be an upstream trigger for tau protein aggregation in Alzheimer's disease. The finding provides a new drug target distinct from amyloid — the focus of most prior (largely failed) Alzheimer's drug trials. Early-stage drug development has begun.

Random Fact

The Dow's First Record Above 50,000

The Dow Jones Industrial Average crossed 50,000 for the first time in history Wednesday — a milestone that took 128 years from its 1896 founding at 40.74. The index has gained more than 1,200x from its starting point. At its 1929 peak of 381, no one could have imagined 50,000.

Word of the Day

Seigniorage (n.)

The profit a government makes by issuing currency — the difference between what money costs to produce and its face value. In an era of trillion-dollar deficits and a Fed chair nominee who's hawkish on inflation, seigniorage is quietly back in financial circles as a topic. The U.S. made roughly $50B from it last year.
