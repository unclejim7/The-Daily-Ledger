Dow Surges to Record as Chips Dump — Warsh Nomination Rattles Bond Market

The Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 282 points to a record close Tuesday as defensive stocks rallied, even as semiconductor stocks cratered on renewed export control fears. Qualcomm shed 9.7% after reports emerged of additional restrictions on China chip shipments. The S&P 500 held near its record, while the Nasdaq slipped 0.4%. President Trump's nomination of Kevin Warsh as Federal Reserve Chair sent bond yields higher as traders priced in a more hawkish policy path.

The 10-year Treasury yield climbed to 4.52%, its highest since March, as Warsh is widely seen as more inflation-vigilant than outgoing Chair Powell. Markets are now questioning whether the two rate cuts penciled in for 2026 survive contact with Warsh's first FOMC meeting in June.

Federal Reserve

Warsh Nominated — June FOMC Now the Most-Watched in Years

Kevin Warsh, a former Fed governor and Morgan Stanley investment banker, was nominated by President Trump to succeed Jerome Powell. Warsh is a hawk's hawk — he dissented against QE in 2010 and has been publicly critical of the Fed's inflation response. Markets immediately priced in fewer cuts. The June 16–17 FOMC meeting, now Warsh's first as Chair-designate, is shaping up as a pivotal policy moment.

Geopolitics & Markets

Iran Ceasefire Talks Open — Oil Retreats From $95 High

U.S. and Iranian negotiators opened preliminary ceasefire discussions in Oman on Tuesday, sending crude oil down 3.2% from its intraday high near $95. Markets are treating the talks as an early-stage positive but remain skeptical — similar talks collapsed twice earlier this spring. Gold held near $3,400 as a geopolitical hedge.

Real Estate & Mortgage
Mortgage Rates

30-Year Fixed at 6.85% — The Lock-In Effect Tightens Its Grip

Freddie Mac's weekly survey pegged the 30-year fixed rate at 6.85% this week, effectively unchanged from the prior week. Inventory remains historically suppressed as existing homeowners with sub-4% pandemic-era mortgages refuse to sell. New home sales are picking up the slack — builders are offering rate buy-downs and incentives not seen since 2009.

Health & Wellness
Research

GLP-1 Drugs Show Promise Beyond Weight Loss

New research published in the New England Journal of Medicine suggests GLP-1 receptor agonists like Ozempic and Wegovy may reduce cardiovascular event risk by up to 20% independent of weight loss. The findings are reshaping how cardiologists think about the drug class — it's no longer just a diet drug.

Fitness

Zone 2 Cardio Emerges as Longevity Cornerstone

A growing body of sports medicine research is cementing Zone 2 cardio — sustained effort at 60-70% max heart rate — as perhaps the single most important longevity-promoting exercise. Metabolic flexibility, mitochondrial density, and insulin sensitivity all respond dramatically to consistent Zone 2 work, according to Dr. Peter Attia and colleagues.

Word of the Day

Hawkish

In monetary policy, a "hawkish" stance favors higher interest rates to combat inflation, even at the cost of slower growth. With Kevin Warsh — a notable hawk — nominated as Fed Chair, expect this word to dominate financial coverage all summer.
