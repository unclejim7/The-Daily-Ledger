Geopolitics

Breaking — Middle East

Iran Halts Strikes on Israel After Trump's Ceasefire Demand — But Warns of Harsher Response

The fragile Middle East ceasefire collapsed overnight as Israel launched missile strikes on Iranian air defenses and petrochemical facilities, prompting Tehran to retaliate with volleys of its own. The exchange — the first direct fire between the two nations since April 8 — shook global markets and sent oil prices climbing. By Monday morning, Iran's military announced it was suspending further offensive operations, minutes after President Trump posted on social media demanding that both countries "immediately stop." Trump declared that "final" peace negotiations were proceeding and that both sides seek an "immediate ceasefire." Iran, however, warned it would resume hostilities — and with greater force — should Israel engage in further "hostile acts." The Israeli military confirmed strikes on Iranian petrochemical targets at Mahshahr, in southwestern Iran, after Israeli jets also hit Beirut's southern suburbs on Sunday, triggering Tehran's initial response.

War's 100th Day

Ceasefire Declared April 8 — Already Fraying Before Sunday

The conflict initiated February 28, 2026, by U.S. and Israeli strikes on Iran entered its 100th day with fragile quiet that shattered over the weekend. The US military previously shot down Iranian missiles and drones targeting Gulf allies near the Strait of Hormuz.

World Cup Complication

U.S. Denies Visas to 15 Members of Iran's World Cup Delegation

Washington has refused entry visas to 15 members of Iran's soccer delegation ahead of the 2026 World Cup, adding a diplomatic flashpoint to an already volatile bilateral standoff. Iran's national team participation remains uncertain.

Trump Message

"Both Sides Are Looking to Do an Immediate Ceasefire" — Trump

In back-to-back social media posts, Trump first urged restraint, then claimed progress: "Final negotiations on Peace are proceeding, subject to ignorance or stupidity getting in its way." Markets responded cautiously, with oil rising and equities mixed.

Federal Reserve & Macro

FOMC June 16–17

Warsh Era Fed: No Rate Change Expected; May CPI on June 10 Could Shift Everything

New Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh — confirmed in a 54–45 Senate vote and in office since May 15 — faces his first major policy test at the June 16–17 FOMC meeting. Markets assign greater than 95% probability to no change in the federal funds rate, currently 3.50–3.75%. The April CPI rose 3.8% year-over-year — the highest since 2023 — fueled by a 17.9% surge in energy costs tied to the Iran war. May's payrolls print of 172,000 jobs slammed the door on near-term cuts. The critical next data point: May CPI releases Tuesday, June 10. If inflation stays elevated, rate-hike whispers could grow louder heading into the June meeting.

Labor Market

172,000 May Jobs — Double Expectations

The May nonfarm payrolls report smashed forecasts of roughly 85,000 jobs. Unemployment held at 4.3%. March and April figures were also revised upward. "Good news is bad news" — stocks slid as yields soared.

Bond Market Signal

30-Year Yield Tops 5% — Highest Since 2023

The 30-year Treasury yield pierced 5% for the first time since 2023, reflecting persistent inflation concerns and fading rate-cut expectations. The 10-year settled at 4.547%. Schwab noted the 2018 parallels: the Fed stayed hawkish when markets wanted relief.

30-Year Mortgage Climbs to 6.38%; Buyers Face Rising Costs as Iran War Keeps Inflation Hot

Experts expect mortgage rates to remain in the mid-6% range through summer, putting a damper on the spring homebuying season.

Supplement Science

Largest-Ever Collagen Study: Supplements Show Real Benefits for Skin, Joints, and Muscle

A landmark review of nearly 8,000 participants published June 5, 2026 found that collagen supplements can measurably improve skin health and reduce osteoarthritis symptoms when taken consistently over several months. Researchers also identified modest benefits for muscle recovery. The findings push back against dismissals of collagen as a wellness fad — but experts caution that quality varies significantly by product and form.

Weight Loss Science

New Diabetes Pill Burns Fat Without Ozempic-Style Side Effects

Scientists announced development of an experimental diabetes and obesity pill that operates via a completely different mechanism than GLP-1 drugs like semaglutide. Early results showed meaningful fat reduction with a distinct side effect profile — potentially offering an alternative for patients who struggle with Ozempic's GI effects.

Vitamin Guidance

For Older Adults, Targeted Supplementation Beats Broad Stacks

A June 6 analysis found that for adults over 65, vitamins B12 and D are most effective when a true deficiency exists — taking them prophylactically shows minimal benefit. Protein, however, shows consistent benefit for muscle preservation regardless of baseline status.

GLP-1 Update

Ozempic Works Remarkably Well in Adults Over 65, Major Analysis Finds

A May 2026 study found semaglutide helps many adults over 65 lose substantial weight while simultaneously improving cardiovascular and metabolic health markers. The results were seen as particularly significant given prior assumptions that older patients respond less robustly.

Random Fact of the Day

The Strait of Hormuz — now a recurring flashpoint in the Iran war — is only about 21 miles wide at its narrowest point, yet roughly 20% of the world's petroleum and 17% of global liquefied natural gas passes through it daily. Controlling it is, quite literally, the world's most expensive chokepoint.

Word of the Day
Brinkmanship

(noun) The practice of pushing a dangerous situation to the verge of disaster in order to achieve the most advantageous outcome. First popularized during the Cold War by Secretary of State John Foster Dulles, it remains the operative geopolitical strategy in the current Iran conflict.
