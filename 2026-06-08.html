Markets & Finance

Chip Stocks Rebound; Nasdaq and Russell 2000 Book Small Comeback After Friday's Rout

Monday, June 8, 2026  ·  Markets Desk

U.S. equity markets opened Monday with a modest but meaningful rebound after Friday's brutal session — when the Nasdaq shed 4.18% following a hot May jobs report (172,000 nonfarm payrolls, unemployment steady at 4.3%) and Broadcom's refusal to raise its AI revenue guidance despite beating estimates. By premarket Monday, S&P 500 futures were up 0.4% as AI-linked names attempted a recovery.

Marvell Technology led the rebound with gains after its historic single-session surge of 32.5% last Tuesday — sparked by Jensen Huang calling it the "next trillion-dollar company" at Computex 2026 — had unwound sharply on the Friday selloff. Micron also recovered some ground. The semiconductor index, which lost 10% on Friday alone, was attempting a stabilization.

Easing hostilities in the Middle East contributed to improved investor confidence Monday morning, after Iran suspended its attacks on Israel following the weekend flare-up. Oil prices eased as the immediate risk of Strait of Hormuz closure diminished.

Federal Reserve

Warsh's First FOMC Meeting Approaches — Hold Expected, Tone the Real Story

Federal Reserve Desk

One week remains until the June 16–17 FOMC meeting — Chair Kevin Warsh's first since being sworn in on May 22. Markets have universally priced in a hold at 3.50%–3.75%. J.P. Morgan expects rates on hold through year-end, with an explicit shift away from an easing bias toward a neutral stance expected in the policy statement. Warsh's press conference tone and the updated dot plot will be the real deliverables, not the rate decision itself.

Bitcoin & Crypto

Bitcoin Finds Floor Near $63K After Dipping Below $60K for First Time Since 2024

Crypto Desk

Bitcoin opened Monday at $63,079, a $1,853 increase from its Friday open after the coin briefly dipped below $60,000 — the first time since October 2024. Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) reinforced institutional confidence by purchasing 1,550 BTC the prior week, even after a brief $2.5 million sale that had briefly rattled sentiment. The company now holds approximately 845,256 BTC. The Fear & Greed Index reads 10: extreme fear. But institutional buying and returning ETF inflows suggest a floor may be forming.

Geopolitics

Iran–Israel Flare-Up Over Weekend; Ceasefire Talks Resume Monday

World Desk

After the IDF struck southern Beirut on June 7 — violating the Lebanon ceasefire — Iran launched waves of ballistic missiles at Israel in retaliation. The exchange marked the first direct confrontation since the April 7 ceasefire. By Monday, Israel halted attacks and Iran suspended operations, with Tehran's airspace returning to normal. Trump administration officials pushed hard for de-escalation, and the president stated Iran has "no choice" but to come to terms, though a formal deal remains elusive. A U.S. Army helicopter went down near the Strait of Hormuz; both crew members were rescued by a drone boat.

Closing Edition

Word of the Day
Brinkmanship

The practice of pushing a dangerous situation to the edge of conflict in order to achieve the most advantageous outcome. Coined during the Cold War era by Secretary of State John Foster Dulles, it now describes the Iran–Israel diplomatic tightrope almost perfectly.

Fact of the Day
The Federal Reserve

The Federal Reserve was established in 1913 by the Federal Reserve Act. Since then, only 16 individuals have served as chair. Kevin Warsh, sworn in May 22, 2026, is the 17th — and only the second chair since 2006 to enter the role without previously holding the position of Fed Governor at the time of appointment.
