Diplomacy · Markets · Weekend Edition

Bürgenstock Talks Stumble Out of the Gate — Then Resume: The Iran MOU's First 48 Hours Are a Preview of What's Ahead

The Memorandum of Understanding signed by Donald Trump and Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian at Versailles on Wednesday night was supposed to produce its first implementation talks on Friday at Switzerland's Bürgenstock resort. Instead, it produced its first crisis. JD Vance withdrew from a planned trip to Switzerland, the Swiss foreign ministry confirmed the day's sessions would not take place, and journalists who had set up cameras on the lakeside mountaintop resort found themselves covering a postponement rather than an opening handshake. The MOU had been in force for less than 48 hours before its implementation track hit a wall.

Saturday brought a reset. The Swiss Federal Department of Foreign Affairs confirmed this morning that the Bürgenstock resort is back in operation as a "discreet and reliable venue" for ongoing discussions, though in keeping with its policy on sensitive talks, it declined to identify which participants are present or what is being discussed. Iran International, citing Axios reporter Barak Ravid, reported overnight that Steve Witkoff — Trump's Middle East envoy — had traveled to Switzerland, with Jared Kushner already in place. The presence of two senior Trump administration figures at the resort has refueled expectations that substantive talks could begin today, even if no formal announcement is forthcoming.

What happened between Wednesday's Versailles signing and Friday's abrupt delay remains murky. Iran International reported that IRGC Quds Force commander Esmail Ghaani issued a statement warning Israel on Friday and invoking Hezbollah and Gaza — a signal that hardliners in Tehran remain uncomfortable with the pace and scope of what Pezeshkian signed. The Times of Israel's live blog noted Israeli airstrikes in southern Lebanon on Saturday morning, a reminder that the regional conflict surrounding this deal has not paused. Pakistan's Interior Minister Mohsin Naqvi is traveling to Tehran today for talks with senior Iranian officials, suggesting the co-mediators are running a parallel track to keep both parties engaged.

For markets, the diplomatic wobble barely registered — U.S. equity markets are closed through Sunday, and Bitcoin, which had been repricing the Fed rather than the peace deal all week, ticked slightly higher Saturday morning to $63,609. The real test comes Monday at 9:30 a.m. when the S&P 500 reopens to trade on the accumulated weight of a hawkish FOMC, a shaky diplomatic week, and whatever the Bürgenstock talks have or haven't produced by then.

Federal Reserve

The Post-FOMC Picture

Markets Settling Into a New Regime: No Cuts, Possible Hike, and a Fed Chair Who Won't Show You His Map

Three days after Kevin Warsh's debut FOMC meeting, the market consensus is crystallizing around a set of uncomfortable facts. The federal funds rate stays at 3.50%–3.75% — that part was expected. What wasn't expected was the totality of the tone shift: the 130-word statement that stripped every trace of forward guidance, the median dot that moved from 3.4% to 3.8% for year-end, the nine officials who penciled in at least one hike, and Warsh's decision to abstain from submitting his own dot to avoid giving false precision. He is not playing by the old rules.

The mortgage market has already repriced: Bankrate's Saturday average jumped to 6.53% from Friday's 6.48%, while NerdWallet's APR surged 7 basis points to 6.40% on Friday evening alone. Zillow data put the 30-year purchase rate at 6.568% on Wednesday, just as the FOMC decision landed. The Freddie Mac weekly average of 6.47% still reflects the pre-FOMC environment; next Thursday's reading will show the full impact. If May PCE confirms what the CPI data suggested — that inflation is running well above 2% with no sign of returning — the July FOMC meeting becomes a live hike meeting.

Bürgenstock, Day Two

Talks Resume Saturday After Friday's Abrupt Delay: Witkoff and Kushner On-Site as MOU Faces Its First Real Test

What was supposed to be a procedural opening session on Friday became a diplomatic stumble that will define early perceptions of the MOU's durability. JD Vance's withdrawal from his planned trip was the visible trigger, but the underlying tensions are more structural: Iran's IRGC hardliners remain deeply ambivalent about what Pezeshkian signed, the Lebanese front is showing new Israeli activity that complicates Tehran's domestic political optics, and the Senate Republican bloc pushing for treaty ratification has given Iranian negotiators fresh reason to wonder whether the agreement will survive American internal politics.

Saturday's confirmation that talks have resumed — with Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner both in Switzerland — is the most important development of the weekend. Switzerland's foreign ministry has declined to name participants or discuss content, citing the "discreet and reliable" nature of the venue. Pakistan's Interior Minister Naqvi is traveling to Tehran today in parallel, suggesting the co-mediators are working both capitals simultaneously. The MOU's 60-day implementation window began at signing Wednesday — meaning the clock runs through approximately mid-August before the agreement expires or requires extension.

Health, Wellness & Fitness

25-Year Study: 7–8 Hours of Sleep Linked to Significantly Lower Dementia Risk

A new cohort analysis tracking more than 280,000 adults over 25 years found that consistently sleeping 7 to 8 hours per night was associated with a 30% lower risk of developing dementia compared to those who slept fewer than 6 hours or more than 9 hours. The effect held across all age groups but was strongest in participants over 60 who maintained consistent sleep patterns across the full study window.

Magnesium Glycinate vs. Melatonin: New Head-to-Head Trial Favors the Mineral for Long-Term Sleep Quality

A randomized controlled trial compared magnesium glycinate supplementation with melatonin in adults with chronic mild-to-moderate insomnia over a 12-week period. Magnesium glycinate users reported better sustained sleep quality improvements and fewer next-day grogginess complaints. Melatonin outperformed magnesium in the first two weeks but showed diminishing returns — consistent with prior research suggesting melatonin works best as a short-term circadian reset tool.

Random Fact

Switzerland's Bürgenstock resort, now hosting the U.S.-Iran implementation talks, sits on a narrow ridge 500 meters above Lake Lucerne and was built in the late 1800s as a Victorian mountain retreat. Audrey Hepburn reportedly honeymooned there in 1954. It hosted the Ukraine peace summit in June 2024 — and now the U.S.-Iran MOU talks in June 2026 — making it, in a span of two years, the venue for the two most consequential diplomatic gatherings of the decade.
