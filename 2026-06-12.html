SpaceX Makes History — And Iran May Sign Sunday

Two seismic events collide on the same Friday morning. Elon Musk's SpaceX begins trading on the Nasdaq today under ticker SPCX at a fixed $135 IPO price — raising $75 billion at a $1.78 trillion valuation, the largest public market debut in history. 🌍 Geopolitics
U.S.–Iran War

Deal Could Be Signed Sunday in Switzerland

Trump said Thursday that a peace accord could be finalized as soon as this weekend, coinciding with the G7 summit in Evian, France (June 15–17). Iran's semi-official Fars news agency reported Tehran is "likely" to accept a draft agreement, though no final text has been approved. A full signing is eyed for Switzerland. Trump extended the ceasefire open-endedly in late April, maintaining the naval blockade until negotiations conclude "one way or the other."

Context

The War That Shook Global Markets

U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran began February 28, 2026, killing Supreme Leader Khamenei and targeting nuclear and military infrastructure. Iran's counter-measures included closing the Strait of Hormuz, driving crude above $100/bbl and pushing U.S. CPI to a 3-year high of 4.2%. An April 8 ceasefire mediated by Pakistan has been repeatedly strained by drone and naval incidents.

💪 Health, Wellness & Fitness
New Research • Published Today in BJSM

Scientists Find the Strength Training Sweet Spot for a Longer Life

A landmark study published today in the British Journal of Sports Medicine — drawing on 30 years of data from 147,374 participants across three major Harvard cohorts — has identified the optimal weekly dose of resistance training for mortality reduction. The benefits were greatest when strength training was combined with aerobic exercise, confirming what exercise scientists have long suspected: concurrent training is the longevity gold standard. The study tracked brisk walking, running, swimming, cycling, and stair climbing as aerobic activities. Key takeaway: doing some resistance training matters far more than the specific routine — even simple weekly sessions yield significant mortality benefit.

Policy

Medicare Now Reimburses Doctors for Exercise Assessments

Starting in 2026, Medicare reimburses physicians for assessing patients' physical activity levels and nutrition habits during clinical visits — formally recognizing exercise as a measurable clinical factor, not just lifestyle advice. HSA and FSA rules now allow pre-tax spending on fitness apps, home gym equipment, and recovery products. The Health Fitness Association is calling on Congress to expand exercise-based prevention programs for older adults.
