Markets & Geopolitics

SpaceX Roars Onto Nasdaq in Largest IPO Ever as Iran Deal Optimism Lifts Wall Street

SpaceX made its long-awaited Nasdaq debut Friday under ticker SPCX, pricing at $135 a share and opening near $150 before surging more than 20% intraday — in what's being called the largest IPO in corporate history, raising an estimated $75 billion for data center and compute buildout. MSCI moved quickly to add the stock to its Large-Cap and Standard indexes.

The listing landed against a backdrop of growing optimism that the U.S. and Iran are nearing a peace deal. President Trump said Thursday the U.S. would cancel planned strikes, calling all points of a deal "essentially agreed," while Iranian state media suggested a U.S.-approved peace text makes a deal "highly" likely — though not yet done. The Dow gained 354 points (+0.70%) to close at 51,202, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq also higher and the VIX falling nearly 12%.
