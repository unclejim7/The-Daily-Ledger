U.S. Launches Self-Defense Strikes on Iran as Markets Reel; Nasdaq, S&P Plunge on Tech Selloff

Fresh U.S. military action in Iran after the Apache helicopter incident drives the Nasdaq down nearly 1% as tech shares bear the brunt and oil climbs.

The U.S. military announced self-defense strikes against Iran on June 9, hours after President Trump said the country had no choice but to respond to escalating Iranian aggression in the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's Revolutionary Guard confirmed it had struck American military positions across the region in retaliation.

Equity markets punished tech in particular, with the Nasdaq falling 0.97% and the S&P 500 dropping 0.26%. The Dow managed a modest 0.17% gain, buoyed by defensive and energy names. Oil climbed on renewed Hormuz fears, while Bitcoin fell 2.36% to around $61,969, reflecting broad risk-off positioning.

The 10-year Treasury yield settled at 4.533% — bond markets pricing in heightened geopolitical risk rather than Fed easing. Warsh's first FOMC meeting on June 16–17 looms as the next critical inflection point. Markets expect a hold but are watching closely for any language shift signaling the end of the easing bias.

Rate Hike Probability
December Hike Odds Climb to 40% on FedWatch

CME FedWatch probabilities for a December rate hike have surged to 40%, up from just 3% at the start of June. The blowout May jobs report (172K payrolls) and persistent inflation have forced a rapid repricing of the Fed's expected path under Warsh.

Federal Reserve
Warsh Watch
First FOMC Meeting June 16–17 — Hold Expected, Tone Critical

Kevin Warsh chairs his first FOMC meeting next week. J.P. Morgan strategists expect a hold with a likely shift away from the easing bias in policy statement language. Trump on Sunday said raising rates would be "wrong," adding political drama to the backdrop.

Divided Committee
April Meeting Saw Four Dissenters — Most Since 1992

The April 28–29 FOMC saw an 8–4 decision to hold. Three dissenters objected to the easing bias in the statement; one voted for a cut. Warsh inherits a deeply divided committee as he begins his tenure amid conflicting economic signals.

A June 2 study found oleic acid — the primary fat in olive oil — was associated with reduced pancreatic cancer growth, while other dietary fats accelerated it. Researchers emphasized the type of fat consumed may matter more than total fat intake in cancer risk.
