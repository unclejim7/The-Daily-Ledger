Month-End Recap: May 2026 Was One for the History Books — What June Holds

May 2026 closes with all three major indexes at record highs, the Dow above 51,000 for the first time, and the S&P up 4.1% for the month. The Nasdaq's 8.1% monthly gain was its best since November 2023. The rally was driven by four converging forces: better-than-expected earnings season, AI infrastructure demand validated by Dell and Nvidia, declining oil prices as Iran ceasefire talks progressed, and a delayed reaction to the improving trade picture.

What could slow June: a hawkish Warsh confirmation, stubborn PCE inflation at 3.8%, and the first FOMC meeting under new leadership on June 16–17. Markets are near record highs with no rate relief in sight. The setup for June is bullish on momentum but requires vigilance on the macro headwinds.

June Prep

Summer Heat Protocols: Acclimatize Gradually Over 10–14 Days

As temperatures rise into summer, exercise physiology research recommends a 10–14 day acclimatization period before attempting high-intensity outdoor workouts in heat. During acclimatization, the body increases plasma volume, improves sweat efficiency, and lowers core body temperature at rest — adaptations that dramatically reduce heat illness risk. Don't go from treadmill to outdoor intervals in 90°F on day one.

Word of the Day

Acclimatization

The biological process by which an organism adjusts to changes in its environment. Applies to both summer heat training (physiological) and market environments (psychological). Investors who have been acclimatized to record-high markets since 2024 may be underestimating tail risk — the same way first-time summer runners underestimate heat illness risk.
