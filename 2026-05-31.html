Markets & Finance

Month-End Markets
May Closes at All-Time Highs — S&P Best Monthly Gain Since 2023
May 2026 will be remembered as the month markets stopped worrying and learned to love the AI trade again. The S&P 500 closed at a record high, up 5% for the month — its best May performance since 2003. The Nasdaq surged 8.1%, powered by Nvidia, Dell's blockbuster Friday, and a parade of AI-related earnings beats. Breadth expanded late in the month, with small caps and value stocks joining the party. The Dow Jones crossed 51,000 for the first time. The confluence of Iran ceasefire optimism, a newly confirmed Fed chair perceived as inflation-focused, and unstoppable AI capex created an almost irresistible bull case heading into summer.
Federal Reserve

Warsh Confirmed — June 17 FOMC Looms Large

Kevin Warsh, confirmed by the Senate as Federal Reserve Chair, will preside over his first FOMC meeting June 16–17. Markets expect a hold at 3.50–3.75%, but all eyes will be on his communication style. Warsh is known for preferring rules-based frameworks and has been skeptical of extended accommodation. His first press conference will set market tone for the summer.

Geopolitics & Oil

The MOU talks have the oil market in a holding pattern. A signed deal could bring significant Iranian supply back to global markets within months, pressuring WTI toward $65. But Trump's additional demands — tougher inspections, missile constraints — give Tehran room to walk away. The binary outcome makes energy positions particularly volatile heading into June.

Macro & Federal Reserve

Inflation

PCE Holds at 3.8% — Progress Stalls

April PCE inflation registered 3.8% year-over-year, flat from March. The disinflation trend that characterized 2024 and early 2025 has paused. Services inflation — particularly rent and insurance — is keeping the overall rate elevated. The Fed needs to see sustained progress before resuming any easing cycle.

GDP & Growth

Q1 GDP Revised to +2.1% Annualized

The second estimate of Q1 GDP came in at 2.1% annualized growth, slightly below the initial 2.4% flash estimate but still solidly positive. Consumer spending moderated but business investment — particularly in data centers and AI infrastructure — remained the standout driver of growth.

Current Events

Diplomacy
Iran Talks on the Edge — Trump Demands Tougher Verification
The U.S.–Iran memorandum of understanding negotiations entered their most sensitive phase Sunday, with the Trump administration inserting stricter inspection protocols and explicit limits on Iran's ballistic missile development into a near-final draft. Iranian negotiators have signaled these additions could be a dealbreaker, while U.S. officials insisted the provisions are non-negotiable. The talks in Muscat have been ongoing for six weeks, surviving several earlier near-collapses. A signed deal would end a military standoff that began in late March and has roiled global energy markets, cost roughly 400 combined military and civilian lives, and drawn in several regional actors. Both sides face domestic political pressure to reach an agreement before summer.
Congress

OBBBA Reaches Senate — SALT Provisions Under Fire

The House-passed One Big Beautiful Bill Act arrived in the Senate facing opposition from both deficit hawks and moderate Republicans who want SALT relief phased in more gradually. Senate Majority Leader has promised a floor vote before the July 4 recess, but the final shape of the bill could look substantially different from the House version.

Domestic

Memorial Day Weekend: Record Travel, Mixed Economic Signal

The record Memorial Day travel numbers mask a bifurcated consumer: affluent households are spending freely on experiences and travel, while lower-income consumers show rising credit stress and reduced discretionary spending. Economists call it a "K-shaped" holiday — good at the top, strained at the bottom.

Real Estate

National

Pending Home Sales Fall 3.2% in April

The National Association of Realtors reported a 3.2% month-over-month decline in pending home sales for April, the third consecutive monthly drop. High mortgage rates and elevated prices continue to freeze the market. Many existing homeowners — locked into sub-3% pandemic-era mortgages — have no incentive to sell, keeping inventory persistently low.

Chicago

North Shore Condos Bucking Slowdown

While single-family transaction volume is down across Chicagoland, the North Shore condo market is seeing unusual activity as downsizing baby boomers and remote workers seek walkable, low-maintenance living. Units in Evanston and Wilmette are moving in under 20 days with multiple offers — a pocket of strength in an otherwise sluggish market.

Tax & Accounting

Mid-Year Tax Planning: What to Watch With the OBBBA in Senate Limbo

Tax advisors are telling clients to plan for multiple scenarios as the One Big Beautiful Bill Act works through the Senate. Key open questions: whether the SALT cap increase survives at $40K or gets trimmed, whether PMI deductibility restoration is retroactive to 2025, and whether the 20% passthrough deduction (Section 199A) extension clears. Until the bill is signed, mid-year estimated tax payments should be calculated conservatively using current law. Illinois filers in high-property-tax areas have the most to gain from the SALT expansion but face the highest uncertainty on timing.

Armstrong Economics

Armstrong: May Pivot Confirmed — Summer Could Accelerate

Martin Armstrong's Socrates system flagged May 2026 as a significant cyclical turning point for global capital flows. With the month closing at all-time highs, Armstrong would view this as confirmation of the bullish cyclical turn he has been projecting. His broader thesis holds that capital flight from European sovereign debt and emerging market instability is sustaining U.S. equity valuations independent of Federal Reserve policy. Armstrong sees the Iran situation as symptom of a broader political cycle he has long modeled — one where geopolitical conflict peaks near the middle of the decade and begins a gradual resolution by 2027. His model implies continued equity strength through the summer with the next significant cyclical test in September–October.

Health & Wellness

Sleep Science

Sleeping 7–8 Hours Reduces Dementia Risk by 30%, New Study Finds

A 20-year longitudinal study published in Nature Aging found that consistent 7–8 hour nightly sleep was associated with a 30% reduction in dementia risk compared to those sleeping fewer than 6 hours. The researchers identified sleep's role in clearing amyloid plaques — the proteins associated with Alzheimer's — as the primary mechanism. The study tracked over 8,000 participants across the UK beginning at age 50.

GLP-1 / Metabolic

Ozempic for Addiction? New Trial Data Is Promising

Researchers at the University of Pennsylvania presented Phase II trial results showing semaglutide — the active ingredient in Ozempic and Wegovy — significantly reduced alcohol cravings and consumption in subjects with alcohol use disorder. The findings extend a growing body of evidence that GLP-1 receptor agonists may work on the brain's reward circuitry broadly, not just appetite suppression.

Random Fact

Memorial Day's Chicago Roots

While the exact origins of Memorial Day are disputed, one of the strongest claims belongs to a Decoration Day ceremony held in 1868 at Chicago's Rosehill Cemetery. The holiday was established to honor Union soldiers killed in the Civil War and evolved into the national observance celebrated today.

Word of the Day

Liquidity Premium

The additional return investors demand for holding assets that are harder to sell quickly. In a bull market like May 2026, liquidity premiums compress — investors accept lower yields on less-liquid assets because confidence is high. When fear returns, those premiums expand rapidly and illiquid assets suffer disproportionate losses.
