Month-End Recap: May 2026 Was One for the History Books — What June Holds

May 2026 closes with all three major indexes at record highs, the Dow above 51,000 for the first time, and the S&P up 4.1% for the month. The Nasdaq's 8.1% monthly gain was its best since November 2023. The rally was driven by four converging forces: better-than-expected earnings season, AI infrastructure demand validated by Dell and Nvidia, declining oil prices as Iran ceasefire talks progressed, and a delayed reaction to the improving trade picture. What could slow June: a hawkish Warsh confirmation, stubborn PCE inflation at 3.8%, and the first FOMC meeting under new leadership on June 16–17.

June Preview — Fed

June 16–17 FOMC: Warsh's First Test — All Eyes on Dot Plot

Warsh's inaugural FOMC meeting on June 16–17 will be the most scrutinized in years. Markets aren't expecting a rate move — but they're dissecting every word of the statement and press conference for signals about the dot plot's 2026 trajectory. If Warsh eliminates the easing bias entirely, Treasury yields will spike and equities will feel it immediately. The question isn't what he does at this meeting — it's what he signals for the rest of the year.

Real Estate & Mortgage
Monthly Recap

May Housing: Inventory Stays Tight, New Construction Fills the Gap

May existing home sales came in at a 4.09 million annualized rate — below the historical average but improved from April. The national median price hit $425,000, a 5.1% year-over-year gain. New home sales continue to outperform at a 743,000 annualized pace. The 30-year fixed at 6.53% (Freddie Mac's latest) is the best reading since late 2024, but affordability math still pencils poorly for first-time buyers in most major markets.

Health & Wellness
Research

Weekend Warrior Pattern Delivers Same Benefits as Daily Exercise

A large-scale UK Biobank study of 89,000 adults confirms that concentrating your recommended weekly exercise into one or two sessions ("weekend warrior" pattern) delivers essentially the same cardiovascular and all-cause mortality benefits as spreading exercise throughout the week — provided total volume meets guidelines (150+ min moderate or 75+ min vigorous per week).

Mental Health

Nature Exposure 120+ Minutes Per Week Cuts Anxiety by 30%

Research published in Scientific Reports finds that spending at least 120 minutes in natural environments weekly — parks, trails, lakefront — is associated with 30% lower self-reported anxiety and significantly better mood regulation. The threshold effect kicks in at 120 minutes total; more time provides diminishing returns. Chicago's lakefront trail and forest preserves qualify.

Random Fact

Memorial Day Has Roots in Chicago's Rosehill Cemetery

While its exact origins are disputed, one of the strongest claims belongs to Decoration Day ceremonies held in 1868 at Rosehill Cemetery in Chicago — the holiday was established to honor Union soldiers who died in the Civil War and evolved into the national Memorial Day observed today.
