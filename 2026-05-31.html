Current Events & Geopolitics

Iran Ceasefire
Strait of Hormuz Fully Reopens Sunday Morning — First Commercial Tanker Transits in 47 Days
The Strait of Hormuz fully reopened to commercial shipping Sunday morning as the first civilian oil tanker transited the waterway since the IRGC blockade began 47 days ago. The ship, carrying 2 million barrels of Kuwaiti crude, was escorted by U.S. and UAE naval vessels. Oil fell to $85.20 on the news — its lowest since before the conflict began. The reopening is the most concrete sign yet that the Iran ceasefire MOU is real and durable. Analysts at Goldman Sachs now forecast oil falling to $75–80 within 30 days if transits continue without incident. That would be transformational for inflation and interest rates.

Roadshow Begins Tomorrow — $1.75T IPO Could Reshape Markets

The SpaceX roadshow kicks off Tuesday (Monday holiday). Goldman and Morgan Stanley are running the book. Over 2.3 million retail accounts pre-registered. Economy

Hormuz Reopening Could Cut 30-Year Mortgage Below 6% by August

Goldman Sachs economists estimate that if oil falls to $75–80 on sustained Hormuz access, the resulting drop in inflation expectations would pull 30-year mortgage rates below 6% by August — a level not seen since early 2023. That would be a genuine demand catalyst for the housing market that has been frozen by rate levels for two years.

One Big Beautiful Bill Clears Senate Committee — What's In It

Trump's "One Big Beautiful Bill" cleared the Senate Finance Committee Friday. Key provisions: extension of 2017 TCJA rate cuts, no taxes on tips, no taxes on overtime pay, enhanced child tax credit to $2,500, and elimination of the SALT deduction cap. The bill heads to a full Senate vote next week. CPA firms are already modeling client impacts.

Health, Wellness & Fitness

Longevity

Your Thymus May Predict How Long You Live

A Mass General Brigham study using AI to analyze CT scans found that people with healthier thymus glands had significantly better cancer survival outcomes and markers of healthy aging. The thymus shrinks dramatically after puberty, but its residual function at midlife is a meaningful longevity signal. Lifestyle interventions that preserve thymus function are now an active research area.

Infection

Methionine Amino Acid Boosts Infection Survival Dramatically

A Salk Institute study found that supplementing with methionine — found in eggs, meat, and fish — dramatically improved survival in mice facing severe infections and inflammatory conditions. A possible nutritional intervention pathway for sepsis and life-threatening inflammatory events. Human trials are expected to begin in 2027.

Metabolism

Intermittent Fasting Rewires Both Gut and Brain

A study of obese adults found that intermittent fasting caused significant weight loss and notable shifts in gut bacteria — alongside measurable changes in brain scan activity. The gut-brain axis appears to be a key mechanism beyond simple caloric restriction. Hunger signals change at the source, not just in willpower.

Random Fact

The Last Commercial Ship Through Hormuz Before Closure

The last commercial oil tanker to transit the Strait of Hormuz before the IRGC blockade was the MT Seavoyager, a VLCC carrying Saudi crude, on April 14, 2026. It passed through at 2:47 AM local time — about six hours before the first IRGC interception of a U.S.-flagged vessel. Today's reopening ship was the first in 47 days.

Word of the Day

Contango (n.)

A market condition where futures prices for a commodity exceed current spot prices — implying the market expects current tightness to ease over time. The oil market was in severe backwardation during the Hormuz closure (near-term prices sky-high, futures lower). As Hormuz reopens, the curve is shifting back toward contango. Normal is returning.
