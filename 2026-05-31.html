Front Page
Breaking — U.S.–Iran War

Trump Signals Decision Imminent on Iran Deal — But Tehran Bristles at Demands

A Situation Room meeting concluded Friday with no final answer. The White House says Trump will only accept terms that satisfy his "red lines" — no nukes, Hormuz open, frozen assets on hold. Iran's response has been cold.

U.S.–Iran

Ceasefire Holding — Barely

The MOU between Washington and Tehran technically exists, but Trump spent Friday itemizing non-negotiables: Iran must hand over its highly enriched uranium, commit to no nuclear program, and fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Treasury Secretary Bessent separately downplayed any prospect of sanctions relief, saying progress "would go very slowly." VP Vance acknowledged the ceasefire has had "little flare-ups," including IRGC warning shots near Hormuz.

Defense Secretary Hegseth has been briefing reporters that the U.S. military stands ready to resume combat if negotiations collapse. Markets have been pricing in a successful deal — a breakdown could spike oil prices sharply.

Israel–Lebanon

Israel Expands Lebanon Operations

As the Iran negotiations stall, Israel stepped up air operations in southern Lebanon over the weekend, warning the public to expect expanded fire. The IDF said there are no changes to Home Front Command guidelines but urged citizens to "remain vigilant." Smoke was reported rising from Kfar Tebnit in the Nabatieh region on Saturday. The situation adds another variable to the already fragile regional picture.

Trade Policy

SCOTUS Tariff Ruling Still Rippling Through Markets

The Supreme Court's February 6–3 decision striking down IEEPA-based tariffs continues to reshape U.S. trade strategy. Trump quickly pivoted to a 10% global tariff under Section 122, but that authority expires in 150 days unless Congress votes to extend it. Billions in refunds to importers remain unresolved. With Iran, tariffs, and the Fed all in flux simultaneously, this may be the most policy-dense summer in memory for financial markets.

National

May Closes as One of the Best Months in Years for Stocks

Despite geopolitical friction and sticky inflation, Wall Street just finished May in dramatic fashion. The Nasdaq posted its best month of 2026 with an 8%+ gain. The S&P 500 finished up 5% and the Dow added nearly 3%. Friday alone saw the Dow close above 51,000 for the first time, with Dell Technologies surging 33% on a blowout earnings report powered by AI-driven server demand.

Story of the Week

Dell's AI Day: +33% in a Single Session

Dell Technologies delivered its best single-day gain on record Friday, surging 33% after a first-quarter earnings beat on both top and bottom lines and raising its full-year guidance. The company's server business — a direct beneficiary of AI infrastructure spending — has been on an extraordinary run, up more than 150% year-to-date. The stock crossed $420 in late Friday trading. As one analyst put it: "The AI opportunity shows no signs of slowing."

Federal Reserve & Macroeconomics
Warsh Era — Week One

The New Chair's First 100 Days Begin

Kevin Warsh was sworn in as the 17th Fed chair at the White House on May 22 — the first such ceremony held there since Alan Greenspan in 1987, confirmed 54–45 in the closest vote of the modern era. He inherited a central bank holding rates at 3.50–3.75% with PCE inflation running at 3.8% — well above the 2% target — and an FOMC that produced four dissents at its most recent meeting.

His first rate decision comes June 16–17. Markets have assigned a 97% probability to no change. But Warsh's language, the composition of any dissents, and his post-meeting press conference will tell Wall Street far more than the decision itself about the trajectory for the rest of 2026.

Warsh's Agenda

Three Ways He Plans to Reshape the Fed

Analysts at Motley Fool this morning outlined Warsh's apparent agenda: redefining how the FOMC thinks about inflation (more flexible, less anchored to lagging data); loosening "forward guidance" to give the committee more room to maneuver; and reducing the Fed's balance sheet more aggressively. Warsh has explicitly said he has made Trump no promises on rate cuts — a position that sets up potential friction as the president continues to call publicly for lower rates.

"The recent rise in market-based breakeven inflation rates strongly implies that Warsh and the FOMC will have to prepare for the chance that inflation will continue to rise."

— Fortune analysis, May 2026
Rate Outlook

Hike Odds Growing, Cut Odds Shrinking

CBS News reported that investors now see more than an 80% probability of rates holding steady in both June and July. More striking: by December, CME FedWatch data shows nearly 70% odds of a rate hike. This is a dramatic reversal from earlier in the year, when markets expected multiple cuts. PCE at 3.8% and the tariff environment have made easing nearly impossible to justify to the committee's hawks.

FOMC Dynamics

Most Divided Committee Since 1992

Four FOMC dissents at the last meeting — the highest since 1992 — underscore the challenge Warsh faces building consensus. While technically holding just one of 12 votes, the chair's ability to broker agreement shapes both policy outcomes and market communication. Warsh reportedly plans to de-emphasize forward guidance, which could itself unsettle bond markets accustomed to the Powell-era "data dependent" framework.

Real Estate
Mortgage Rates

30-Year Holds at 6.53% — A Weird Spring Season

Freddie Mac's May 30 weekly survey put the benchmark 30-year fixed rate at 6.53%, a slight tick higher from the prior week. Bankrate's daily tracker showed 6.56% as of Saturday. The good news: Norada's 30-year refinance rate tracker showed a 9-basis-point dip from last week. The broader picture for May–July: forecasters expect rates to stay in the "low-to-mid 6%" range with no dramatic swings absent a major economic shock.

Fannie Mae's May forecast put the Q2 average at 6.3%, trending toward 6.1% by year-end. NAHB expects a fall below 6% by December, partly contingent on Iran conflict resolution reducing the risk premium on Treasuries.

Housing Market

A Spring Season Unlike the Others

TheStreet's real estate team described this homebuying season as "weird" — and the data backs that up. April 2026 was the first month this year where annual home listings outpaced sales. The Case-Shiller index is up a modest 0.7% year-over-year, a far cry from the double-digit surges of 2021–22. Texas and Florida have flipped to buyer's markets; parts of the Midwest and Northeast remain seller's markets. For Chicago-area buyers, increased inventory is providing some leverage for the first time in years.

Health & Wellness
Research Alert

Microplastics Found in Nearly Every Human Brain Sample Studied

A study published in Nature Health in April found microplastics and nanoplastics in virtually all human brain tissue analyzed, including healthy samples — with concentrations higher in tumor-affected tissue. Nanoplastics were more abundant than microplastics. A separate Nature Medicine study earlier this year found even greater accumulation in brains of patients with documented dementia, with notable deposits in cerebrovascular walls and immune cells.

Experts caution against panic — the health consequences in humans are still not fully understood — but practical steps to reduce exposure include avoiding plastic utensils with hot foods, switching to glass or steel water bottles, and using wooden cutting boards.

2026 Wellness Trends

Neurowellness: The Year's Top Trend

The Global Wellness Summit named "neurowellness" — defined as using technology to manually regulate the nervous system — as the top wellness trend of 2026. With digital life keeping nervous systems in near-constant fight-or-flight mode, demand is surging for tools that measure and retrain stress and resilience patterns in real time. Wearables tracking heart-rate variability, breathwork apps, and somatic therapy are all riding this wave.

Separately, the longevity market is pivoting: after years of catering primarily to men, women's healthspan is emerging as the next frontier — focused on ovarian aging and its downstream effects on systemic health across the lifespan.
