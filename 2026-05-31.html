⚔️ Middle East

U.S.–Iran Ceasefire Extension Agreed In Principle — Trump's Signature Still Pending

U.S. and Iranian negotiators finalized a preliminary memorandum of understanding this week to extend the ongoing ceasefire by 60 days and open formal nuclear talks — but President Trump has yet to sign. He convened advisers in the Situation Room Friday to make what he called a "final determination," reiterating that Iran must never obtain a nuclear weapon and demanding immediate reopening of the Strait of Hormuz.

The MOU would include a pledge from Tehran not to seek nuclear weapons, while the first item on the negotiating agenda would be disposing of Iran's 440.9 kilograms of 60%-enriched uranium. Treasury Secretary Bessent has been firm: no sanctions relief until the enriched uranium is turned over. Wall Street closed at records Friday on hopes of a deal; oil prices slid. The signature could come as early as this weekend — or not at all.

📈 May Closes

All Three Indexes End May at Records — Nasdaq +8% for the Month

The S&P 500, Dow Jones, and Nasdaq Composite all closed Friday at fresh all-time highs. Nasdaq surged 8.1% for May — its best month of the year — on continued AI enthusiasm. The S&P added 5% and the Dow 2.9%. Dell's 33% single-session eruption post-earnings was the week's signature move. The S&P logged its 9th consecutive weekly gain — the longest streak since 2023.

🏦 Fed

Warsh Sworn In May 22 — His First Test Comes June 16–17

Kevin Warsh became the 17th Federal Reserve Chair on May 22, inheriting a central bank with PCE inflation at 3.8% and an FOMC that logged four dissents at its April meeting — the most divided since 1992. The June meeting is widely expected to hold rates steady, but the press conference will be watched as carefully as any rate decision in recent memory. Odds of a December rate hike are now nearly 40%.

🌐 Strait of Hormuz

Iran Effectively Controls the Strait — Whatever Any Deal Says

Even as negotiations continue, experts warn that Iran has de facto control over the Strait of Hormuz regardless of MOU language. Former Biden energy adviser Amos Hochstein said Friday: "No matter what happens, the Iranians will control the Strait for the foreseeable future." That reality is already priced into energy markets — and is the central variable in any Fed inflation calculus.

₿ Bitcoin

BTC Slips Into May Downturn — Spot ETFs Post Largest Monthly Outflow of 2026

Bitcoin closed May near $73,500, off roughly 3.7% for the month even as equity markets surged. Spot Bitcoin ETFs saw $2.3 billion in net outflows — the largest single-month exit of 2026, reversing the prior two months of inflows. Whale addresses and long-term holders are trimming. The Fear & Greed Index sits at 30. Key support: $72,000. A break lower opens a path to $68,000.

😴 Sleep Research

New Study: Sleeping More Than 7.8 Hours Also Ages Your Organs

A large-scale study published this week found that too much sleep — consistently above 7.8 hours — is associated with accelerated biological aging across nearly every organ in the body. The sweet spot per the data: 6.4 to 7.8 hours nightly. Earlier OHSU research this year found sleep duration to be a stronger predictor of lifespan than diet or exercise. Together, the findings push sleep firmly to the top of the longevity hierarchy.

🌐 Wellness Trends

Microplastics, Neurowellness & the Year of Women's Longevity

The Global Wellness Summit's 2026 trends report marks microplastics as a genuine public health issue — the first year the wellness industry is moving from awareness to intervention. Separately, nervous system regulation is identified as the "next frontier" — consumer neurotech, breathwork, and vagal stimulation are moving from fringe to mainstream. And 2026 is being called the year women's longevity finally gets its own science: ovarian aging research is driving a rethinking of menopause as something to actively manage, not just endure.

From the Editor — Sunday, May 31, 2026

May goes out with the equity markets at full throttle and everything else still unsettled. The S&P 500 logged its ninth straight weekly gain. All three major indexes are at records. The tech trade — the AI infrastructure boom — is still working. Dell's 33% day was the exclamation point on a month that made a lot of bulls very rich.

And yet: the war is still on. The deal is still unsigned. The Fed's new chair inherits an inflation problem that hasn't gotten easier. The mortgage market remains frozen for millions of would-be buyers. Bitcoin, almost alone among risk assets, has gone the wrong way this month. The paradoxes of this economy are real, and they'll be with us in June too. Start the week with your eyes open.

June is named after Juno — the Roman goddess of marriage
The month of June takes its name from Juno, queen of the Roman gods and protector of marriage and women. This is why June remains the most popular month for weddings in the Western world — a tradition nearly 2,000 years old.
