Breaking · Iran War & Diplomacy

Iran Suspends Talks — Trump Insists Deal Is Still Coming "This Week"

The Iran situation turned volatile again overnight. An Iranian news outlet linked to the Revolutionary Guard announced the regime was suspending indirect negotiations and opening "other fronts" in the conflict — then walked it back within hours. President Trump posted on Truth Social early Tuesday morning that talks were proceeding at a "rapid pace," and separately told ABC News he believes a deal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz and extend the ceasefire is reachable "over the next week." On the Lebanon front, Trump declared that Israeli forces would not move on Beirut after phone calls with both sides. Hezbollah agreed to a US ceasefire proposal halting strikes on Beirut; Israel's Netanyahu said his military would keep striking southern Lebanon "as planned." Qatar continues working the phones on both tracks. The situation remains fluid and oil markets are watching every headline.

World Affairs

Strait of Hormuz Remains the Prize

Trump's stated red line is clear: the Strait must reopen fully and Iran cannot go nuclear. Oil at $94/bbl (Brent) reflects the ongoing premium. Any deal that reopens the Strait could send crude down sharply — a tailwind for inflation relief the Fed has been waiting for.

🔭 Armstrong Economics

Martin Armstrong · June 1, 2026 Market Talk

ECM Downcycle Midpoint Arriving — Volatility Rising Into 2027

Armstrong's Economic Confidence Model (ECM) is scheduled to hit its downcycle midpoint in June 2026, after which the model projects a yearlong plunge in confidence into mid-2027. The Benner cycle similarly peaks in 2026 before a six-year decline to 2032. Armstrong has long argued that the 2025–2027 window is a major cyclical turning point — not just for the US, but for global confidence in government institutions. His computer model identifies 2027 as the next major peak for volatility. On Monday, Armstrong Economics posted its June 1 Market Talk reviewing the weekend's Iran developments alongside AI-driven equity moves — noting that markets are beginning to price in more geopolitical risk premium than at any point since the energy shock of 2022. The $90+ oil print and rising gold prices are, in Armstrong's framework, consistent signals of capital flowing out of confidence in governments and into tangible assets.

Iran War Keeps Mortgage Rates Elevated — NAHB Eyes Sub-6% by Year-End

Rates have risen since the Iran conflict began, as war-premium oil prices feed directly into inflation expectations. The 10-year Treasury yield, which mortgage rates closely track, rose 2 basis points Monday to 4.48% as Iran peace talks wobbled. 💊 Health & Wellness

Your Thymus May Predict How Long You Live — New AI Study

Researchers at Mass General Brigham used AI to analyze CT scans from tens of thousands of adults and found that people with healthier thymuses — a small, often-forgotten gland behind the breastbone that trains immune cells — had significantly better outcomes for aging and cancer survival. The thymus shrinks with age, but the rate varies widely. The finding opens a new window for longevity medicine: the thymus as a biomarker for healthspan, not just lifespan. (Published June 1, 2026)

Amino Acid Methionine Dramatically Improves Infection Survival in Mice — Salk Institute

A Salk Institute study found that supplementing with methionine — a simple dietary amino acid found in eggs, fish, and meat — dramatically improved survival rates in mice facing severe infections and inflammatory conditions. The mechanism appears tied to immune cell metabolism. While mouse models don't always translate to humans, the finding is a notable step toward nutritional immunology as a therapeutic field. (Published June 1, 2026)

Intermittent Fasting Rewires Gut and Brain Simultaneously

A study published May 31 found that an intermittent fasting-style diet in obese adults produced significant weight loss alongside measurable shifts in gut bacteria — and corresponding changes visible on brain scans. The gut-brain axis appears to be more plastic than previously understood. Researchers note the changes were observable within weeks, suggesting the gut microbiome responds faster to dietary intervention than to long-term lifestyle changes alone.

🎲 Closing Notes

Random Fact of the Day

The Chicago River flows backwards.

In 1900, Chicago engineers reversed the flow of the Chicago River — making it flow away from Lake Michigan rather than into it — to prevent sewage contamination of the city's drinking water supply. It remains one of the great feats of civil engineering in American history.

Word of the Day

Contango

A market condition where futures prices are higher than the expected spot price at delivery. In today's oil market, contango reflects expectations that current war-premium prices will eventually normalize — traders are willing to pay more for future delivery than present supply warrants.
