Iran MOU
Iran MOU Deadline June 15 — Both Sides Signal Willingness to Convert to Formal Treaty
With the Iran–U.S. MOU deadline 13 days away, diplomatic signals are cautiously optimistic. Iranian Foreign Minister Araghchi told state television that Tehran is "prepared to engage seriously" on a formal treaty framework. The State Department called the talks "moving in the right direction." The Strait of Hormuz has now seen 31 commercial tanker transits since reopening — a normalizing flow. Oil has drifted from $94 to $91.80 as the market prices in continued access. The biggest wildcard remains Iranian hardliners who opposed the MOU from the start.

Health, Wellness & Fitness

Longevity

Thymus as Longevity Organ — AI Study Confirms Signal

The Mass General Brigham AI thymus study is getting broader coverage as its implications sink in. The key finding: thymus health at midlife is a stronger predictor of cancer survival than many genetic factors. Resistance training and caloric restriction are the two lifestyle factors most associated with preserved thymus function after age 40.

Nutrition

Ultra-Processed Foods Linked to 32 Adverse Health Outcomes

A landmark umbrella review of 45 meta-analyses found ultra-processed food consumption associated with 32 adverse health outcomes including cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, depression, and all-cause mortality. The dose-response relationship was linear — every 10% increase in UPF consumption increased mortality risk by 3%.

Fitness

Zone 2 Cardio Found Optimal for Metabolic Health and Longevity

A Stanford study confirmed what sports physiologists have long suggested: Zone 2 cardio (conversational pace, 60–70% max heart rate) for 150+ minutes per week produces the greatest improvements in mitochondrial density, fat oxidation capacity, and metabolic health. No need to suffer. Go long and easy.

Word of the Day

Arbitrage (n.)

The simultaneous purchase and sale of the same asset in different markets to profit from a price difference. In geopolitics, the Iran ceasefire represents an arbitrage between the war premium in oil (high) and the actual probability of renewed conflict (lower). As that gap closes, oil falls. That's the trade that's been playing out all week.
