Markets
Equities Hold Near Records as Iran Deadline Looms — Jobs Data Friday
U.S. equity markets opened June's second week in tight range-bound trading near all-time highs. The S&P 500 continues to hold above 7,500 while traders await two binary events that could drive June's direction: the Iran MOU decision, now expected any day, and Friday's May employment report. The consensus forecast calls for approximately 145,000 new jobs and an unemployment rate steady at 4.1%. A strong number would confirm the "higher for longer" rates narrative heading into Warsh's June 17 FOMC debut; a weak number reopens the rate cut conversation. Meanwhile oil is in binary-outcome mode around Iran — trading with roughly 30% built-in geopolitical premium that would evaporate on a deal.

Current Events

Iran
Iran MOU Decision Expected This Week — Trump Holding the Pen
The U.S.–Iran memorandum of understanding is on Trump's desk. Diplomats in Muscat have delivered a final text; what remains is presidential sign-off. The core MOU — a 60-day cessation of hostilities — is agreed. Trump's added provisions on missile verification are described by Iranian officials as "under review." The administration is signaling a decision by Wednesday. The geopolitical stakes are clear: a signed MOU would be among the most significant diplomatic events of the Trump second term and would reset the risk premium across global markets. Continued delay creates its own uncertainty tax. The market has been patient; it won't wait indefinitely.

Tax & Accounting

Q2 Estimated Tax Payments Due June 16 — What to Do With OBBBA Uncertainty

Q2 estimated tax payments are due June 16 — the same day Warsh's FOMC meeting begins. With the OBBBA still in Senate limbo, taxpayers face a planning dilemma: calculate based on current law or the proposed new law? The safe harbor answer is always current law — paying at least 100% of prior year liability (or 110% for high earners) protects against underpayment penalties regardless of what ultimately passes. For Illinois taxpayers specifically, note that the state's own conformity to any federal changes is not automatic; separate analysis is required. If the OBBBA passes with retroactive 2026 effective dates, Q3 estimated payments (due September 15) can be recalculated with updated figures. Don't overpay Q2 trying to anticipate a bill that hasn't been signed.

Armstrong Economics

Armstrong: The Iran Resolution Will Not End the Bull — It Will Accelerate It

Martin Armstrong's analysis holds that when the Iran MOU is signed, the immediate market reaction will be positive but the deeper effect will be more significant: the removal of a geopolitical tail risk will accelerate the capital flight dynamic that has been driving flows into U.S. equities. With Iran resolved, Armstrong's model suggests European and emerging market capital — currently partially on the sidelines due to conflict risk — will rotate more aggressively into dollar-denominated assets. His summer outlook is constructive with the caveat that the September–October cyclical window remains a watch point for a correction within the broader bull structure. He continues to view U.S. Treasuries at the long end as the primary systemic risk globally — not equities.

Health & Wellness

Longevity

Daily Walking Beats Gym Sessions for Long-Term Adherence

A 5-year adherence study found that daily 30-minute walking programs had a 74% continuation rate at year five, versus 31% for structured gym programs. The health outcomes were comparable for cardiovascular risk reduction. The takeaway: the best exercise is the one you'll actually keep doing. For most people, walking wins on adherence alone.

Mental Health

Social Connection Reduces Dementia Risk by 40% — Harvard Study

A landmark 30-year Harvard study confirmed that strong social connections in midlife (ages 45-65) are associated with a 40% reduction in dementia risk later in life — independent of physical health, cognitive engagement, or genetic factors. The mechanism appears to involve reduced chronic stress and maintained neural plasticity through regular social interaction.

Random Fact

Chicago's Magnificent Mile Was a Cow Path

Michigan Avenue's famous Magnificent Mile shopping district follows the route of an old Native American and fur trader trail that was literally used to drive cattle to market in the early 1800s. The elegant boulevard with its luxury retailers and landmark architecture sits atop what was once a muddy livestock path through the prairie.

Word of the Day

Binary Risk

A market situation where an asset's price is largely determined by a single yes-or-no outcome. The Iran situation is creating binary risk in oil markets: deal = $67 crude, no deal = $85 crude. Binary risks make traditional technical analysis less useful because price action between the outcomes is essentially random noise.
