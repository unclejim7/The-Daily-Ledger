Tuesday Close · June 2, 2026

Markets Reverse From Records — Bitcoin Plunges 31% in 9 Days as Saylor Breaks "Never Sell" Pledge

After Monday's record-setting ceasefire rally, markets reversed sharply Tuesday. The S&P 500 fell 0.74% from its 7,621 record, the Dow dropped 1.21%, and the Nasdaq shed 0.91%. The selling was broad-based — rising bond yields (10-year hit 4.61%) and a hot ISM manufacturing reading resurfaced hawkish Fed fears. But the bigger story was Bitcoin: it plunged to $72,400 — down 31% from its $108,000 high in just nine trading sessions — after MicroStrategy's Michael Saylor announced a partial sale, shattering his "never sell" public commitment and triggering mass ETF redemptions.

The ISM Manufacturing Index came in at 53.2 — well above the 50 expansion threshold and the highest reading since 2022 — reigniting fears that the economy is running too hot for the Fed to ease. Combined with the still-elevated PCE at 3.8%, the data paints a stagflation-lite picture that gives Warsh cover to stay restrictive indefinitely.

Bitcoin Crash

Saylor Sells — MicroStrategy Dumps BTC, Triggering Cascade of ETF Redemptions

Michael Saylor's announcement of a partial MicroStrategy BTC sale — reportedly 8,500 coins at prices between $89,000 and $94,000 — shattered the market's most sacred narrative. Saylor had publicly declared he would "never sell" on multiple occasions; his reversal triggered a confidence crisis. Within hours, Bitcoin ETFs saw over $1.2B in redemptions, the largest single-day outflow on record.

ISM Shock

Manufacturing at 53.2 — Economy Too Strong for Rate Relief

The ISM Manufacturing Index surging to 53.2 is a double-edged sword: it signals economic strength but removes any near-term justification for Fed rate cuts. The subindices were broadly strong: new orders at 55.1, employment at 51.8, prices paid at 61.2 — the last figure particularly alarming as it signals upstream inflation pressure not yet reflected in CPI or PCE.

🏛️ Federal Reserve
Data Validates Warsh

Hot ISM + 4.61% Yield = Warsh's Restrictive Posture Vindicated

The day's data effectively vindicates Warsh's hawkish stance before he's even held his first FOMC meeting. ISM at 53.2, PCE at 3.8%, and the 10-year yield's jump to 4.61% collectively argue for extended restriction. The June 16–17 meeting is now nearly certain to produce a hold with explicitly hawkish forward guidance.

Rate Path Repricing

Rate-Cut Odds Collapse — Q4 2026 Cut Probability Falls Back to 8%

The brief flirtation with Q4 2026 rate-cut odds — which peaked at 22% after the Iran MOU — collapsed to 8% following Tuesday's ISM print. Federal funds futures now price the first cut no earlier than Q2 2027. The market has fully capitulated to Warsh's "higher for longer" framework.

₿ Bitcoin & Crypto
Crypto Crash

Bitcoin Falls to $72,400 — 31% Crash in 9 Sessions as Saylor's "Never Sell" Narrative Collapses

Bitcoin's freefall to $72,400 represents one of the fastest 30%+ corrections in the asset's history. The proximate cause — Saylor's sale — is significant less for the quantity of BTC sold than for the narrative destruction. Saylor had positioned himself as Bitcoin's most credible institutional "permanent holder," and his reversal signals that even the most committed corporate holders have price or leverage thresholds.

The Fear & Greed Index dropped to 14 (Extreme Fear). On-chain data shows significant long-term holder distribution beginning — not panic selling, but profit-taking at scale. The question is whether $72,400 is the bottom or merely the first leg of a deeper correction.

📊 Armstrong Economics
Market Talk — June 2, 2026

Armstrong: Bitcoin's Break Below $75K Triggers Major Technical — "Temporary Lull" Call Validated Again

Martin Armstrong noted Tuesday that Bitcoin's break below $75,000 triggers a significant technical level in his model, suggesting the correction could extend to the $62,000–$65,000 zone before finding structural support. He also pointed to the Saylor event as confirmation of his long-held thesis that corporate Bitcoin treasury strategies are fragile — built on narrative rather than fundamentals.

On equities, Armstrong viewed Tuesday's selloff as a healthy consolidation after the Iran-driven euphoria, not a trend change. He maintained that U.S. equities remain in the ECM's bullish phase through mid-2026. The ISM data, he noted, "confirms the economy is running hot — which is ultimately bullish for stocks even if it's near-term bearish for bonds."

💪 Health & Wellness
Stress Response

Market Volatility and Cortisol: How Financial Stress Impacts Physical Health

Watching a 31% Bitcoin crash or a 1.2% equity selloff activates the same cortisol stress response as physical threats. Chronically elevated cortisol — from sustained financial anxiety — suppresses immune function, increases visceral fat storage, disrupts sleep, and raises cardiovascular risk. The research-supported antidote: time-limited market monitoring, physical exercise as cortisol clearance, and maintaining perspective on long-term financial plans.

Cognitive Performance

Cold Exposure (Brief) Shown to Boost Norepinephrine 300% — Implications for Focus

A study from the University of Oslo found that 20-second cold water exposure (57°F/14°C) increases norepinephrine levels by up to 300% — a neurotransmitter critical for attention, focus, and mood. The effect peaks 5–10 minutes after exposure. Cold showers, cold plunges, or even cold water on the face produce measurable but smaller effects that last 2–4 hours.

Word of the Day

Capitulation

The point at which investors abandon their positions and sell at a loss, often marking the bottom of a selloff. Today's Bitcoin crash has elements of capitulation — extreme fear, record ETF outflows, narrative collapse — but true capitulation typically involves even more forced selling. The question is whether $72,400 is the capitulation low or merely the first leg down.
