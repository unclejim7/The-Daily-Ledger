Markets Reverse From Records — Bitcoin Plunges 31% in 9 Days as Saylor Breaks "Never Sell" Pledge

After Monday's record-setting ceasefire rally, markets reversed sharply Tuesday. The S&P 500 fell 0.74% from its 7,621 record, the Dow dropped 1.21%, and the Nasdaq shed 0.91%. The selling was broad-based — rising bond yields (10-year hit 4.61%) and a hot ISM manufacturing reading resurfaced hawkish Fed fears. But the bigger story was Bitcoin: it plunged to $72,400 — down 31% from its $108,000 high in just nine trading sessions — after MicroStrategy's Michael Saylor announced a partial sale, shattering his "never sell" public commitment and triggering mass ETF redemptions.

Bitcoin

Saylor Sells — Bitcoin ETFs Bleed $2.3B in May

MicroStrategy disclosed a partial Bitcoin sale of 8,500 BTC — the first time Michael Saylor has sold after years of public "never sell" declarations. The announcement triggered $2.3B in Bitcoin ETF outflows in May, the worst monthly redemption total since the ETFs launched. Bitcoin fell to $72,400, its lowest since March. The strategic reserve bill's progress is the remaining bullish catalyst keeping it from a deeper correction.

Real Estate & Mortgage
Housing Market

30-Year Fixed Holds at 6.57% — Lock-In Effect Keeps Inventory Frozen

Freddie Mac's latest survey holds the 30-year fixed at 6.57%. The lock-in effect is structural: homeowners with sub-4% pandemic-era mortgages face a 250+ basis point effective cost to move. A new NAR study estimates 4.1 million potential home sales are being suppressed annually by the lock-in effect. Relief requires either rates dropping to sub-5.5% or enough time passing that life events (divorce, death, job relocation) force transactions regardless of rate differentials.

Health & Wellness
Research

Olive Oil Polyphenols Target Pancreatic Cancer Pathways

New research from the University of Edinburgh identifies the specific oleocanthal compound in extra virgin olive oil as a potent inducer of lysosomal membrane permeabilization in pancreatic cancer cells — causing cancer cell death while leaving healthy cells unaffected. Pancreatic cancer's notoriously poor prognosis makes every promising lead significant. Human trials are being planned.

Fitness

Strength Training Now America's #1 Fitness Goal — Cardio Displaced

For the first time in the 30-year history of IHRSA's annual fitness survey, strength training has displaced cardiovascular exercise as Americans' primary fitness goal. The shift is driven by longevity research showing muscle mass as a stronger predictor of healthspan than aerobic fitness, and by growing awareness of sarcopenia — age-related muscle loss — as a primary driver of late-life disability.

Armstrong Economics
Martin Armstrong

Bitcoin Crash Consistent With Armstrong's Crypto Warning — Capital Cycle Watch

Armstrong's market commentary Tuesday noted that Bitcoin's 31% correction from its high is consistent with his broader capital cycle analysis — speculative assets tend to reverse sharply when liquidity tightens and the 10-year yield rises above the "comfort zone" around 4.5%. He is not calling for a Bitcoin bottom but notes that the strategic reserve legislation remains a structural support that wasn't present in prior cycles. He continues to watch ECM dates in June for signs of the broader market transition.

Random Fact

The Willis Tower Was the World's Tallest Building for 25 Years

Chicago's Willis Tower (formerly Sears Tower) held the record as the world's tallest building from its completion in 1973 until 1998, when the Petronas Towers in Kuala Lumpur surpassed it. At 110 stories and 1,451 feet, it remains the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere.

Word of the Day

Sarcopenia

Age-related loss of muscle mass and strength — typically accelerating after age 50 at roughly 1-2% per year without intervention. Sarcopenia is a primary driver of late-life falls, disability, and metabolic dysfunction. Heavy resistance training is the only proven treatment.
