S&P 500 Crosses 7,600 as Iran Ceasefire Wobbles and Chip Stocks Surge

New York — Tuesday, June 2, 2026

The S&P 500 closed above 7,600 for the first time Tuesday — the latest milestone in a run that has produced nine consecutive weekly gains. Hewlett Packard Enterprise surged 19% on chip news from the Computex Taipei conference, dragging broader technology higher. The Dow added 0.45% while the Nasdaq barely edged up 0.03%.

But the backdrop remained unsettled. Semiofficial Iranian news agencies reported that Tehran had suspended contact with ceasefire mediators in protest of Israeli military operations in Lebanon and Gaza, sending oil prices sharply higher in morning trading. President Trump pushed back on Truth Social, asserting talks were continuing. U.S. forces struck a tanker headed for an Iranian port; CENTCOM confirmed the action as defensive.

On the downside, KKR fell 5.7% amid a broader selloff in alternative asset managers after Switzerland's Partners Group capped withdrawals from a major fund — raising questions about liquidity in private markets. GitLab slid 6% and Ares Management dropped 5%.

Warsh Watch · June 16–17 FOMC

14 Days to Warsh's Opening Statement

Kevin Warsh was sworn in May 22 — the first White House Fed swearing-in since Greenspan in 1987. He inherits a 3.50%–3.75% funds rate and the most internally divided FOMC in three decades. The June 16–17 meeting is his debut. Markets price 97% probability of no rate change — but the language will matter more than the number. Will he drop the easing bias? Will there be dissents? Those questions will set the tone for the rest of 2026.

Health & Wellness
Longevity Research

Thymus Health Predicts Longevity, AI Study Finds

Mass General Brigham researchers used AI to analyze CT scans from tens of thousands of adults, finding that thymus health — the small immune organ in the chest that shrinks with age — was a strong predictor of healthy aging and cancer survival. People with more active thymuses lived longer regardless of other known factors. The finding opens a new biomarker avenue for longevity medicine.

Nutrition Science

Methionine Amino Acid Boosts Survival in Infection Models

A Salk Institute study published June 1 found that methionine supplementation dramatically improved survival rates in mice with severe infections, working via kidney function improvement rather than direct immune activation. The compound is common in protein-rich foods. Human trials are the logical next step for this low-cost potential intervention at critical illness thresholds.

Random Fact of the Day

Michigan Avenue Was Once a Muddy Cattle Trail

The elegant boulevard with its luxury retailers and landmark architecture sits atop what was once a muddy livestock path through the prairie. The "Magnificent Mile" designation came in 1947, nearly a century after the path was first widened into a proper avenue. Chicago's transformation from frontier trading post to global financial center happened in roughly 150 years.

Word of the Day

Binary Risk

A market situation where an asset's price is largely determined by a single yes-or-no outcome. The Iran ceasefire is creating binary risk in oil: deal = $67 crude, no deal = $85+ crude. Binary risks make technical analysis less useful because price action between the outcomes is essentially random noise waiting for resolution.
