Lead Story · Equities
Dow Sets Fresh Record as Chips Stumble, Consumer Stalwarts Rally

Closing BellWednesday's session delivered one of the year's more schizophrenic performances: the Dow Jones Industrials surged nearly 183 points to a new all-time high, even as semiconductor heavyweights dragged the Nasdaq and S&P 500 nearly flat.

The divergence was stark. Qualcomm plunged nearly 9.7%, Nvidia shed 2.6%, and AMD fell 2.8% — all weighed by a midday geopolitical reality check from the White House, which poured cold water on morning headlines about an imminent Iran peace deal. Oil had spiked to $94/barrel on ceasefire optimism before clawing back.

On the Dow's winning side: Procter & Gamble (+3.4%), Nike (+2.9%), Home Depot (+2.5%), and Coca-Cola (+2.4%) — a classic defensive rotation. Consumer discretionary and staples led all S&P sectors, rising over 1%.

"The first big test for the Warsh Fed is watching markets navigate geopolitical fog without a Powell steadying hand."

Kevin Warsh was officially sworn in as Federal Reserve Chair, replacing Jerome Powell whose term expired May 15. His swearing-in statement — described as an 11-word market warning — has Wall Street on edge about the new regime's posture on rates.

After the bell, Snowflake (SNOW) rocketed 25% on a blockbuster announcement: Amazon Web Services committed $6 billion to use Amazon's new Graviton AI chips — one of the largest AI infrastructure deals ever. Salesforce (CRM) slid ~2.8% on light guidance.

🏛 Macroeconomics & The Fed
Federal Reserve
New Fed Chair Warsh Sworn In — Markets Brace for Policy Shift

Kevin Warsh officially took the helm of the Federal Reserve following Jerome Powell's May 15 term expiry. His first public statement — cryptically described as just 11 words — has sparked speculation about a more hawkish tilt. Wall Street, analysts warn, may not be ready for it.

The FOMC has held the federal funds rate steady at 3.50%–3.75% for two consecutive meetings (December 2025 and March 2026). Policymakers still project one rate cut this year and another in 2027, though timing is murky. The Iran conflict's economic fallout remains a wildcard.

Economic Outlook
GDP Forecast Upgraded; Inflation Sticky Above Target

The Fed's March projections revised 2026 GDP growth upward to 2.4% (from 2.3% in December). The economy is expanding at a solid pace, and job gains, while modest, remain resilient.

The bad news: both PCE and Core PCE inflation are now projected at 2.7% for 2026 — above the December estimate of 2.4%–2.5% and well above the 2% target. Unemployment is forecast at 4.4% this year.

The Iran war's impact on energy and supply chains adds significant uncertainty to an otherwise positive macro backdrop.

Geopolitics & Markets
Iran Ceasefire Frays — Oil Surges, Then Retreats

The fragile April ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran is under stress. CENTCOM confirmed new strikes on southern Iran Monday — targeting missile sites and speedboats allegedly placing naval mines — calling the action "self-defense." Tehran called it "bad faith."

Iran's Supreme Leader hinted at resuming strikes on U.S. military installations. The IRGC vowed a "decisive reciprocal response." WTI crude briefly surged to $94/bbl on the news — a direct inflation risk — before retracing on White House signals about ongoing Doha negotiations.

Trump stated Iran is "negotiating on fumes" and that November midterms won't rush him into a deal.

🏠 Real Estate
Housing Market
Spring Market Cooling: Rates Elevated, Inventory Rising, Prices Plateauing

The 2026 spring housing season is playing out as a "rebalancing year," not a crash — but buyers and sellers are operating in very different environments depending on location. Mortgage rates surged to their highest level since July earlier this month, dampening what is historically the hottest buying period.

April home-buying activity came in below seasonal norms, per Zillow. Inventory is up 4.2% year-over-year to 1.23 million active listings nationwide. Annual home values have declined in nearly half of major U.S. metros — a rare development. Sellers face longer listing times; buyers have more leverage than in years.

The national median existing home price hit a record $417,700 in April, even as price growth has slowed sharply. Pending home sales rose a modest 1.4% in April, surprising economists.

Fannie Mae forecasts the 30-year fixed mortgage rate will drop below 6% by year-end, reaching 5.7% — contingent on inflation cooling and geopolitical stability. The NAR expects historically normal seasonal uptick in May sales of ~10.7% month-over-month.

Real Estate Brief
Apartment Giants Merge; Buyers Weigh Real Estate vs. Small Business

DealEquity Apartments and AvalonBay Communities have announced a merger that would create a residential real estate giant — one of the largest combinations in the apartment REIT space in years. Details on the combined entity's portfolio size and strategic rationale are still emerging.

Investment Dilemma: With appreciation slowing, a growing cohort of real estate investors is asking whether a dollar is better deployed in property or small business. Analysts note real estate no longer "leans on appreciation to do the heavy lifting" — making cash flow and operational discipline more important than ever.

About 40% of buyers and sellers surveyed express concern about a potential housing market crash this year — but economists broadly describe current conditions as a structural rebalancing, not a collapse. The market is fragmenting sharply by geography: what's true in Phoenix bears no resemblance to what's happening in suburban Chicago.

📋 Tax & Accounting
Filing Season Wrap
IRS Declares 2026 Tax Season a Success — With Caveats

The IRS characterizes the 2026 filing season as a success, with 134 million individual returns filed — 98% electronically. Of those, 80% received a refund, and 98% of refunds were issued electronically.

The average refund was $3,400, up 11% from 2025, largely attributable to new deductions for tip income, overtime pay, and car loan interest under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA).

The caveat: paper return filers faced significant delays. Final regulations on the tip income deduction weren't issued until April 10, effective June 12 — creating compliance confusion. The IRS is flagging widespread errors on returns claiming the new deductions.

Legislation
Taxpayer Due Process Enhancement Act Passes House

On May 19, 2026, the House passed H.R. 6506, the Taxpayer Due Process Enhancement Act — a direct congressional override of the Supreme Court's controversial June 2025 ruling in Commissioner v. [redacted]. The bill strengthens taxpayer rights in Collection Due Process (CDP) proceedings.

For practitioners: this is significant relief for clients in IRS collection actions. The legislation now heads to the Senate. Conservation easement settlement opportunities also remain open — the IRS announced yet another window for taxpayers to resolve disputes.

OBBBA Impact
2026 Tax Brackets Adjusted; SALT, OBBBA Changes in Effect

The IRS 2026 tax adjustments are live. Most changes reflect inflation adjustments to marginal bracket thresholds, standard deductions, and credit phaseouts. Additional provisions stem from the One Big Beautiful Bill Act.

Key new deductions effective this tax year: tip income, overtime pay, and car loan interest. SALT deduction modifications also apply. Final regulations for several OBBBA provisions are still being issued — practitioners should monitor IRS.gov for guidance through mid-2026.

The IRS enhanced its Tax Withholding Estimator to reflect OBBBA changes, including the no-tax-on-tips provision. Recommend clients review withholding before Q3.

Trump Tightens Primary Grip; Paxton Defeats Cornyn in Texas

President Trump continues to flex his influence over Republican primaries. On Tuesday, Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton defeated four-term incumbent Sen. John Cornyn in a stunning primary result. Trump had backed Paxton. The win follows Trump-supported upsets in Indiana, Kentucky, and Louisiana.

The April ceasefire between the United States and Iran is under significant strain, with both sides trading strikes this week despite the truce. The U.S. conducted strikes on southern Iran; Tehran downed a U.S. drone and fired at a fighter jet.

Markets remain nervous: oil's intraday swing from $90 to $94/bbl on ceasefire headlines illustrates the direct economic stakes.

🫘
Eating More Beans & Soy Could Cut Hypertension Risk by ~30%
A major analysis published this week found that regular consumption of beans, lentils, chickpeas, tofu, and other soy foods could reduce high blood pressure risk by nearly 30%. The effect appears tied to both fiber content and specific amino acid profiles in legumes that modulate vascular tone.
🧪
Nasal Spray Appears to Reverse Brain Aging in Mice — Texas A&M
Texas A&M researchers developed a nasal spray that reversed markers of brain aging in animal models by calming neuroinflammation and restoring energy metabolism. Human trials are years away, but the mechanism — targeting glial cell activation — has attracted significant scientific interest.
❤️
UT Southwestern Finds "Master Switch" Controlling LDL Cholesterol
Scientists identified a protein called HELZ2 that functions as a previously unknown regulator of how much LDL the liver releases into the bloodstream. Manipulating this "switch" in animal models dramatically reduced harmful cholesterol — opening a new drug target beyond statins.

Ozempic Weight Loss Plateau Mystery Solved
New research published this week explains why semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) eventually stops driving weight loss in many patients. The mechanism involves adaptive metabolic responses that partially offset the drug's appetite-suppression effects over time — pointing toward combination therapies as the next frontier.
