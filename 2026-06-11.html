Trump Cancels Iran Strikes — Markets Surge; Tornado Outbreak Hits Chicago

President Trump reversed course late Thursday, calling off planned strikes on Iran and signaling a peace deal could arrive soon. Stocks surged: Dow +930 pts (+1.86%), S&P 500 +1.75%, Nasdaq +2.54%. The VIX collapsed nearly 12%. May CPI came in at 4.2% YoY — a three-year high driven by the energy shock — keeping the Fed firmly on hold. Meanwhile, the NWS confirmed a tornado outbreak across northern Illinois and NW Indiana, with Tornado and Flood Watches issued as a frontal system moved through the Midwest.

May CPI • Three-Year High

Inflation at 4.2% YoY — Warsh Inherits the Hardest Inflation Fight in Years

May CPI came in at 4.2% YoY — the highest since 2023 — driven primarily by gasoline prices up 40.5% year-over-year from the Iran-driven energy shock. Kevin Warsh chairs his first FOMC meeting June 16–17. The rate is almost certain to hold at 3.50%–3.75%. The real question: does the dot plot push any 2026 cuts off the table? Fed futures markets price near-zero probability of a cut at June's meeting.

🌍 Geopolitics
U.S.–Iran War • Day ~104

Trump: "Agreement In Principle" — Final Text Still Unsigned

Trump claimed several Middle East allies had agreed in principle to a peace deal, calling off evening strikes. Iranian officials stated no final text had been approved. The U.S. shot down two Iranian drones attempting to strike ships in the Strait of Hormuz early Thursday morning. Netanyahu reportedly spoke to Trump about a "memorandum of understanding." Fars news agency reported a deal is likely, though intermittent violations persist. A signing in Switzerland during the G7 summit (June 15–17) is being floated.

🏠 Real Estate & Mortgage
Freddie Mac PMMS — June 4, 2026

30-Year Mortgage at 6.48% — Rates Holding in Mid-6% Range

The 30-year fixed-rate mortgage averaged 6.48% as of the June 4 survey, down from 6.52% in the current week's data. Freddie Mac's chief economist said purchase demand is softening but remains above last year. Middle East hostilities and inflation concerns continue to put upward pressure on rates. The upcoming Warsh FOMC could push the 10-year higher and drag mortgage rates with it if the dot plot turns hawkish.

⛅ Chicagoland Weather

Tornado Outbreak — Northern Illinois & NW Indiana

The NWS Chicago confirmed a tornado outbreak, including multiple strong tornadoes, across northern Illinois and northwest Indiana on Thursday, June 11. Tornado and Flood Watches were in effect as a frontal system rapidly expanded in coverage and intensity. The KZZ-81 Lockport NOAA Weather Radio also reported a degraded signal. Friday brings clearing with a high near 86°F; the weekend looks calmer with highs 71–83°F.

Fitness Research

Swedish 47-Year Study: Strength Fades Sooner Than We Think

A Karolinska Institute study tracking participants for 47 years found that physical decline begins quietly earlier than most assume, with fitness, strength, and muscle endurance all showing measurable losses years before most people take notice — reinforcing the case for early, sustained resistance training throughout middle age.

Scientists unveiled an experimental diabetes and obesity pill that works through a completely different mechanism than GLP-1 drugs like Ozempic/Wegovy. Early results show significant fat reduction without the nausea, muscle loss, and rebound effects associated with semaglutide. Clinical trials ongoing.
