Health, Wellness & Fitness
Longevity Science

Add 5 Min Sleep + 2 Min Exercise + Half a Serving of Vegetables — Gain a Year

University of Sydney researchers studied 59,078 UK Biobank participants and found that the minimum combined improvements to sleep, physical activity, and diet add approximately one year to lifespan. Harvard's analysis corroborates: just 5 extra minutes of moderate-to-vigorous activity daily may prevent up to 10% of deaths over an 8-year window. The maximum gain — 9.35 additional life years — required 42–103 minutes of added exercise plus 7–8 hours nightly sleep plus a very healthy diet.

Sleep Research

Sleep Beats Diet and Exercise for Longevity — Oregon Health Study

Oregon Health & Science University researchers determined sleep is the single most important lifestyle factor for life expectancy, second only to not smoking. Quality sleep improves metabolism, immune function, mood, focus, and stress resilience — and makes maintaining all other healthy habits easier. Adults need 7–9 hours for optimal brain, immune, and cardiovascular health. Poor sleep creates a compounding negative feedback loop across every other health domain.

Word of the Day

Capitulation

In financial markets: the point at which investors give up and sell at a loss, often marking the bottom of a selloff. Bitcoin watchers are debating whether the Saylor sell + record ETF outflows constitute capitulation.
