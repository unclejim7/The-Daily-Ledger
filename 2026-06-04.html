Health, Wellness & Fitness

Cancer Research

Fat Type — Not Amount — Drives Pancreatic Cancer Risk

A new study finds the kind of dietary fat consumed may matter more than total fat intake for pancreatic cancer. One type of fat promoted tumor growth while another cut disease incidence in half. Olive oil and omega-3-rich fats are emerging as the protective categories — qualitative dietary guidance is the new frontier.

Longevity

AI Scans Reveal Thymus as a Longevity Predictor

A Mass General Brigham study used AI to analyze CT scans from tens of thousands of adults and found that people with healthier thymus glands — the small immune organ behind the sternum — had significantly better cancer survival and healthy aging outcomes. The thymus shrinks after puberty, but its midlife function is a meaningful longevity signal.

Sleep Science

Melatonin May Boost DNA Repair for Night Shift Workers

New research suggests melatonin supplements help night shift workers strengthen the body's DNA repair processes, potentially offsetting cellular damage from disrupted circadian rhythms. Night shift work is linked to elevated cancer and metabolic disease risk; this low-cost intervention may partially mitigate that risk.

Random Fact

The Windy City Isn't That Windy

Chicago's nickname "Windy City" has nothing to do with the weather. It was coined as political slang by New York Sun editor Charles Dana in 1890 to mock Chicagoans for being full of hot air while lobbying for the 1893 World's Fair. Chicago ranks 12th among major U.S. cities for average wind speed — behind Amarillo and Dodge City.

Word of the Day

Contagion (n.)

In finance: the spread of market disturbances from one market or institution to another through mechanisms that weren't anticipated. Broadcom's miss is being watched as a potential contagion vector for the semiconductor sector — the engine of the AI bull market. If chipmakers catch a cold, the indices may sneeze for longer than one session.
