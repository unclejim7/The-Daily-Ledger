Semiconductor Selloff

Chips Crater — Nasdaq Suffers Worst Day Since April 2025 as Broadcom Disappoints

Friday was ugly. The Nasdaq plunged 4.18%, its worst single-session loss in more than a year, as a savage rotation out of semiconductor names spread from Wall Street to Seoul to Frankfurt. The S&P 500 surrendered 2.64%, closing at 7,383.74. The Dow itself fell 695 points, or 1.35%, settling at 50,866.78.

The carnage was concentrated in chipmakers. Broadcom's decision to hold its full-year AI chip revenue targets unchanged proved the spark. NVDA lost nearly 6%, Marvell plunged 16%, Micron dropped 13%, AMD and Intel both shed roughly 11%. The 10-year yield moved back toward 4.50%.

📡 Armstrong Economics

Armstrong Market Talk — Week of June 3–5, 2026

Martin Armstrong's Market Talk posts this week tracked the global bond yield surge and commodity rotations that preceded Friday's equity rout. US 10-year yields pushed to 4.496% (+5 bps) by June 3, with the 30-year crossing 5.00% — a psychologically significant threshold Armstrong's models flag as consistent with sovereign debt stress building in peripheral European markets. Italy's BTP yield moved to 3.81% (+12 bps), and Turkey spiked to 34.985% (+270 bps).

Armstrong's cyclical framework has long warned that the June 2026 window represents a critical inflection point in the ECM (Economic Confidence Model) downcycle — the current data pattern is consistent with that thesis: sovereign yields rising, equity volatility spiking, and capital rotating defensively.
