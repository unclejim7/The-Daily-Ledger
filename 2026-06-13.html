Geopolitics & Markets

"Peace Has Never Been This Close": Pakistan Says U.S.-Iran Deal Text Finalized, Signing Could Come Within 24 Hours

Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif said early Saturday that a "final, agreed upon text" of a peace deal between the United States and Iran has been reached, with Islamabad now preparing for an electronic signing ceremony to be followed by technical-level talks next week. Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi echoed the optimism, saying the proposed memorandum of understanding "has never been closer," while a senior Trump administration official cautioned the deal isn't "100%" locked in pending final sign-off from Iran's Supreme Leader.

The framework reportedly centers on reopening the Strait of Hormuz to commercial traffic and lifting oil sanctions on Iran — both of which markets have been pricing in for days. Crude slipped roughly 2% toward $85/barrel on the news. Two Iranian drones were shot down near the Strait overnight, a reminder that the on-the-ground situation remains fragile even as diplomats inch toward a signature.

Risk assets cheered the headlines. Bitcoin pushed back above $64,000 and spot Bitcoin ETFs snapped a five-day outflow streak with $85.8 million in net inflows Friday, led by BlackRock's IBIT. Wall Street closed out a strong week on Friday, with the SpaceX Nasdaq debut (SPCX) stealing the show — more on that below.

SpaceX IPO

SpaceX (SPCX) Roars Onto Nasdaq in History's Largest IPO

SpaceX priced its IPO at $135 a share, opened near $150, and closed Friday at $161.11 — up roughly 19% on debut day, in what's being called the largest initial public offering on record. The raise, reported around $75 billion, is earmarked for data center and compute buildout. MSCI moved quickly to add SPCX to its Large-Cap and Standard indexes, kicking off a multi-day push for broad index inclusion. Speaking at SpaceX's Texas headquarters, Elon Musk called it hard to believe that "a little company that started in a warehouse in El Segundo" was now going public at this scale. AI infrastructure names were mixed in the wake of the listing — AMD, Qualcomm and Sandisk gained alongside the chip rally, while some megacaps pulled back as the SPCX debut absorbed market attention.

Macroeconomics & The Federal Reserve

FOMC Preview — June 16-17

Warsh's First Meeting as Chair Arrives With Inflation Running Hot

Next week's FOMC meeting is the most consequential of the year — it's Kevin Warsh's first as Fed Chair, and it comes with a full Summary of Economic Projections and updated dot plot. The fed funds rate has sat at 3.50%-3.75% across three straight meetings, and CME FedWatch puts the odds of a hold near 98%. The real story is the bias: with core CPI running near 4.2% and the May jobs report showing 172,000 payrolls added, several banks (including Goldman Sachs) have abandoned rate-cut forecasts entirely, with some now modeling a 2027 hike. J.P. Morgan and Chase strategists both expect the Committee to shift its language from an easing bias toward neutral. Warsh, seen as personally more dovish, will be navigating a committee likely to act as a brake on any quick pivot — his press conference tone on Wednesday afternoon will be parsed line by line.

Geopolitics Meets Crypto

BTC Reclaims $64K on Peace-Deal Optimism

Bitcoin traded back above $64,000 Saturday, supported by its strongest ETF inflow day in a month and a wave of optimism after Pakistan's prime minister said a U.S.-Iran deal could be finalized within 24 hours. BlackRock's IBIT led Friday's inflows at $57.7 million, with Fidelity's FBTC adding $18.0 million — notably, no major spot ETF reported a net outflow on the day, a meaningful shift after a stretch of redemptions. Ether funds, by contrast, continued to see outflows. Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) reportedly added to its BTC position during the recent pullback, while attention also turned to corporate adoption: roughly a quarter of so-called "Mag8" companies are now said to hold Bitcoin on their balance sheets.

Real Estate & Mortgage Rates

Freddie Mac PMMS — Week of June 11

30-Year Fixed Ticks Up to 6.52%, But Buyers Are Showing Up Anyway

The 30-year fixed-rate mortgage averaged 6.52% for the week ending June 11, up from 6.48% the prior week, while the 15-year fixed rose to 5.84% from 5.79%. Both remain meaningfully below year-ago levels (6.84% and 5.97%, respectively). Freddie Mac's chief economist Sam Khater pointed to stronger employment momentum pushing existing home sales to a five-month high, noting that buyers appear to be "looking past short-term rate fluctuations and actively entering the market" — a notable change in tone after a long stretch of rate-driven hesitation. With the FOMC meeting next week and inflation data still running hot, expect rate volatility to persist through the holiday-shortened week.

Armstrong Economics — ECM & War Cycle

Market Talk & the War Cycle

Geopolitical Risk Premium Still Embedded Across Asset Classes

Martin Armstrong's recent commentary has continued to frame the Iran conflict through the lens of his War Cycle model, arguing that the volatility in bonds, gold, and energy this spring reflects deeper capital-flow dynamics tied to the broader Economic Confidence Model rather than headlines alone. This week's Market Talk data showed continued divergence across sovereign bond yields — U.S. 10-year near 4.50%, with Turkish yields spiking sharply versus relatively stable European peripheral spreads — consistent with Armstrong's longstanding view that capital concentration accelerates during periods of geopolitical stress, benefiting perceived safe havens even as headline "peace" narratives develop. Commodity moves were mixed, with crude and silver among the session's bigger decliners even as gold-adjacent flows stayed firm. Readers following the ECM should watch how bond markets react in the days around the FOMC decision, which Armstrong-aligned analysts view as a potential confirmation point for the next phase of the cycle.

Health, Wellness & Fitness

A New Diabetes Pill Aims to Burn Fat Without Ozempic's Downsides

Researchers have developed an experimental diabetes and obesity medication that works through a different mechanism than GLP-1 drugs like Ozempic and Wegovy, potentially offering similar metabolic benefits with a different side-effect profile. The compound is still in earlier-stage development, but it reflects a broader 2026 trend: a wave of next-generation metabolic drugs aiming to broaden options beyond the current GLP-1 class.

Grip Strength Emerges as a Longevity Signal — Especially for Women

A large study of more than 5,000 older women found that simple measures of muscle strength, including grip strength, were strongly associated with longer, healthier lives. The findings reinforce a now-familiar 2026 wellness theme: strength training isn't just about aesthetics — it's one of the most consistent predictors of healthy aging in the research literature.

Collagen Supplements: What a Major Review of 8,000 Participants Actually Found

A large meta-analysis covering nearly 8,000 participants found that collagen supplementation can meaningfully improve skin health and ease osteoarthritis symptoms when taken consistently over longer periods, with more modest benefits observed for muscle mass. For readers weighing supplement spending, the consistency finding is the key takeaway — short-term use appears to deliver far less benefit than sustained, months-long supplementation.
